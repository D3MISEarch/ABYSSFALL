# ABYSSFALL CLASS BIBLE
# VOIDBRINGER — THE WEIGHT BETWEEN WORLDS

Status: Approved  
Codex version: 1.0  
Implementation status: Design approved  
Last updated: 2026-07-22  
Canonical class ID: `voidbringer`  
Compatibility ID: `void_warlock`

---

# Core fantasy

The Voidbringer is fused to a forbidden device that can alter **distance, direction, momentum and mass**.

They do not cast ordinary shadow magic. They weaponize the physical laws holding reality together.

The battlefield becomes a machine:

- Enemies become counterweights
- Terrain becomes an anchor point
- Projectiles become orbiting ammunition
- Distance becomes something that can be shortened, stretched or removed
- Groups of enemies can be crushed together
- The Voidbringer can increase their own mass until movement becomes an attack

The player’s fundamental fantasy is:

> **I decide where everything is allowed to exist.**

---

# The player promise

A new Voidbringer understands:

> Place anchors, move enemies around them, then collapse everything.

A skilled Voidbringer understands:

> Build spatial geometry, manipulate momentum, deliberately trigger a Breach and resolve the battlefield in the correct order.

A master Voidbringer understands:

> Every enemy, projectile, wall, corpse and movement skill is potential mass waiting to be weaponized.

---

# Class silhouette

The Voidbringer should never resemble a robed wizard.

They wear a damaged containment harness fused through the spine, ribs and dominant arm. Several fragments of impossible black material hover around their body without physically touching it.

These fragments are called **Null Shards**.

They respond to the Voidbringer’s movements:

- Forming blades around the forearm
- Locking together into shields
- Orbiting as ranged weapons
- Embedding themselves into enemies
- Hanging in midair as Mass Anchors
- Expanding into crushing gravitational limbs

The character should appear as though reality is constantly struggling to keep their body in one place.

## Visual language

Void abilities should avoid generic purple energy.

The class should use:

- Light bending around invisible shapes
- Background distortion
- Dust and blood moving sideways
- Enemies stretching briefly before displacement
- Black surfaces reflecting environments that are not present
- Sudden absence of sound before impact
- Low-frequency pressure rather than magical explosions
- Fractured afterimages occupying slightly different positions

When something collapses, it should feel like the world clenched its fist.

# Signature combat structure

The Voidbringer is built around four actions:

## 1. Anchor

Place Mass Anchors onto enemies, terrain or specific objects.

## 2. Load

Increase the amount of Mass stored inside those anchors.

## 3. Bend

Manipulate enemies, projectiles and movement around the anchor network.

## 4. Collapse

Destroy selected anchors to release their accumulated Mass and reduce Instability.

This creates a recognizable rhythm:

> **Anchor → Load → Bend → Collapse**

The player can follow that sequence simply, interrupt it, reverse it or build an entire character around only part of it.

---

# Primary class mechanic: Mass Anchors

The Voidbringer can maintain **three Mass Anchors** by default.

An anchor may be attached to:

- An enemy
- The ground
- A wall
- A destructible object
- A corpse
- A projectile through specialized upgrades
- The Voidbringer through Hollow Form abilities

Placing a fourth anchor destroys or replaces the oldest anchor unless an ability says otherwise.

## Anchor Mass

Each anchor can contain up to three visible levels of Mass:

### Dormant

The anchor has been placed but contains little stored force.

### Dense

The anchor has absorbed movement, impacts or nearby deaths.

### Critical

The anchor is close to collapse and heavily influences everything around it.

The player does not manage numerical Mass values during combat. Each level has a clear visual state:

- Dormant anchors distort light slightly
- Dense anchors begin pulling loose objects toward them
- Critical anchors warp nearby bodies and produce deep structural sounds

## Increasing Mass

Anchors gain Mass through actions such as:

- Pulling enemies toward them
- Causing enemies to collide near them
- Passing projectiles through their field
- Killing attached enemies
- Blocking attacks near them
- Performing high-impact melee attacks
- Moving rapidly between anchors
- Absorbing another destroyed anchor

Different builds load anchors in different ways.

## Collapsing an anchor

Collapse skills consume an anchor and release its Mass.

The result depends on:

- How much Mass it contained
- What it was attached to
- Which ability collapsed it
- Whether other anchors were connected
- The Voidbringer’s current Instability
- Which progression paths the player has invested in

A ground anchor might implode an area.

An enemy anchor might rupture the target internally.

A wall anchor might produce a directional shockwave.

A self-anchor might turn the Voidbringer into the center of the collapse.

---

# Fold Lines

Active anchors are connected by barely visible distortions called **Fold Lines**.

The player does not manually draw them. They form automatically between compatible anchors.

Fold Lines provide advanced interaction without requiring another resource or interface.

Abilities may:

- Travel along Fold Lines
- Cut everything crossing them
- Pull enemies toward their midpoint
- Accelerate projectiles passing through them
- Allow the Voidbringer to move between anchors
- Transfer Mass from one anchor to another
- Redirect attacks from one endpoint to another

A beginner can largely ignore Fold Lines.

A veteran builds around their shape.

This is one of the primary sources of the class’s long-term mastery.

---

# Primary resource: Instability

Manipulating reality generates **Instability**.

Instability ranges from zero to one hundred.

## Instability generation

Instability rises when the Voidbringer:

- Places anchors
- Folds large distances
- Redirects heavy projectiles
- Repeats spatial abilities
- Increases personal mass
- Forces multiple enemies into the same position
- Uses abilities through several Fold Lines

## Instability release

Instability is primarily reduced by:

- Collapsing anchors
- Severing Fold Lines
- Releasing stored momentum
- Ending certain defensive states
- Successfully resolving a Breach

Instability is therefore not ordinary mana.

The Voidbringer does not run out of power. They become increasingly dangerous and difficult to contain.

---

# Breach state

Reaching maximum Instability triggers a temporary **Breach**.

Reality loses its grip around the Voidbringer.

During Breach:

- All active anchors exert stronger influence
- Fold Lines become damaging spatial seams
- Many abilities originate from both the Voidbringer and an anchor
- Pulls become more violent
- Projectiles bend more aggressively
- Movement abilities travel farther
- Critical anchors begin producing secondary effects

Instability drains throughout the Breach.

## Resolving Breach correctly

Collapsing anchors during Breach slows the Instability drain and strengthens the next spatial action.

If the player destroys or safely resolves their anchor network before the Breach ends, they achieve a **Clean Closure**.

Clean Closure provides:

- A temporary defensive barrier
- Faster anchor placement
- Reduced Instability generation
- A bonus based on how much Mass was successfully collapsed

If Breach expires while several critical anchors remain, the Voidbringer suffers **Spatial Recoil**:

- Movement becomes heavy
- Event Step loses range temporarily
- The remaining anchors collapse inefficiently
- Stored Mass is partially wasted

This is not intended as a severe punishment. It creates pressure to finish the sequence properly.

The class fantasy becomes:

> Build toward losing control, then prove that you never actually lost it.

---

# Breach Laws

Later progression allows players to choose a **Breach Law** that determines how their Breach behaves.

Only one Law may be active at a time, but it can be changed outside combat.

## Law of Compression

During Breach, everything is pulled toward the nearest anchor.

Best for:

- Control
- Implosions
- Dense enemy groups
- Close-range builds
- Boss stagger

## Law of Inversion

During Breach, pulls can become violent repulsions and Fold Lines reverse directional force.

Best for:

- Enemy launching
- Defensive control
- Collision builds
- Projectile reflection
- Terrain impacts

## Law of Orbit

During Breach, enemies and projectiles begin circling anchors instead of moving directly toward them.

Best for:

- Projectile builds
- Sustained damage
- Orbital shard setups
- Mobile combat
- Building extreme momentum

These Laws make the same core mechanic support fundamentally different builds.

# Weapon families

The containment harness remains part of the character, but players equip different **Manifold Frames** that alter how the Null Shards behave.

## Shear Frames

Null Shards form close-range weapons:

- Cleavers
- Talons
- Segmented blades
- Crushing fists
- Spatial axes

Shear Frames favor:

- Melee
- Collisions
- Personal Mass
- Armor
- Point-blank collapse

## Vector Arrays

Null Shards become orbiting or launched projectiles.

Vector Arrays favor:

- Projectile speed
- Ricochets
- Orbiting ammunition
- Trajectory manipulation
- Long-range anchor interaction

## Well Cores

Null Shards remain detached and improve the anchor network.

Well Cores favor:

- Additional anchor functionality
- Area control
- Mass generation
- Powerful collapses
- Fold Line interactions

The weapons do not lock the player out of abilities. They influence animations, bonuses and available item effects.

A melee Voidbringer can still place distant anchors. A control build can still form a blade when enemies get close.

# Core ability package

Every Voidbringer begins with access to the same foundational tools.

These establish the class identity before the player specializes.

---

## Mass Brand

The Voidbringer launches or drives a Null Shard into an enemy or surface, creating a Mass Anchor.

Against an enemy, Mass Brand deals a small impact and follows the target.

Against terrain, it creates a larger stable field.

### Possible mutations

**Carrier Brand**

Enemy anchors remain active briefly after the enemy dies, attached to the corpse.

**Grave Brand**

Enemies killed while branded transfer their Mass into a nearby anchor.

**Penetrating Brand**

The shard passes through the first target and anchors the second.

**Terminal Brand**

The player may only maintain one anchor, but it has much greater capacity and strength.

**Splinter Brand**

Mass Brand creates two weaker anchors positioned closely together.

---

## Null Shard

Launch a shard that damages the first enemy struck.

The projectile accelerates whenever it crosses a Fold Line.

At extreme speed it punches through enemies and carries part of their momentum into the next target.

### Build potential

- Basic ranged weapon
- Fold Line railgun
- Orbiting ammunition
- Anchor-loading projectile
- Ricochet tool
- Defensive projectile interception

---

## Worldshear

Form a short spatial blade and cut through enemies.

If the attack crosses a Fold Line, the line ruptures and repeats the cut along its entire length.

This is the foundational melee ability but can also serve control and ranged builds.

### Possible mutations

**Crosscut**

Worldshear ruptures every Fold Line crossed rather than only one.

**Pressure Edge**

Damage increases based on the Mass stored at both ends of the line.

**Open Wound**

The severed space remains open briefly, damaging enemies that cross it.

**Event Blade**

Worldshear becomes a slower two-handed attack with enormous collision force.

---

## Event Step

Fold the distance between the Voidbringer and a targeted anchor.

The Voidbringer does not travel across the space normally. Both positions are briefly treated as adjacent.

Enemies caught along the folded route are compressed together.

Without a target anchor, Event Step becomes a shorter directional displacement.

### Possible mutations

**Exchange**

Swap positions with an anchored enemy.

**Smuggle**

Carry one nearby enemy through the Fold.

**Severance**

Leave a temporary spatial cut along the route.

**Afterevent**

A delayed echo repeats the Voidbringer’s previous attack at the starting location.

**Closed Circuit**

Event Step does not consume movement momentum when traveling between two critical anchors.

---

## Hard Vacuum

Compress the air and loose matter around the Voidbringer.

For a brief duration:

- Nearby projectiles slow
- Lightweight enemies are pulled closer
- Sound becomes muffled
- Incoming impact force is stored

When Hard Vacuum ends, stored force may be released outward or transferred into an anchor.

This is both defensive and offensive.

---

# Event Horizon discipline

## Identity

Event Horizon focuses on battlefield architecture, anchor networks and catastrophic collapse.

The player feels like a calculating executioner arranging the room before destroying it.

## Core strengths

- Group control
- Area damage
- Boss stagger
- Anchor capacity
- Terrain interaction
- Chain reactions

## Weaknesses

- Requires setup
- Poor anchor placement reduces effectiveness
- Less immediately mobile
- Enemies that constantly relocate can interrupt geometry

---

## Convergence

Pull all affected enemies toward the midpoint between two anchors.

Enemies arriving from opposite directions collide.

The collision damage depends on:

- Their combined weight
- Their velocity
- Anchor Mass
- Distance traveled

Convergence can therefore be used to:

- Group enemies
- Slam enemies together
- Pull enemies away from allies
- Crush smaller enemies against bosses
- Interrupt attacks
- Load both anchors

### Advanced mutation: Shared Center

Instead of pulling toward the geometric midpoint, the player chooses which anchor becomes dominant.

### Advanced mutation: False Center

The convergence point appears somewhere that contains no anchor, allowing more flexible traps.

---

## Crush Point

Collapse one selected anchor into a concentrated implosion.

The area is relatively small, but its damage and armor destruction scale heavily with stored Mass.

When used against an enemy carrying the anchor, the implosion begins inside the target.

### Mutations

**Internal Failure**

Reduced area but massively increased damage to the branded enemy.

**Accretion**

Enemies killed by Crush Point add their remaining body mass to another anchor.

**Vacancy**

The collapse leaves an empty region that continues pulling enemies inward.

---

## Tidal Lock

Bind an enemy into orbit around an anchor.

The target continues attempting its normal actions but is forced along a circular path.

Possible uses:

- Pull a melee enemy away from the player
- Drag enemies through hazards
- Repeatedly collide orbiting targets
- Force projectiles to fire in changing directions
- Spin a heavy enemy into nearby targets

Larger enemies resist full orbit but still experience directional displacement.

---

## Black Meridian

Select two anchors and intensify the Fold Line between them.

Anything crossing the line is spatially divided.

The line itself deals relatively low repeated damage, but enemies forcibly dragged through it suffer extreme damage.

This creates a build based on using positioning and movement as the actual weapon.

---

## Event Horizon major nodes

### Accretive Violence

Enemy deaths near an anchor add Mass based on the enemy’s size.

### Common Grave

Collapsing one critical anchor pulls nearby anchors into the same event.

### Heavy Silence

Enemies being pulled cannot use movement abilities, but the Voidbringer’s own movement is reduced near critical anchors.

### Terminal Geometry

Maintaining exactly three anchors causes Fold Lines to form a closed shape. Enemies inside it experience increasing pressure.

### No Escape Vector

Enemies moving away from an anchor add Mass to it.

### Black Sun

Critical anchors gradually consume nearby projectiles and release them during Collapse.

---

# Redshift discipline

## Identity

Redshift focuses on speed, stolen momentum, projectiles and violent repositioning.

This is not a stationary caster.

The Redshift Voidbringer races through the anchor network, bends attacks and converts movement into damage.

## Core strengths

- Extreme mobility
- Projectile manipulation
- Sustained pressure
- Rapid Breach generation
- High technical ceiling

## Weaknesses

- Less durable
- Requires constant movement
- Can lose damage if momentum is interrupted
- Poor positioning can send attacks in the wrong direction

---

## Velocity Theft

Strip momentum from enemies and hostile projectiles in a targeted area.

Affected objects slow dramatically.

The stolen momentum is stored temporarily and can enhance:

- Event Step distance
- Projectile velocity
- Worldshear impact
- Enemy launching
- The next Collapse

Velocity Theft does not merely apply a slow. It transfers physical movement from one thing to another.

---

## Periapsis

Rush around an anchor in a curved path and strike enemies along the orbit.

The closer the Voidbringer passes to the anchor, the faster the exit velocity becomes.

Players can use Periapsis to:

- Circle behind enemies
- Build momentum
- Avoid frontal attacks
- Load an anchor
- Launch into another ability
- Turn a stationary anchor into a movement tool

---

## Rail Collapse

Destroy an anchor by launching it forward as a hypervelocity object.

An enemy carrying the anchor is violently thrown.

A terrain anchor tears free a piece of condensed space and fires it along the targeted direction.

Damage increases based on:

- Stored Mass
- Launch distance
- Fold Lines crossed
- Enemies penetrated
- Momentum stored through Velocity Theft

This supports a full ranged artillery build without becoming a normal spellcaster.

---

## Trajectory Theft

Capture the directional intent of incoming projectiles.

The captured attacks begin orbiting the Voidbringer or a selected anchor.

They can then be:

- Released toward another target
- Fed into a Collapse
- Redirected through Fold Lines
- Used as temporary protection
- Accelerated through an orbit

Boss projectiles may be partially redirected rather than fully captured.

---

## Redshift major nodes

### Stolen Distance

Movement performed by slowed enemies increases Event Step range.

### No Rest Frame

The Voidbringer deals increased damage while both they and the target are moving.

### Relativistic Edge

Worldshear damage scales with the Voidbringer’s current movement velocity.

### Ricochet Geometry

Projectiles striking a Fold Line change direction toward another anchor.

### Slingshot Flesh

Enemies released from orbit become physical projectiles capable of damaging other enemies.

### Redline

Using three movement or trajectory abilities rapidly causes the next action to occur from both the starting and ending position.

---

# Hollow Form discipline

## Identity

Hollow Form focuses on turning the Voidbringer’s own body into a gravitational weapon.

It is the heavy melee, counterattack and close-range discipline, but it should never feel like an ordinary warrior.

The player does not become powerful by swinging harder.

They become powerful by becoming too heavy for reality to move correctly.

## Core strengths

- Close-range destruction
- Survivability
- Point-blank control
- Impact damage
- Enemy collision
- Personal anchor builds

## Weaknesses

- Reduced range
- Slower while heavily loaded
- High Instability generation
- Requires commitment to dangerous positions

---

## Gravitic Skin

Increase personal Mass and form orbiting Null Shards around the body.

While active:

- Incoming knockback is reduced
- Nearby enemies are pulled closer
- Heavy attacks cause larger impacts
- Dodging becomes shorter but more forceful
- The Voidbringer can act as a Mass Anchor

The player may release Gravitic Skin to launch the stored defensive force outward.

---

## Mass Driver

Form a massive gravitational limb and strike forward.

The attack does not simply knock enemies backward.

It temporarily increases the target’s mass and launches everything else away from the impact point.

Against large enemies, the Voidbringer is pulled toward the target instead.

This keeps the ability useful against both normal enemies and bosses.

---

## Zero-Range Collapse

Collapse all nearby spatial pressure directly around the Voidbringer.

Enemies are pulled against the character before the implosion.

The attack becomes stronger based on:

- Personal Mass
- Nearby enemy Mass
- Active Gravitic Skin
- Number of anchors attached to enemies
- Damage recently prevented

This is the defining Living Singularity ability.

---

## Countermass

Briefly oppose an incoming attack with equal spatial force.

A correctly timed Countermass:

- Negates displacement
- Stores impact
- Adds Mass to the attacker’s anchor
- Creates a Fold Line between the Voidbringer and the enemy
- Empowers the next melee attack

A mistimed use still provides partial protection, ensuring it is not useless to less-skilled players.

---

## Hollow Form major nodes

### Personal Gravity

The Voidbringer permanently counts as a Mass Anchor.

### Absolute Body

While stationary, personal Mass steadily increases. Moving releases it through the first impact made.

### Bone Orbit

Null Shards orbit closer to the body and damage enemies pulled into point-blank range.

### Counterweight

Anchored enemies deal less damage to the Voidbringer but become harder to move.

### Weight of the Dead

Nearby corpses contribute to personal Mass until the Voidbringer leaves the area.

### Unmovable Object

At maximum personal Mass, the Voidbringer cannot use normal movement but Event Step becomes significantly stronger.

# Cross-discipline mastery

The most interesting builds should mix disciplines rather than simply selecting one path.

## Event Horizon + Redshift
### Orbital Executioner

This hybrid uses anchor geometry to accelerate projectiles and enemies around the battlefield.

Gameplay includes:

- Orbiting ammunition
- Curved rail attacks
- Slingshot movement
- Projectile capture
- Remote Collapse
- Constant anchor repositioning

---

## Redshift + Hollow Form
### Kinetic Butcher

This hybrid turns personal movement into close-range impact damage.

Gameplay includes:

- High-speed Event Steps
- Relativistic Worldshear
- Countermass
- Launching through enemy groups
- Stealing boss momentum
- Heavy attacks repeated from movement echoes

---

## Hollow Form + Event Horizon
### Living Singularity

This hybrid places the Voidbringer at the center of the anchor network.

Gameplay includes:

- Pulling entire groups into melee range
- Extreme personal Mass
- Point-blank Collapse
- Defensive projectile consumption
- Corpses contributing weight
- Slow but overwhelming battlefield control

# Hybrid keystones

These require meaningful investment in two disciplines.

## Catapult Doctrine
### Event Horizon + Redshift

Anchored enemies can be fired through Fold Lines as living projectiles.

Enemy collision damage becomes a primary build strategy.

## Devouring Center
### Event Horizon + Hollow Form

Ground anchors cannot be placed. The Voidbringer becomes the center point of every active Fold Line.

## Impact Event
### Redshift + Hollow Form

Event Step no longer compresses enemies along its path. Instead, all compressed force is released through the next melee strike.

## Closed Orbit
### Redshift + Event Horizon

Projectiles cannot travel directly toward an anchored enemy. They must orbit at least once, gaining damage and speed before striking.

## Collapsing Body
### Hollow Form + Event Horizon

Collapsing a self-anchor damages the Voidbringer’s containment barrier but causes every nearby enemy anchor to collapse simultaneously.

# Ultimate ability: Dead Star

The Voidbringer creates a miniature dead star above the battlefield.

The star initially has little power.

During the ultimate:

- Enemies are gradually pulled toward it
- Projectiles begin entering orbit
- Dead enemies contribute Mass
- Collapsed anchors feed the star
- Fold Lines redirect toward its center
- The Voidbringer can move the star by sacrificing an anchor
- Heavy enemies distort its position
- The star grows visibly denser

The player decides when to end Dead Star.

## Early detonation

Produces a controlled implosion with lower damage and no Spatial Recoil.

## Late detonation

Creates a catastrophic collapse but may pull the Voidbringer into the impact if the anchor network was not properly arranged.

## Discipline interactions

### Event Horizon

The star becomes larger, slower and capable of consuming entire anchor networks.

### Redshift

The star forms a violent orbit that accelerates captured projectiles before releasing them.

### Hollow Form

The star descends into the Voidbringer’s body, temporarily transforming them into a walking collapse point.

Dead Star is therefore not one identical cinematic button. Its behavior reflects the build.

# Example endgame builds

## Black Sun Architect

### Identity

A deliberate battlefield-control build based on creating a three-anchor enclosure.

### Combat loop

1. Place three anchors around an enemy group.
2. Build Mass through Convergence and enemy deaths.
3. Form a closed Fold Line structure.
4. Drag enemies repeatedly through Black Meridian.
5. Trigger Breach.
6. Collapse the structure into Dead Star.

### Strengths

- Huge area control
- Excellent pack destruction
- Strong boss stagger
- Safe positioning

### Weaknesses

- Setup time
- Less effective against constantly teleporting enemies
- Requires accurate anchor placement

---

## Orbital Executioner

### Identity

A ranged build using Null Shards and captured enemy projectiles as orbiting ammunition.

### Combat loop

1. Establish anchors.
2. Capture projectiles with Trajectory Theft.
3. Force them into orbit.
4. Build speed through Fold Lines.
5. Release them through Rail Collapse.
6. Enter Law of Orbit during Breach.

### Strengths

- High sustained ranged damage
- Converts enemy attacks into resources
- Extremely strong against projectile-heavy encounters
- Visually distinctive

### Weaknesses

- Requires active anchor maintenance
- Less effective when enemies provide no projectiles
- Vulnerable while preparing a large orbit

---

## Living Singularity

### Identity

A heavy close-range build where the player becomes the anchor.

### Combat loop

1. Activate Gravitic Skin.
2. Walk or Event Step into enemy groups.
3. Pull everything inward.
4. Use Countermass to absorb heavy impacts.
5. Increase personal Mass.
6. Trigger Zero-Range Collapse.
7. Resolve Breach through self-centered anchor destruction.

### Strengths

- Extremely durable
- Brutal point-blank damage
- Strong enemy grouping
- Simple foundation with deep timing mastery

### Weaknesses

- Limited range
- Reduced movement at maximum Mass
- Dangerous against enemies that punish close proximity

---

## Kinetic Butcher

### Identity

A fast melee build that converts movement velocity into cutting force.

### Combat loop

1. Steal momentum from enemies.
2. Use Periapsis to accelerate around an anchor.
3. Event Step through the group.
4. Release the accumulated movement through Worldshear.
5. Repeat the attack from the starting position through Afterevent.
6. Launch survivors into terrain.

### Strengths

- Extreme mobility
- Strong burst damage
- High mastery ceiling
- Excellent target switching

### Weaknesses

- Less defensive
- Loses damage when immobilized
- Requires mechanical precision

---

## Mass Driver

### Identity

A collision build that turns enemies into weapons.

### Combat loop

1. Brand several enemies.
2. Increase their Mass.
3. Position anchors around the battlefield.
4. Launch enemies through Fold Lines.
5. Slam smaller enemies into elites.
6. Use boss summons as ammunition against the boss.

### Strengths

- Unique combat behavior
- Excellent crowd destruction
- Uses enemy composition dynamically
- Strong environmental interaction

### Weaknesses

- Bosses cannot be launched normally
- Performs differently depending on enemy size
- Requires knowledge of collision angles

---

## Breach Engine

### Identity

A high-risk build designed to remain in Breach as often as possible.

### Combat loop

1. Generate Instability rapidly.
2. Enter Breach before fully preparing the anchor network.
3. Build and collapse anchors during the Breach.
4. Extend the state through successful collapses.
5. Chain Clean Closures into the next Breach.
6. Accept severe Spatial Recoil when the sequence fails.

### Strengths

- Enormous power ceiling
- Constant altered ability behavior
- Rewards mastery
- Fast aggressive combat

### Weaknesses

- Highly punishing when mismanaged
- Requires precise ability sequencing
- Vulnerable between failed Breaches

# Gear interaction philosophy

Voidbringer equipment should not merely provide:

- More damage
- More critical chance
- More Instability
- More anchor damage

Those statistics may exist, but memorable equipment should change the player’s relationship with space.

## Example build-changing properties

### The Last Orbit

Anchors can no longer attach to terrain.

All anchors orbit the Voidbringer at different distances.

This turns the class into a mobile orbital weapons platform.

---

### Grave of Distance

Event Step no longer moves the Voidbringer instantly.

Instead, the space between both points collapses toward the player, dragging enemies and objects with it.

---

### The Unfallen Weight

The Voidbringer cannot be displaced.

Every prevented displacement is stored as Mass, but Event Step generates significantly more Instability.

---

### Inward-Facing World

Pull effects become repulsions.

Repulsed enemies gain Mass based on the distance traveled and implode when they strike terrain.

---

### The Empty Throne

The player may maintain only one anchor.

That anchor can reach five Mass stages and alters nearby environmental physics.

---

### Funeral Geometry

Corpses can become permanent anchors until collapsed.

Corpse anchors cannot move but gain Mass from nearby deaths.

---

### Lightless Velocity

Null Shards become invisible while accelerating.

They deal increased damage based on how many Fold Lines they crossed, but the player must read their trajectory through environmental distortion.

---

### Event Cannibal

Collapsing an anchor restores no Instability.

Instead, the next anchor absorbs the collapsed anchor’s Mass and effects.

This enables enormous delayed collapses but makes Breach management much harder.

# The mastery curve

## First hour

The player learns:

- Place anchors
- Pull enemies
- Collapse anchors
- Event Step toward anchors
- Avoid maximum Instability at bad times

The class already feels powerful and understandable.

## Ten hours

The player learns:

- Anchor placement matters
- Enemy weight changes collision behavior
- Fold Lines alter attacks
- Breach is an opportunity rather than a failure state
- Some abilities should load Mass while others release it

## Thirty hours

The player begins:

- Preparing Breach sequences
- Redirecting enemy attacks
- Using movement as damage
- Building hybrid discipline interactions
- Replacing standard rotations with situational geometry
- Using corpses and terrain as resources

## Endgame mastery

A master Voidbringer:

- Plans several collapses ahead
- Understands enemy weight and movement resistance
- Manipulates hostile projectiles
- Deliberately enters Breach at specific moments
- Changes anchor geometry during attacks
- Converts defensive actions into offensive Mass
- Uses the same skill differently depending on what carries the anchor

The skill ceiling comes from understanding relationships between abilities, not from pressing more buttons.

# Non-negotiable design rules

## Voidbringer must never become a generic dark mage

Avoid:

- Shadow bolts
- Demon summons
- Generic curses
- Purple explosions
- Ordinary teleport spam
- Passive damage circles with no spatial interaction

## Every major ability must interact with at least one of these:

- Mass
- Momentum
- Anchors
- Fold Lines
- Instability
- Position
- Collision

## Control must remain physical

Enemies should be:

- Dragged
- Launched
- Orbiting
- Compressed
- Pinned
- Redirected
- Crushed

Not merely “crowd controlled” through invisible status effects.

## Breach must remain intentional

The player should eventually want to enter Breach.

It should feel like operating a machine beyond its safe limits, not being punished for using abilities.

## Builds must change behavior, not only output

A build should not merely become:

> The same Voidbringer, but with stronger Collapse damage.

It should become:
