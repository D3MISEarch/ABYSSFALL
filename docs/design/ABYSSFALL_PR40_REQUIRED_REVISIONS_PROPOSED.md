# 1. World-Lattice and Voidbringer Codex reconciliation

The World-Lattice is the global containment architecture of the shared world. It is not a replacement name for the Black Measure, the Last Measure, or the Meridian Vault network.

## Required cosmology mapping

### World-Lattice

The **World-Lattice** is the broad, continent-scale family of incompatible systems used across history to force a wounded reality to remain coherent.

It may contain multiple anchoring traditions and technologies, including:

- spatial reference systems;
- ritual law;
- adaptive biological repair;
- dream-law containment;
- reliquary intelligence;
- fixation and mineralized stasis;
- pressure anchors;
- temporal restraints.

The Lattice is not one clean machine built by one culture. It is a layered emergency architecture created, repaired, misused, and partially forgotten across multiple civilizations.

### The Black Measure

The **Black Measure** remains the Voidbringer Codex’s approved spatial truth: accumulated rejected positions, distances never traveled, unresolved locations, and the pressure of places reality refused to occupy.

It is one of the deepest failure domains pressing against the World-Lattice. It is not generic “void energy,” and it is not automatically identical to every manifestation of the Abyss.

The shared campaign may suggest that the Black Measure is:

- one wound-layer of the Abyss;
- one measurable consequence of the deeper wound;
- one interface through which the wound enters ordinary reality.

Launch must not definitively collapse all Abyss phenomena into a single explanation.

### The Last Measure

The **Last Measure** remains the approved engineering order that discovered impossible Mass and built Manifold technology.

Within the shared history, it is a specialist order that learned to manipulate one anchoring principle: position, distance, momentum, Mass, and Closure.

It did not create the entire World-Lattice. It inherited, rediscovered, repaired, or connected to older global containment structures.

### Meridian Vaults

The **Meridian Vault network** is the Last Measure’s spatial-containment subsystem within the wider World-Lattice.

Required relationship:

- World-Lattice = global layered containment architecture.
- Meridian Vaults = spatial-reference facilities tied to fault lines and Black Measure pressure.
- Axis Vault = central Last Measure attempt to establish a universal spatial coordinate.
- Integrated Manifold Harness = living operator interface for local certainty and Closure.

This preserves the Codex’s central claim that machines can calculate possibilities but require a living Reference Body to decide which location matters.

### Mass Anchors, Fold Lines, Instability, and Breach

These remain canonical Voidbringer mechanical and narrative vocabulary.

Shared-campaign documents must not reduce them to generic gravity magic.

- **Mass Anchors** assign force and spatial importance to enemies, terrain, objects, corpses, projectiles, or the Voidbringer.
- **Fold Lines** are automatic spatial relationships between compatible anchors.
- **Instability** is the rising failure pressure caused by repeated manipulation of reality.
- **Breach** is controlled loss of spatial containment at maximum Instability.
- **Clean Closure** is the deliberate resolution of the anchor network before recoil.

The Voidbringer campaign arc must continue to ask whether control used for protection becomes domination.

### Manifold Trials

The eight **Manifold Trials** remain load-bearing Voidbringer class progression and narrative content.

They must be integrated into the shared campaign as class-specific trials reached through:

- the Gaugehouse;
- recovered Meridian routes;
- class-specific optional areas inside shared acts;
- erased or unstable locations uncovered as campaign regions fail.

They do not replace the six shared acts. The shared acts do not erase them.

Required production rule:

- Shared campaign completion must remain possible for every launch class.
- Voidbringer players receive additional Trial progression, dialogue, class revelations, and optional encounters.
- Trial completion may unlock class progression and endings without requiring eight separate campaign productions.

### Edras Vey

**Edras Vey** remains the approved Voidbringer class antagonist:

- First Integrated Operator;
- the Stillpoint;
- creator of the Stillpoint Engine doctrine;
- believer that distance is the first cruelty;
- intended destroyer of separation, movement, privacy, and individual position.

He must not be silently replaced by the Architect Below, Hollow King, Eightfold Custodian, or a generic final boss.

Recommended hierarchy:

- Edras is the culmination of the Voidbringer’s personal class arc.
- His Stillpoint plan exploits a major failure in the World-Lattice.
- His actions may worsen or reveal the shared campaign crisis.
- Defeating or rejecting Edras does not fully resolve the Architect Below or the Abyss.
- The shared final campaign confrontation may occur after, alongside, or through consequences of the Edras arc, but Edras must retain a complete dramatic resolution.

### Architect Below

The **Architect Below** remains deliberately ambiguous through launch.

It is not another name for Edras Vey.

Evidence may support several interpretations:

- a consciousness using the Lattice as a body;
- the sacrificed intelligence beneath the first Binding;
- a future civilization causing the wound backward through time;
- a false explanatory persona built by frightened institutions;
- a pattern emerging independently from several failure domains.

No launch document may declare one interpretation definitively canonical without a new owner decision.

# 2. Campaign scale revision

The six acts are the complete campaign vision, not the first simultaneous production target.

## Complete campaign structure

1. Sunken Crypts and the Hollow King
2. Red Basilica
3. Country Behind Sleep
4. Reliquary March
5. Inverted Sea
6. Hour Without End

## Production milestones

### Milestone A — Act 01 vertical campaign

Must prove:

- campaign exploration;
- quest flow;
- one major settlement or refuge;
- one hostile region with branching routes;
- elite encounters;
- one dungeon arc;
- one major boss;
- one class-personal mission integration;
- persistent loot, leveling, equipment, and saved-build continuity;
- cinematic tone without excessive cutscene dependence;
- environmental narrative delivery;
- replay value through builds and optional encounters.

### Milestone B — Early-campaign arc

Expand to a coherent two- or three-act arc only after measured Act 01 production data exists.

### Milestone C — Complete campaign

Complete all six acts as one escalating descent, not six unrelated class-biome demonstrations.

## Narrative escalation rule

Each act must deepen the same crisis:

- Act 01 reveals the world is structurally wounded.
- Act 02 reveals institutions preserved stability through sanctioned cruelty.
- Act 03 reveals perception and identity are part of containment.
- Act 04 reveals memory and the dead are active infrastructure.
- Act 05 reveals the world’s pressure systems contain non-human histories.
- Act 06 reveals time and causality were also bound into the same failing architecture.

The campaign must feel like one terrible truth being uncovered layer by layer.

# 3. Tone and originality requirements

## Approved tone

- dark;
- abysmal;
- dread-heavy;
- mythic;
- violent;
- serious;
- cinematic;
- emotionally grounded;
- oppressive but not colorless;
- mysterious but not evasive.

## Quality-reference rule

*Elden Ring* and *Diablo IV* are quality and mood references only.

AbyssFall must not copy:

- their cosmologies;
- named gods or demonic hierarchies;
- visual symbols;
- exact quest structures;
- faction roles;
- boss concepts;
- dialogue cadence;
- terminology;
- plot revelations;
- map progression;
- cinematic framing shot-for-shot.

## AbyssFall-owned identity

The universe stands apart through:

- incompatible anchoring principles embodied by classes;
- damaged survivors rather than chosen champions;
- reality as continuously enforced structure;
- biological, ritual, dream, relic, pressure, fixation, spatial, and temporal containment systems;
- characters whose powers are forms of damage, duty, or imprisonment;
- victories that stabilize or reclaim fragments without making existence safe;
- horror expressed through function and consequence, not random grotesquerie.

## Narrative delivery rules

- Environmental storytelling must support, not replace, understandable character motivations.
- Major bosses require personal, regional, and cosmological meaning.
- NPCs must have goals beyond explaining lore to the player.
- Tragedy must create choices, consequences, or changed relationships.
- Item lore may deepen mysteries but cannot be the only place the main plot is understandable.
- Dialogue should be severe, human, and precise; avoid parody-archaic sentence construction.
- Occasional dry humor may exist through character truth, never through tension-breaking quips.

# 4. Beginner-loop requirements

Every launch class summary must contain a beginner promise and a starter loop that can be understood before advanced systems unlock.

## Voidbringer

**Beginner promise:** Place anchors, pull enemies into them, and collapse the setup.

**Starter loop:**

1. Place one Mass Anchor.
2. Damage or move enemies near it to increase Mass.
3. Use one Fold or pull tool.
4. Collapse the anchor.
5. Learn Instability and Breach only after this loop feels natural.

## Penitent

**Beginner promise:** Mark enemies through deliberate attacks and rituals, then judge completed setups.

**Starter loop:**

1. Apply Rite progress.
2. Use a Seal or ritual movement tool.
3. Complete a Rite.
4. Cash it out with a judgment skill.
5. Add self-cost and multi-ritual sequencing later.

## Graftborn

**Beginner promise:** Fight to gain Biomass, grow the body needed for the encounter, rupture enemies, and smash the opening.

**Starter loop:**

1. Gain Biomass.
2. Grow one Arms adaptation.
3. Apply Rupture.
4. Consume Rupture with a heavy attack.
5. Introduce Trauma and multi-region architecture later.

## Somnarch

**Beginner promise:** Claim a small piece of the battlefield, place one rule inside it, and punish enemies who violate it.

**Starter loop:**

1. Establish one compact Sovereign Dream zone.
2. Choose one beginner Clause.
3. Build Dread through a clearly communicated action.
4. Trigger one payoff.
5. Add multi-Clause architecture and state transitions later.

## Relic Host

**Beginner promise:** Carry a small council of dead Presences, satisfy one demand, and invest the right Presence for the fight.

**Starter loop:**

1. Equip two beginner Presences outside combat.
2. Fulfill one visible Memory Condition.
3. Build Accord.
4. Quick-swap Investiture during combat.
5. Add Conflict, complex Testaments, and larger Councils later.

## Gorgon

**Beginner promise:** Choose prey, hold its attention, build Fixation, and break it during a deliberate execution window.

**Starter loop:**

1. Mark one Exposed target.
2. Build Fixation with attacks and one counter tool.
3. Enter a Lock State.
4. Execute or shatter the target.
5. Add venom-family routing and reflection mastery later.

## Tidewrought

**Beginner promise:** Build pressure, push enemies into bad routes, create a Breach, and release the stored sea.

**Starter loop:**

1. Build one Depth band.
2. Apply Soaked or Compression.
3. Use one short Current Line.
4. Create a Breach window.
5. Release pressure before Hull Strain becomes dangerous.

## Anachron

**Beginner promise:** Record one short action, mark a delayed consequence, and replay the authored attack at the correct moment.

**Starter loop candidate only:**

1. Record one whitelisted attack.
2. Apply one delayed mark.
3. Reprise the attack once.
4. Resolve the delayed consequence.
5. No broader system unlock until the deterministic replay spike passes.

Anachron’s beginner loop is conceptual only and does not authorize implementation.

# 5. Boss-safe class-mechanic requirements

Every class summary must name how its signature mechanic remains useful against bosses without trivializing them.

## Voidbringer — Spatial resistance conversion

Bosses resist unrestricted displacement, but Mass Anchors, Fold Lines, and collapse effects convert into:

- stagger pressure;
- limb or armor stress;
- directional vulnerability;
- projectile and hazard redirection;
- scripted displacement during valid windows;
- Clean Closure rewards.

## Penitent — Rite and ritual boss scaling

Bosses remain valid Rite targets. Ritual completion must create:

- judgment damage;
- stagger or posture pressure;
- vulnerability windows;
- phase-specific ritual interactions;
- self-cost decisions.

Bosses cannot be permanently locked by Seals or ritual law.

## Graftborn — Rupture weak-point conversion

Boss Rupture converts into:

- increased stagger damage;
- temporary weak-point exposure;
- improved Biomass return;
- finisher eligibility during scripted vulnerability windows;
- build-specific structural damage.

## Somnarch — Nightmare Pressure

Bosses cannot be permanently subjected to binary dream control. Dream Clauses build **Nightmare Pressure**, which converts into:

- phase debuffs;
- local hazard changes;
- stagger pressure;
- Dread generation;
- temporary rule enforcement during valid windows.

## Relic Host — Testament and Accord scaling

Bosses satisfy or contest Memory Conditions through readable actions. Presence mechanics convert into:

- Accord generation;
- stance-specific damage or defense windows;
- Last Testament triggers;
- boss-pattern counters;
- controlled Conflict decisions.

Ejection must never hard-disable the boss or remove player control.

## Gorgon — Monument Pressure

Bosses resist full petrification. Fixation builds **Monument Pressure**, which produces:

- carapace fractures;
- stagger and weak-point windows;
- reflected attack opportunities;
- venom-family conversions;
- phase-specific shatter payoffs.

## Tidewrought — Pressure Fracture

Bosses resist arbitrary forced movement. Compression and Current interactions build **Pressure Fracture**, which converts into:

- stagger;
- armor or shell stress;
- repositioning during valid windows;
- Breach vulnerability;
- redirected boss momentum rather than free displacement.

## Anachron — Temporal Strain

Bosses cannot be frozen, freely rewound, or restored. Valid authored replays build **Temporal Strain**, which may produce:

- delayed damage windows;
- stagger pressure;
- vulnerability to Convergence;
- limited phase-safe positional echoes;
- no restoration of boss health, phases, drops, or destroyed state.

This remains design-only pending the replay spike.

# 6. Required prototype gates

## Somnarch territory-readability spike

Must test:

- narrow rooms;
- open arenas;
- multiple elevations where supported;
- dense enemy packs;
- overlapping enemy telegraphs;
- controller targeting;
- colorblind and low-effects readability;
- one beginner Clause versus multiple advanced Clauses;
- boss Nightmare Pressure.

Pass criteria:

- Player can identify zone boundary, active Clause, Dread state, and enemy telegraphs at a glance.
- The class works without covering the arena in opaque effects.
- The beginner controller loop uses no more simultaneous management than the current input budget can support.

## Relic Host combat-tempo spike

Must test:

- two-Presence beginner Council;
- in-combat quick Investiture;
- visible Memory Conditions;
- active Accord generation;
- one Last Testament;
- Conflict without random loss of control;
- boss-pattern interaction;
- no mid-pack menu dependence.

Pass criteria:

- Rotation feels like weapon or stance mastery, not administrative upkeep.
- The player acts more often than the UI explains.
- Presence identity is communicated through animation, sound, and skill mutation.

## Tidewrought arena-geometry spike

Must test:

- straight corridors;
- irregular obstacle fields;
- circular arenas;
- open boss rooms;
- enemies immune or resistant to movement;
- current routing around walls;
- telegraph preservation;
- clear visual distinction from gravity.

Pass criteria:

- Current Lines never require a specially authored arena to function.
- Invalid routes fail cleanly and predictably.
- Pressure Fracture replaces forced movement against resistant bosses.
- Hull Strain rewards rhythm rather than punishing normal ability use.

## Anachron deterministic replay spike

Must be isolated from full class production.

Whitelisted prototype payload:

- one direct attack;
- one movement action;
- one delayed consequence;
- one Reprise;
- no loot, kill reward, item, quest, world-object, summon, or boss-phase restoration.

Must prove:

- exactly-once cost and cooldown behavior;
- exactly-once damage payload capture;
- no recursive proc emission;
- no duplicate death or reward event;
- target validity checks;
- collision-safe replay movement;
- deterministic results under identical inputs;
- session isolation;
- safe save/load behavior if replay state is ever persisted.

Failure to prove these rules keeps Anachron held without weakening or deleting the design bible.

# 7. Graftborn vertical-slice revision

The approved bounded slice remains:

- Biomass;
- Arms and Torso regions;
- two Major Adaptations per region;
- Rupture application and consumption;
- one Trauma Imprint;
- one cross-path interaction;
- one build-changing unique effect.

## Added constraints

- The slice must remain agnostic to the unresolved global class-item-slot decision.
- Anatomical state must serialize through existing approved save boundaries or remain session-only until an ADR approves persistence changes.
- Trauma may remember approved damage-family metadata, never replay raw arbitrary event payloads.
- Rupture must include boss conversion in the first slice.
- The starter loop must work with one Body Region before two-region synergy is taught.
- The unique effect must alter behavior, not merely add damage.
- The slice must measure animation, VFX, UI, test, and content cost before the full anatomy matrix is planned.
