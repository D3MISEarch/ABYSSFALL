# AbyssFall Campaign and Shared Systems Bible

> Status: **PROPOSED — owner review required.**
>
> This document establishes a shared campaign frame and numerical vocabulary for future class bibles. It does not replace approved class mechanics, existing prototype behavior, or implementation priorities.

## 1. Locked project constraints

These points are treated as established direction rather than proposals:

- AbyssFall uses one shared world and one campaign timeline.
- Every launch class experiences the same central campaign while receiving a personal arc inside it.
- Launch roster: Voidbringer, Penitent, Graftborn, Somnarch, Relic Host, Gorgon, Tidewrought, and Anachron.
- Choirborn, Echo Thief, and Plaguebringer remain expansion or specialization concepts.
- Progression is persistent: character level, equipment, class trees, currencies, and saved builds persist between sessions.
- Combat is not interrupted by mandatory three-choice upgrade popups.
- Seasonal content expands persistent characters rather than forcing resets.
- Temporary blessings, curses, and run modifiers are deferred to optional modes such as Abyss Siege.

## 2. Existing canon anchors

The following are already represented in the repository and must remain compatible with future narrative work:

- The Void Warlock controls gravity, portals, soul energy, and a living biomechanical Corruption resource.
- The Penitent physically writes rules into the battlefield through Brands, Circles, Rite Patterns, Fervor, ritual strikes, and sacrifice.
- The Sunken Crypts and the Hollow King exist as a playable location and boss encounter.
- AbyssFall is brutal dark fantasy rather than heroic high fantasy.

Everything below is proposed unless explicitly listed above.

# Part I — World and Campaign

## 3. Central premise

**PROPOSED:** The world is not being invaded by the Abyss. It is falling into an ancient wound that reality was built to conceal.

Centuries ago, a coalition of kingdoms, priesthoods, and pre-human powers constructed the **World-Lattice**, a continent-spanning system of seals, drowned engines, sacrificial bloodlines, and temporal anchors. The Lattice did not destroy the Abyss. It distributed its pressure across the world so no single place had to bear the whole weight.

The system is now failing.

Every region experiences the collapse differently: gravity folds, dreams become predatory geography, dead saints awaken inside relics, seas climb into the sky, blood remembers extinct bodies, and lost moments return wearing human faces. These are not separate apocalypses. They are symptoms of one structural failure.

The player belongs to one of eight condemned lineages capable of surviving contact with a broken Lattice node. Each class is useful because each is already partially incompatible with ordinary reality.

The campaign question is not simply, “Can the world be saved?” It is:

> What kind of world deserves to exist after the old one is exposed as a machine built from hidden atrocity?

## 4. Tone rules

1. **No clean institutions.** Every surviving power has compromised itself to endure.
2. **No weightless cosmic jargon.** Supernatural concepts must have bodily, architectural, ecological, or social consequences.
3. **Power leaves evidence.** Every major ability should scar flesh, terrain, memory, weather, or time.
4. **Victory changes the problem.** Bosses guard necessary functions; killing them may solve one danger while exposing another.
5. **The player is dangerous, not chosen.** The classes are tolerated because ordinary heroes cannot survive the work.
6. **Hope is earned through action, not prophecy.** The world may continue because people build something better, not because fate promised survival.
7. **Horror remains legible.** Enemies, factions, and regions should have understandable motives and rules even when visually grotesque.

## 5. Historical timeline

### ERA-00 — The First Descent

**PROPOSED:** Pre-human civilizations discover that reality has depth. Attempts to draw energy from the lower layers create the original world-wound.

### ERA-01 — The Binding Epoch

Surviving powers create the World-Lattice. Its construction requires eight incompatible anchoring principles that later correspond to the launch-class lineages: void, penance, flesh, dream, relic, venomous transformation, abyssal tide, and arrested time.

### ERA-02 — The Crowned Silence

Human kingdoms inherit fragments of the Lattice without understanding the whole. They convert maintenance rites into religion, monarchy, burial law, sea taboos, medical doctrine, and calendars.

### ERA-03 — The Hollow Reign

The ruler later known as the Hollow King attempts to centralize several Lattice functions beneath the Sunken Crypts. His court is consumed, but the node remains active in a damaged state.

### ERA-04 — The Present Fall

Multiple nodes fail within the same generation. The world begins physically descending toward the wound. The campaign starts after the first irreversible continental fracture.

## 6. Campaign structure

The campaign is designed as a shared six-act spine. Class-specific missions, dialogue, and choices occur within these acts rather than branching into separate campaigns.

### ACT-01 — The Sunken Crypts

**Purpose:** Establish the Lattice, the scale of the failure, and the fact that the Hollow King is both monster and broken custodian.

- Region: drowned necropolis beneath a collapsing frontier city.
- Existing anchor: Hollow King boss encounter.
- Core revelation: the crypt machinery was holding back pressure from below.
- Campaign consequence: defeating the Hollow King releases a map encoded into his empty crown, identifying seven additional failing anchors.
- Personal-arc function: each class recognizes a different impossible symbol in the crown-map.

### ACT-02 — The Red Basilica

**PROPOSED:** A pilgrimage metropolis where laws are carved into living bodies.

- Primary faction: The Covenant of Measure.
- Regional threat: uncontrolled rites rewrite guilt into physical mutations.
- Major boss: **The Absolution Engine**, a cathedral-sized organism that converts confession into fuel.
- Penitent emphasis: discovers that the priesthood’s rites descend from maintenance procedures for a Lattice node.
- Graftborn emphasis: finds evidence that “heretical” fleshcraft originated as emergency repair medicine.

### ACT-03 — The Country Behind Sleep

**PROPOSED:** A region overlapping the dreams of an entire dead population.

- Primary faction: The Quiet Parliament.
- Regional threat: dreams acquire legal ownership of waking bodies.
- Major boss: **The Sleepless Regent**, who has remained awake for generations by distributing nightmares into citizens.
- Somnarch emphasis: confronts the source or author of their sovereign dream-state.
- Anachron emphasis: finds memories of events that have not yet happened.

### ACT-04 — The Reliquary March

**PROPOSED:** A moving nation built from processional fortress-shrines carrying the remains of failed saints and dead machines.

- Primary faction: The Procession of Last Things.
- Regional threat: relics begin selecting new hosts without consent.
- Major boss: **Saint Orison, the Many-Bodied**, one consciousness distributed through thousands of devotional objects.
- Relic Host emphasis: must decide whether possession is inheritance, imprisonment, or symbiosis.
- Gorgon emphasis: encounters a saint whose preserved gaze fossilizes identity rather than flesh.

### ACT-05 — The Inverted Sea

**PROPOSED:** The ocean rises above the land as a suspended abyssal mass, exposing cities that were built on its floor.

- Primary faction: The Drowned Compact.
- Regional threat: pressure, memory, and predatory currents invade the atmosphere.
- Major boss: **The Ninth Tide**, a migrating intelligence using drowned armies as organs.
- Tidewrought emphasis: learns whether their transformation came from the sea or from the anchor beneath it.
- Voidbringer emphasis: gravity failures reveal portals are wounds guided by pressure differentials.

### ACT-06 — The Hour Without End

**PROPOSED:** The central Lattice citadel exists in several ruined eras simultaneously.

- Primary faction: remnants of every prior faction, each trying to control the final repair.
- Regional threat: cause and effect separate; dead bosses return as alternate outcomes rather than resurrections.
- Major bosses:
  - **The Calendar Eater**, guardian of the temporal anchor.
  - **The Eightfold Custodian**, a composite defense made from the original anchoring lineages.
  - **The Architect Below**, final campaign antagonist or final truth depending on owner decision.
- Anachron emphasis: becomes the only class able to perceive the citadel’s complete sequence.
- Shared climax: the player chooses how the Lattice is replaced, not merely whether it is restored.

## 7. Antagonist structure

### Layer A — Regional custodians

Bosses such as the Hollow King are failed maintainers, rulers, saints, or weapons bound to local Lattice functions. They provide immediate villains with tragic utility.

### Layer B — Mortal factions

Factions understand fragments of the collapse and pursue incompatible survival plans.

### Layer C — The Eightfold Custodian

**PROPOSED:** A system-level antagonist assembled from distorted versions of the eight anchoring principles. It exists to prevent unauthorized repair, even when following its orders will destroy the surviving world.

### Layer D — The Architect Below

**PROPOSED, HIGH-IMPACT OWNER DECISION:** Possible identities:

1. A conscious Abyss trying to become a world.
2. The sacrificed intelligence used as the World-Lattice’s foundation.
3. A future version of civilization that created the wound while escaping backward through time.
4. No singular being at all; “the Architect” is a lie invented to make a structural catastrophe emotionally manageable.

The campaign can be built through Act 05 without locking this answer.

## 8. Major factions

### FAC-01 — Covenant of Measure

Body-law priesthood maintaining ritual standards through confession, mutilation, and public accounting of sin.

### FAC-02 — Quiet Parliament

Dream-bound delegates who vote through shared sleep. Some members are dead, unborn, or imaginary.

### FAC-03 — Procession of Last Things

Nomadic reliquary nation that believes nothing sacred should remain buried where the Abyss can reclaim it.

### FAC-04 — Drowned Compact

Coastal survivors, pressure-adapted raiders, and abyssal diplomats who treat the rising sea as a political power.

### FAC-05 — The Unfinished

Fleshworkers, failed grafts, medical cults, and escaped war-organisms who reject the idea that a body has one correct shape.

### FAC-06 — The Meridian Court

Chronologists and executioners who erase dangerous events by murdering their causes.

### FAC-07 — The Black Transit

Void smugglers using unstable gates to move refugees, weapons, and forbidden relics between collapsing regions.

## 9. Class integration matrix

Every class receives three recurring personal-arc beats:

1. **Recognition:** an early campaign element reveals that the class is tied to the Lattice.
2. **Temptation:** a faction offers the class mastery at the cost of becoming a tool or custodian.
3. **Definition:** late campaign content lets the class define what their condition means beyond its original purpose.

### CLS-VOID — Voidbringer

- Recognition: the Hollow Crown map reacts to Corruption.
- Temptation: Black Transit offers stable portal mastery through deliberate feeding of the world-wound.
- Definition: choose whether void power is extraction, navigation, containment, or controlled collapse.

### CLS-PEN — Penitent

- Recognition: Rite Patterns match hidden maintenance grammar beneath religious scripture.
- Temptation: Covenant of Measure offers authority to rewrite human law through sacrificial geometry.
- Definition: decide whether guilt is a weapon, a debt, or a lie used to control bodies.

### CLS-GRA — Graftborn

- Recognition: their tissues contain dormant repair structures from the Binding Epoch.
- Temptation: The Unfinished offers unlimited adaptation at the cost of stable identity.
- Definition: choose a self-authored body rather than returning to an “original” form.

### CLS-SOM — Somnarch

- Recognition: recurring dreams are maps of sealed Lattice corridors.
- Temptation: Quiet Parliament offers a permanent kingdom inside collective sleep.
- Definition: determine whether dreams should govern waking reality, protect it, or remain sovereign but separate.

### CLS-REL — Relic Host

- Recognition: carried relics speak with voices that remember the Lattice’s construction.
- Temptation: Procession of Last Things offers elevation into a mobile saint-machine.
- Definition: establish terms of coexistence between host and inherited dead.

### CLS-GOR — Gorgon

- Recognition: their transformative gaze exposes the fixed pattern beneath living matter.
- Temptation: a petrified oracle offers freedom from change by turning entire populations into perfect memory.
- Definition: choose whether transformation preserves, punishes, liberates, or weaponizes identity.

### CLS-TID — Tidewrought

- Recognition: their blood responds to the Inverted Sea’s pressure-song.
- Temptation: Drowned Compact offers union with the Ninth Tide.
- Definition: decide whether they belong to land, sea, both, or neither.

### CLS-ANA — Anachron

- Recognition: remembers the campaign’s final citadel before reaching it.
- Temptation: Meridian Court offers the ability to erase their own disastrous origin.
- Definition: choose whether chronology is law, tool, burden, or negotiable consensus.

## 10. Endgame bridge

**PROPOSED:** The campaign ends with a temporary stabilization rather than total repair. The player creates a new network of living anchors using recovered node fragments and class-specific techniques.

This opens three endgame structures without invalidating the campaign:

- **Fracture Hunts:** revisit regions where alternate Lattice failures generate changed bosses and environments.
- **Anchor Depths:** descend through increasingly unstable layers beneath repaired nodes.
- **Abyss Siege:** later optional horde mode where temporary modifiers may be introduced without replacing persistent progression.

Persistent characters continue into all endgame modes. No canonical seasonal reset is required.

# Part II — Shared Numerical Design Framework

## 11. Design goals

The framework should make classes readable at first contact while leaving room for deep buildcraft through sequencing, conversion, positioning, resource control, and gear interaction.

It is a vocabulary and target model, not locked balance data.

## 12. Character scale

### Level range

**PROPOSED:**

- Campaign target: levels 1–50.
- Early endgame: 50–70.
- Long-term cap target: 100.

The prototype does not need to implement the full range immediately. Save formats should avoid hardcoding a low cap.

### Core attributes

Use a small universal set. Class trees and gear create complexity through interactions rather than ten base attributes.

- **Might:** physical force, stagger, armor requirements, heavy weapon scaling.
- **Finesse:** attack cadence, precision, evasion requirements, agile weapon scaling.
- **Will:** ritual power, control resistance, resource stability, occult scaling.
- **Vitality:** life, recovery thresholds, ailment tolerance.

No attribute should be mandatory for every build. Skills may have mixed scaling.

## 13. Offensive vocabulary

### Damage families

- **Physical:** impact, cut, puncture, crush.
- **Ritual:** imposed rules, sigils, brands, sacrificial effects.
- **Void:** gravity, spatial rupture, absence, portal shear.
- **Decay:** rot, venom, corrosion, failed regeneration.
- **Oneiric:** dream, terror, memory, sleep-state damage.
- **Relic:** sanctified or inherited force emitted by bound artifacts.
- **Tidal:** pressure, brine, cold depth, current force.
- **Temporal:** delayed, repeated, accelerated, or erased damage.

These are mechanical tags, not necessarily separate resistance bars. Early implementation may combine them into fewer defense channels while preserving tags for later gear and skill logic.

### Hit categories

- **Strike:** direct weapon or body contact.
- **Cast:** immediate projected effect.
- **Zone:** persistent area with ownership and duration.
- **Trigger:** effect caused by another condition completing.
- **Companion:** damage from a controlled entity or inherited presence.
- **Echo:** repeated portion of prior damage or action.

### Build-critical verbs

Use consistent wording across all class bibles and future tooltips:

- **Mark:** attach persistent class-readable state to a target.
- **Prime:** prepare a target, zone, skill, or resource for a stronger follow-up.
- **Complete:** satisfy a multi-step condition.
- **Consume:** remove a resource or state to cause an effect.
- **Convert:** change damage, cost, defense, resource, or state into another type.
- **Echo:** repeat part of an earlier action without counting as a full recast unless stated.
- **Rupture:** immediately end a persistent effect for a burst outcome.
- **Bind:** create a relationship between entities or between an entity and terrain.
- **Anchor:** prevent forced movement, displacement, state loss, or timeline change.
- **Overcap:** gain value beyond a normal maximum for temporary or conditional benefit.

## 14. Defensive vocabulary

### Universal layers

- **Life:** primary death threshold.
- **Armor:** reduces appropriate direct hit damage; effectiveness may vary against heavy hits.
- **Evasion:** chance or movement-based avoidance; never the only defense against unavoidable mechanics.
- **Ward:** renewable barrier protecting life.
- **Guard:** short-duration active mitigation tied to timing or stance.
- **Resistance:** mitigation against tagged non-physical damage families or status effects.
- **Recovery:** life, ward, or resource regained over time or through action.

### Control states

- **Stagger:** interruption pressure that can break ordinary enemies and open bosses during designated windows.
- **Slow:** reduces movement or action speed within explicit caps.
- **Root:** prevents voluntary movement but not actions.
- **Silence:** prevents designated casts; never disables a class’s entire kit in PvE without counterplay.
- **Displace:** pull, push, launch, swap, portal, or forced reposition.
- **Daze:** reduces tracking and reaction rather than fully stunning.

Hard crowd control on bosses should become **Break Pressure** contributing toward stagger windows rather than simply failing.

## 15. Resource conventions

Every class resource must answer four questions:

1. How is it generated through intended play?
2. What decision spends it?
3. What happens at high or low values?
4. How does gear alter the loop without erasing it?

### Shared rules

- Basic functionality cannot require perfect resource uptime.
- A resource should influence decisions, not merely function as a colored mana bar.
- Resource gains use clear sources and avoid hidden passive trickles as the primary loop.
- Overcap behavior should be deliberate and class-specific.
- Resource loss on death, room transition, or town entry must be explicitly defined per class.

## 16. Baseline combat targets

All values below are starting targets for prototypes, not final balance.

### Time-to-kill bands

- Fodder enemy: 0.5–2 seconds once a build is functioning.
- Standard enemy: 2–5 seconds.
- Elite enemy: 8–20 seconds depending on mechanics and density.
- Campaign boss phase: 45–120 seconds for a competent on-level build.

Dense packs should derive danger from overlapping roles, movement denial, and priority targets rather than every enemy becoming a health sponge.

### Cooldown bands

- Basic/core action: 0–1.5 seconds.
- Frequent tactical skill: 3–8 seconds.
- Major setup or payoff: 8–20 seconds.
- Ultimate: 45–120 seconds or resource/event-driven equivalent.

### Movement bands

- Every class receives a reliable movement or displacement tool.
- Movement skills should preserve class identity rather than all becoming identical dashes.
- Baseline movement cooldown target: 3–6 seconds before build investment.
- Boss arenas must remain navigable without mandatory movement-skill spam.

## 17. Skill-kit structure

Each class bible should define at least:

- One zero-cost or low-cost core action.
- One resource-building action.
- One resource-spending action.
- One movement tool.
- One defensive or recovery tool.
- One setup/control tool.
- One signature payoff.
- One ultimate.

Skills may fill multiple roles, but no class should depend on one button for generation, defense, movement, and damage simultaneously.

## 18. Persistent progression structure

### Class tree

**PROPOSED shared implementation shape:** one reusable node-graph framework with class-specific topology, art, rules, and node content.

Each class begins with three major paths. Cross-path links and keystones create hybrid builds. A class may later gain specialization layers, but the launch tree must support multiple distinct builds without subclasses being required.

### Skill points

- Earned persistently through level and campaign milestones.
- Banked until manually allocated.
- Never force a combat interruption.
- Allocation occurs in town, safe rooms, or an explicit menu state.

### Respec

- Free during development and balance testing.
- Data model should support a configurable future currency cost.
- Multiple saved build slots are a long-term requirement and should not depend on seasonal resets.

## 19. Equipment framework

### Proposed launch slots

- Main Hand
- Off Hand or Focus
- Head
- Chest
- Hands
- Feet
- Amulet
- Two Rings
- Class Relic

The Class Relic slot is mechanically distinct for each class and provides a controlled home for identity-defining item effects.

### Item layers

1. Base type establishes broad stat identity and requirements.
2. Rarity controls affix count, not raw color-coded superiority alone.
3. Affixes support general build direction.
4. Unique items change rules, conversions, targeting, costs, or sequencing.

A unique that only grants larger numbers is not sufficient unless the number creates a genuine threshold build.

### Unique-item design test

Every proposed unique should answer:

- What build does this enable or transform?
- What cost or constraint prevents universal best-in-slot status?
- What visual or audio evidence communicates its rule change?
- What happens when two transformative effects interact?

## 20. Status and ailment framework

### Shared ailments

- **Bleed:** moving or acting worsens a physical wound.
- **Poison:** stacking decay damage with class-specific conversion hooks.
- **Brittle:** increases stagger or break pressure received.
- **Dread:** oneiric vulnerability affecting behavior or control resistance.
- **Corrode:** reduces armor, ward efficiency, or object durability.
- **Drown:** tidal pressure state that intensifies when movement or escape conditions fail.
- **Desync:** temporal state causing delayed actions, repeats, or timing vulnerability.

Classes can create proprietary states, but universal ailments should remain consistent.

## 21. Boss and encounter rules

- Bosses must expose readable windows rather than being permanently immune to class mechanics.
- Class control contributes to Break Pressure when direct control is inappropriate.
- Bosses can interact with zones, marks, companions, and terrain without deleting them arbitrarily.
- Every boss should test positioning, target priority, timing, and build expression—not only damage output.
- Multi-phase bosses should change rules or geography, not merely refill health.
- Campaign bosses may drop a guaranteed class-relevant item choice without using an interrupting mid-combat card screen.

## 22. Co-op compatibility rules

The game may support solo and co-op, but no class may require another player to complete its loop.

- Party buffs must also provide a self-use benefit.
- Ally-targeted mechanics need a solo fallback involving companions, zones, or self-casting.
- Multiple instances of the same class should create additive or alternate interactions rather than overwriting all proprietary states.
- Screen readability must cap simultaneous persistent effects without silently reducing mechanical value.

## 23. Accessibility and readability rules

- Critical class states require more than color alone: shape, motion, icon, sound, or animation must reinforce them.
- Enemy and player zones use different edge language.
- Builds with many triggers need a readable hierarchy of primary and secondary effects.
- Camera shake, hit stop, bloom, and flashing must be individually adjustable.
- Input prompts dynamically match keyboard/mouse, PlayStation-family, and Xbox-family devices.
- Tooltips use the shared verbs in this document consistently.

# Part III — Consistency Check and Owner Decisions

## 24. Compatibility check

### Voidbringer

No existing mechanic is contradicted. Void, gravity, portals, soul energy, and Corruption fit the proposed Lattice pressure model. The full class bible must clarify whether “Void Warlock” remains the in-game name or becomes “Voidbringer.”

### Penitent

No existing mechanic is contradicted. Brands, Circles, Fervor, Rite Patterns, Ritual Blade, Seal of Binding, Brand of Ruin, Martyr’s Chain, Sacrament, Ashen Procession, and Cathedral of Flesh remain intact. Proposed campaign lore reframes religious rites as degraded Lattice maintenance grammar without invalidating their current presentation.

### Current progression pivot

The framework supports persistent skill points, equipment, respec, build slots, and uninterrupted combat. Optional modifiers remain outside core progression.

## 25. Explicit open questions for the owner

### OQ-01 — Final class naming

Should the current playable “Void Warlock” be formally renamed **Voidbringer**, or is Voidbringer a broader class title while Void Warlock remains the playable archetype?

### OQ-02 — Final antagonist truth

Which Architect Below model should become canon, or should the campaign intentionally preserve ambiguity through launch?

### OQ-03 — Campaign ending agency

Should the ending include a meaningful player choice affecting post-campaign world state, or should all classes converge on one canonical stabilization to simplify future content?

### OQ-04 — Hollow King role

Was the Hollow King originally a willing custodian, a punished ruler, or an ambitious usurper who accidentally became necessary?

### OQ-05 — World naming

The setting currently needs a canonical world, continent, and principal frontier-city name. Temporary design documents should avoid locking these until approved.

### OQ-06 — Resistance implementation

Should the first persistent-progression implementation expose all eight damage families as separate player-facing resistances, or use a smaller defense set while retaining detailed damage tags internally?

### OQ-07 — Attribute presentation

Approve or replace Might, Finesse, Will, and Vitality before itemization implementation begins.

### OQ-08 — Class Relic slot

Should every class receive a dedicated identity slot, or should transformative class effects live entirely in ordinary equipment slots?

### OQ-09 — Respec economy

Keep respec permanently free, charge a common currency, or reserve costs only for endgame tree reconstruction?

### OQ-10 — Endgame emphasis

Should Abyss Siege become the primary long-term endgame loop after launch, or remain one optional mode beside Fracture Hunts and Anchor Depths?

## 26. Next documentation deliverable

Create the full **Graftborn Class Bible** against this proposed framework. Any Graftborn mechanic that depends on an unresolved owner decision should remain modular and explicitly labeled rather than silently treated as canon.
