> Status: **PROPOSED / OWNER REVIEW REQUIRED**

> Purpose: Provide a fast, decision-first review path without requiring a linear read of the full design package.

---

## 1. What this packet is for

The overnight package is intentionally broad. This packet separates decisions that must be made now from details that can remain provisional.

The owner does **not** need to approve every skill name, item name, stat value, boss title, or faction name before the package becomes useful. The immediate goal is to approve or revise the structural identity of the world and classes.

Use three responses for each gate:

- **APPROVE** — direction is accepted and can be normalized into canonical documentation.
- **REVISE** — core is promising, but the listed issue must change before canonization.
- **HOLD** — stop further development of this area until another decision is resolved.

---

## 2. Ten-minute review path

Review these in order:

1. Confirm the shared campaign premise.
2. Choose the playable class name: Voidbringer or Void Warlock.
3. Approve, revise, or hold each new class core.
4. Choose the equipment-slot philosophy.
5. Choose the respec philosophy.
6. Confirm whether the proposed six-act campaign is the correct scale.
7. Identify any class that feels weaker, less original, or less exciting than the rest.

Everything else can remain proposed while implementation continues on already approved prototype work.

## 3. World and campaign approval card

### Proposed core

The world is not being invaded by the Abyss. Reality is collapsing into an ancient wound that previous civilizations concealed through the **World-Lattice**, a continent-scale network of seals, sacrificed lineages, drowned engines, dream laws, relic intelligences, altered bodies, pressure anchors, and temporal restraints.

The eight launch classes descend from or embody incompatible anchoring principles used to maintain that system. The player is valuable not because they are chosen, but because they are already damaged enough to survive contact with the failing machinery.

### Proposed campaign shape

- **Act 01:** Sunken Crypts and the Hollow King
- **Act 02:** Red Basilica
- **Act 03:** Country Behind Sleep
- **Act 04:** Reliquary March
- **Act 05:** Inverted Sea
- **Act 06:** Hour Without End

Every class travels through the same campaign. Personal arcs alter dialogue, optional encounters, class revelations, and certain quest outcomes without creating eight separate campaigns.

# 5. Class core approval cards

## 5.1 Graftborn

### Core fantasy

A self-engineering combat organism that deliberately authors its anatomy. It does not randomly mutate and is not a werewolf, plague caster, or detached-limb summoner.

### Core loop

1. Generate Biomass through aggression, damage intake, and anatomical actions.
2. Configure or trigger Body Region adaptations.
3. Apply Rupture and record Trauma Imprints.
4. Rebuild the body around the current threat.
5. Spend anatomy, health, or stored trauma for brutal payoffs.

### Owns

- Authored anatomy
- Body Regions
- Major Adaptations
- Trauma memory
- Structural flesh damage

### Primary risk

Animation and silhouette combinations could become too expensive or unreadable.

## 5.2 Somnarch

### Core fantasy

A waking sovereign who overlays hostile dream architecture onto real space. The Somnarch does not merely cast sleep spells; it makes the battlefield obey a private nightmare kingdom.

### Core loop

1. Establish Sovereign Dream territory.
2. Place Dream Clauses that define local rules.
3. Build Dread through positioning, witnessed actions, and controlled violations.
4. Shift between Waking, Veiled, and Dominion states.
5. Collapse the constructed nightmare for payoff or reposition it.

### Owns

- Dream territory
- Clause-based battlefield law
- Nightmare architecture
- Dread thresholds
- Waking and dream-state transitions

### Primary risk

Territory and clause visuals could overwhelm the screen or create excessive controller complexity.

## 5.3 Relic Host

### Core fantasy

A living reliquary carrying dead intelligences that can be negotiated with, rotated, suppressed, or forcibly invested. The player remains in control; possession creates decisions rather than taking agency away.

### Core loop

1. Configure a Reliquary Council before combat.
2. Fulfill a Presence's Memory Conditions.
3. Build Accord or deliberately create Conflict.
4. Invest a Presence to alter skills and combat posture.
5. Trigger Last Testaments, rotate office, or risk Ejection.

### Owns

- Negotiated possession
- Inherited dead intelligences
- Presence rotation
- Memory Conditions
- Last Testaments

### Primary risk

Too many Presence states could feel like menu management or passive aura stacking.

**REV-CLS-RLH:**

- APPROVE CORE
- REVISE CORE: ____________________
- HOLD

## 5.4 Gorgon

### Core fantasy

A metamorphic hunter-controller who weaponizes attention, fixation, venom, reflection, and structural petrification. It is not simply a poison rogue or Medusa recreation.

### Core loop

1. Designate Exposed prey.
2. Build Fixation through gaze, counters, movement reads, and venom interactions.
3. Push enemies through Lock States.
4. Petrify normal enemies or build Monument Pressure against bosses.
5. Break fixed structures and prey during deliberate execution windows.

### Owns

- Attention as a combat resource
- Fixation thresholds
- Deliberate petrification
- Reflection and counterplay
- Venom families without disease ecology

### Primary risk

Target-attention mechanics must remain readable in dense packs and useful against bosses.

**REV-CLS-GOR:**

- APPROVE CORE
- REVISE CORE: ____________________
- HOLD

## 5.5 Tidewrought

### Core fantasy

A pressure-forged amphibious executioner carrying an impossible sea inside the body. It controls pressure, brine, current, depth, and hull strain without becoming a water wizard, frost caster, or pirate.

### Core loop

1. Build Depth to enter stronger pressure bands.
2. Shape Current Lines and apply Soaked or Compression.
3. Use forced movement and heavy attacks to create Breach windows.
4. Manage Hull Strain to avoid Forced Surface.
5. Release stored pressure in short, violent execution sequences.

### Owns

- Internal ocean pressure
- Depth bands
- Hull Strain
- Current routing
- Compression and Breach

### Primary risk

Forced movement must not destroy encounter telegraphs or overlap Voidbringer's gravity identity.

**REV-CLS-TID:**

- APPROVE CORE
- REVISE CORE: ____________________

## 5.6 Anachron

### Core fantasy

A condemned temporal combatant who authors short sequences and forces enemies to suffer consequences from paths they never took. It never performs unrestricted world rewind or copies enemy abilities.

### Core loop

1. Record curated own-action Temporal Imprints.
2. Anchor position or state before committing.
3. Apply Delayed Consequences and Severance.
4. Reprise an authored sequence under controlled rules.
5. Collapse multiple possible outcomes through Convergence.

### Owns

- Own-action temporal replay
- Curated sequence authorship
- Delayed consequences
- Positional anchoring
- Convergence of incompatible outcomes

### Primary risk

Replay can duplicate invalid procs, damage, movement, or world state unless payload capture is tightly curated.

## 9. Recommended next production step

After owner review, convert decisions into a canonical ledger and normalize approved terminology. Then select exactly one new class for a narrow vertical slice.

Recommended first slice remains **Graftborn**, because it tests several foundational systems without requiring the extreme replay complexity of Anachron or the persistent territory complexity of Somnarch.

Minimum Graftborn slice:

- Biomass
- Arms and Torso Body Regions
- two Major Adaptations per region
- Rupture application and consumption
- one Trauma Imprint
- one cross-path interaction
- one build-changing unique effect

This slice should prove that the class is fun and readable before the project commits to the complete animation and anatomy matrix.

## 10. Canonization rule

No proposed document should be renamed, stripped of its status banner, or treated as implementation authority until its corresponding owner gate is recorded as approved.

When approved:

1. Record the decision ID and date.
2. Identify every affected document.
3. Normalize stable IDs and naming.
4. Separate launch requirements from post-launch expansion ideas.
5. Create implementation issues only for approved slices.
6. Preserve rejected concepts in an archive rather than silently deleting design history.
