# Graftborn Class Bible

> Status: **PROPOSED — owner review required.**
>
> Stable class ID: `CLS-GRA`
>
> This document defines the Graftborn as a launch class inside the shared AbyssFall campaign. It preserves the locked persistent-ARPG direction and treats all unapproved mechanics, names, values, and lore as proposals.

---

## 1. Class thesis

The Graftborn is not a shapeshifter, plague caster, or berserker with extra flesh effects.

The Graftborn is a **self-engineering combat organism** that survives by deciding what its body is allowed to become.

Its fantasy is controlled anatomical violence: growing the right weapon, moving organs out of danger, converting injuries into specialized tissue, and building a combat form through deliberate sequencing. The body is not a costume and mutation is not random. Every adaptation is authored, paid for, and physically legible.

### Player promise

The Graftborn lets the player say:

> “I built this body for this fight, and the enemy is feeding the design.”

### Core identity pillars

1. **Authored mutation** — the player chooses adaptations; the class never becomes random mutation soup.
2. **Damage becomes material** — receiving or inflicting specific damage types can produce tissue used to reconfigure the build.
3. **Anatomical commitment** — powerful forms occupy real body regions and create tradeoffs.
4. **Violent utility** — movement, defense, control, and offense all emerge from physical restructuring.
5. **Persistent buildcraft** — class trees, gear, unique items, saved loadouts, and skill synergies define the long-term character.

### Explicit anti-pillars

The Graftborn must not become:

- a generic werewolf transformation class;
- a poison-only rogue;
- a pet class made of detached body parts;
- a random-proc mutation gimmick;
- a conventional life-steal tank with gore visuals;
- a “press ultimate to become big” class whose normal kit feels unfinished;
- a body-horror joke class.

## 2. Lore and campaign role

### Origin

**PROPOSED:** Graftborn descend from emergency repair lineages created during the Binding Epoch. Their ancestors were not soldiers. They were living maintenance organisms designed to enter damaged Lattice structures, survive impossible conditions, and replace broken biological components with their own tissue.

Over centuries, kingdoms mistook this inheritance for disease. Fleshworkers learned to awaken fragments of it through surgery, transfusion, starvation, battlefield grafting, and ritual cultivation. Most subjects became unstable. A true Graftborn is different: their body contains a dormant **Morphogenic Archive**, an inherited system that can classify damage and produce a deliberate answer.

The class begins after surviving an execution, vivisection, battlefield harvest, or failed cure. The exact origin can remain selectable background flavor rather than separate campaigns.

### Shared campaign integration

The Graftborn follows the same six-act campaign as every launch class.

#### ACT-01 — Recognition

The Hollow Crown map reacts to structures hidden in the Graftborn’s tissue. The class sees the map not as symbols, but as a wound pattern distributed across the world.

#### ACT-02 — The Red Basilica

The Covenant of Measure declares the class a blasphemous body without lawful boundaries. Beneath the Basilica, the Graftborn discovers that outlawed fleshcraft began as emergency medicine for repairing Lattice nodes.

Personal mission proposal: recover the **First Sutures**, records proving that sanctioned religious anatomy was derived from Graftborn ancestors.

#### ACT-03 — The Country Behind Sleep

Dream-forms attempt to assign the Graftborn an “original body.” The class confronts versions of itself based on other people’s expectations: weapon, patient, monster, child, corpse.

#### ACT-04 — The Reliquary March

Relics attempt to colonize unused organs and claim the Graftborn as ideal host material. This act establishes the hard boundary between Graftborn self-authorship and Relic Host coexistence.

#### ACT-05 — The Inverted Sea

Extreme pressure reveals deep-sea repair structures in the Morphogenic Archive. The player learns that Graftborn tissue was once used to patch non-human Lattice organisms beneath the sea.

#### ACT-06 — Definition

The Eightfold Custodian identifies the Graftborn as replacement material rather than a person. The class arc resolves by rejecting both restoration to an imagined original form and surrender to unlimited adaptation.

The defining statement is:

> “There was never a pure body to return to. This one is mine because I choose what it becomes.”

### Personal-arc temptation

The Unfinished offers access to unrestricted evolution: no limits, no stable identity, no distinction between survival and appetite.

The temptation is mechanically reflected through late-game keystones that increase adaptation freedom while escalating physiological cost.

## 3. Visual language and silhouette

### Default silhouette

The Graftborn should read as dangerous before any skill is used, but remain recognizably humanoid.

- asymmetrical shoulders;
- one visibly cultivated weapon limb or reinforced forearm;
- segmented scar channels showing where tissue can unfold;
- rib, spine, or hip structures that open only during specific actions;
- layered cloth, restraints, braces, and medical hardware used to control growth;
- no constant tentacle halo;
- no permanent giant monster posture.

### Transformation rules

1. Every adaptation has a consistent body origin.
2. The same body region cannot visually host two incompatible major adaptations at once unless a build specifically unlocks that exception.
3. Mutation silhouettes should communicate function before particles do.
4. Tissue retracts, scars, sheds, hardens, ruptures, or is reabsorbed; it does not disappear cleanly.
5. Gore is impact language, not comedy or visual clutter.

### Material palette

- dark wet muscle;
- pale scar tissue;
- blackened cartilage;
- marrow-white weapon structures;
- coagulated crimson;
- surgical metal and restraint leather;
- small bioluminescent or acidic accents only where a build requires them.

### Readability colors

Proposed combat-readability accents:

- **Red:** fresh tissue, blood generation, aggressive growth.
- **Bone white:** hardened armor and weapon structures.
- **Amber:** stored adaptation material.
- **Sickly green:** corrosive or toxic specialization.
- **Violet-black:** Lattice contamination and forbidden overgrowth.

These are readability conventions, not fixed costume colors.

## 4. Combat role

### Primary role

Adaptive melee-midrange bruiser with strong build-dependent specialization.

### Secondary roles

Depending on build, the Graftborn can become:

- sustained close-range executioner;
- reactive counter-tank;
- mobile rupture skirmisher;
- corrosive control fighter;
- self-damaging burst predator;
- hybrid weapon-and-organ artillery platform.

### Baseline strengths

- high sustain through active tissue management;
- strong answers to repeated damage patterns;
- excellent close-range pressure;
- unusually flexible damage conversion;
- strong stagger and body-control options;
- can trade health, armor, reach, speed, and resource flow in deliberate ways;
- deep synergy between skills and equipment.

### Baseline weaknesses

- adaptations require setup and commitment;
- poor decisions can lock body regions into the wrong configuration;
- mobility is powerful but less effortless than a pure agile class;
- excessive self-damage can create sudden failure states;
- enemies with mixed damage profiles are harder to adapt against;
- body-state readability must be learned;
- strongest builds often sacrifice one defensive layer to amplify another.

### Intended mastery curve

A new player understands:

- attack enemies;
- collect Biomass;
- spend Biomass to grow useful adaptations;
- use Moulting to reset or reposition the body state.

An advanced player masters:

- predicting incoming damage;
- selecting which body regions to commit;
- sequencing rupture, growth, and reabsorption;
- converting self-inflicted damage into offense;
- maintaining the correct tissue tags for gear and keystone interactions;
- switching between stable and overgrown states without losing tempo.

- high Vitality weighting;

- natural resistance to Bleed and physical degeneration;
- no blanket immunity to poison, disease, or corruption.

## 6. Unique resource: Biomass

Stable resource ID: `RES-GRA-BIOMASS`

### Definition

Biomass represents usable tissue, marrow, blood, mineralized growth, and captured biological patterning available for immediate reconfiguration.

### Generation

Baseline sources:

- dealing direct damage;
- rupturing marked enemies;
- taking a meaningful hit;
- consuming corpses or eligible remains;
- reabsorbing an adaptation;
- triggering certain organ skills.

Biomass generation should use per-source limits to avoid degenerate multi-hit scaling.

### Spending

Biomass is spent to:

- grow active adaptations;
- reinforce existing adaptations;
- regenerate damaged body zones;
- empower organ skills;
- perform emergency moults;
- trigger class-specific finishers.

### Capacity

Biomass has a visible maximum capacity. Capacity can be modified through class tree, gear, and unique items.

A full Biomass bar should create pressure to spend, but should not automatically trigger effects unless a build opts into that behavior.

### Resource feel

Biomass should feel abundant during committed combat and scarce during indecisive play. The class is not meant to wait passively for regeneration.

## 7. Signature mechanic: Body Regions and Adaptations

Stable mechanic ID: `MEC-GRA-ANATOMY`

The Graftborn has four major body regions:

1. **Arms** — attacks, reach, grappling, weapon formation.
2. **Torso** — defense, organ projection, resource storage, counterattacks.
3. **Legs** — movement, stance, impact, pursuit.
4. **Cranial/Spinal** — perception, targeting, command, dangerous overgrowth.

### Adaptation slots

Each region supports one **Major Adaptation** by default. Minor traits can stack beneath it through passives and gear.

Growing a Major Adaptation:

- costs Biomass;
- has a short, interrupt-resistant growth action;
- changes one or more skills;
- adds one benefit and usually one explicit cost;
- remains active until reabsorbed, replaced, destroyed, or removed through Moulting.

### Adaptation categories

- **Predatory:** damage, reach, pursuit, execution.
- **Bulwark:** armor, stability, retaliation, damage redirection.
- **Parasitic:** drain, mark, attach, consume, debuff.
- **Catalytic:** corrosion, heat, volatile tissue, self-damage conversion.
- **Mnemonic:** copied damage patterns, enemy traits, Lattice repair functions.

### Commitment rule

The strongest effects require two or more compatible adaptations. This creates build identity through body architecture rather than simply equipping the highest numbers.

### Damage memory

A proposed secondary layer called **Trauma Imprints** records the last significant damage families received:

- physical;
- elemental;
- toxic/corrosive;
- occult/void;
- temporal or dream where supported.

Certain adaptations can consume an imprint to produce a tailored response. The number of tracked imprints must remain small and readable.

## 8. Secondary mechanic: Rupture

Stable status ID: `STS-GRA-RUPTURE`

Rupture is a class-applied anatomical instability placed on enemies through specific strikes, hooks, bone weapons, and organ projectiles.

### Baseline behavior

- Rupture stacks to a modest cap.
- Heavy Graftborn attacks consume stacks for bonus physical damage, stagger, Biomass generation, or build-specific effects.
- Rupture is not just Bleed under another name.
- Rupture represents structural failure: opened armor seams, displaced joints, torn magical anatomy, cracked shells, or exposed anchor points.

### Boss compatibility

Bosses cannot be trivially dismembered, but Rupture can:

- increase stagger damage;
- expose temporary weak points;
- improve Biomass return;
- enable class finishers during scripted vulnerability windows.

## 9. Weapons and equipment identity

### Supported weapon families

The Graftborn can use conventional weapons, but grows around them.

Primary proposed families:

- cleavers and heavy blades;
- axes;
- polearms;
- hooked chains;
- fist weapons and graft-braces;
- one-handed weapon plus anatomical off-hand;
- two-handed weapons integrated into arm growths.

### Anatomical off-hand

A class-specific gear category may occupy the off-hand or relic slot depending on final equipment architecture.

Possible category names:

- Graft Core;
- Cultivation Vat;
- Suture Frame;
- Organ Reliquary;
- Marrow Loom.

This item modifies Body Region behavior, adaptation tags, Biomass economy, and transformation limits.

### Weapon integration rule

The Graftborn should not discard loot fantasy by replacing every weapon with flesh. Instead:

- bone growths extend weapon reach;
- muscles alter attack cadence;
- tendons redirect swings;
- organs add secondary projectiles;
- growths sheath, brace, duplicate, anchor, or consume weapon properties.

## 10. Movement identity

The Graftborn moves through forced anatomical solutions rather than teleportation or graceful acrobatics.

### Baseline dodge

**Sinew Lurch** — a forceful lateral or forward displacement using stretched tendons. It is quick, low to the ground, and visibly strains the legs.

### Movement skill families

- tendon launches;
- bone-spur vaults;
- hooked pulls toward enemies;
- wall or terrain anchoring where technically feasible;
- short predatory bursts after Rupture consumption;
- emergency body collapse followed by reformation.

### Movement restrictions

- no free sustained flight;
- no repeated screen-wide teleports;
- no elegant rogue-style infinite dash chain;
- mobility should reflect current leg adaptation.

## 11. Baseline active skill kit

The final game can support many skills. This section defines the launch-facing mechanical vocabulary.

### `SKL-GRA-001` — Marrow Cleave

A broad weapon or forearm strike that creates a temporary bone edge.

- reliable generator;
- applies Rupture;
- gains reach from Predatory arm adaptations;
- gains armor damage from Bulwark arm adaptations;
- can become a double-ended sweep with a specific keystone.

### `SKL-GRA-002` — Suture Hook

Launch a tendon-and-bone hook.

- pulls light enemies;
- pulls the Graftborn toward heavy enemies or bosses;
- can attach a Parasitic organ;
- consumes Rupture for a violent collision or joint break;
- can be modified into terrain anchoring if implementation proves reliable.

### `SKL-GRA-003` — Ossuary Guard

Grow a temporary rib or plate structure over the torso.

- converts a portion of incoming damage into delayed Trauma;
- grants stagger resistance;
- can release stored force as a counterattack;
- can become directional rather than omnidirectional for higher skill expression.

### `SKL-GRA-004` — Organ Lance

Open the torso and fire a compressed biological projectile.

- consumes Biomass;
- damage type follows equipped organ adaptation;
- can fire bile, calcified spikes, pressurized blood, or captured elemental material;
- should remain brutal and physical, not read as generic spellcasting.

### `SKL-GRA-005` — Harvest the Weak

Execute or maul a low-health, staggered, or heavily Ruptured enemy.

- large Biomass return;
- brief displacement resistance;
- boss version becomes a vulnerability-window strike rather than instant execution;
- build paths can trade resource return for explosions, graft copying, or defensive growth.

### `SKL-GRA-006` — Moulting Step

Shed damaged external tissue and relocate.

- movement plus partial adaptation reset;
- removes selected negative statuses;
- leaves behind a false body or hazardous remains with investment;
- costs either Biomass or current adaptation durability.

### `SKL-GRA-007` — Borrowed Reflex

Consume a Trauma Imprint to gain a temporary response based on the damage family received.

Examples:

- physical: stability and counterforce;
- fire: heat venting and burning retaliation;
- cold: rigid armor and slowing contact;
- lightning: nerve acceleration;
- toxic: filtration and corrosive weapon coating;
- void: dangerous resistance conversion with Corruption risk.

### `SKL-GRA-008` — Colony Heart

Plant or extrude a temporary organ that pulses within a limited area.

- shares Biomass generation with the Graftborn;
- can heal, corrode, reinforce, or mark depending on build;
- remains a deployed organ, not a permanent pet;
- enemies can destroy it unless a build internalizes it.

### `SKL-GRA-009` — Splinter Rush

Reconfigure the legs and charge through enemies.

- applies Rupture along the path;
- can pin enemies to terrain;
- can become a series of short bursts rather than one charge;
- body-region commitment changes handling and recovery.

### `SKL-GRA-010` — Reabsorb

Collapse one active Major Adaptation.

- refunds part of its Biomass cost;
- triggers adaptation-specific exit effects;
- allows advanced players to cycle body architecture mid-fight;
- can be simplified to hold-input interaction on controller.

### `SKL-GRA-011` — Redundant Organ

Grow a temporary duplicate organ.

- can store a lethal-hit prevention charge, extra resource, status immunity, or skill echo depending on specialization;
- always has a visible body tell;
- cannot provide every benefit at once.

### `SKL-GRA-012` — Open the Archive

Temporarily exposes dormant Binding-Epoch repair patterns.

- reduces adaptation growth time;
- allows one normally incompatible combination;
- increases Biomass generation and physiological strain;
- functions as a major cooldown, not the class’s only exciting state.

## 12. Progression architecture

The Graftborn class tree is divided into four main paths with cross-links and shared central anatomy nodes.

### Path A — Butchered Engine

Stable path ID: `PATH-GRA-BUTCHER`

Focus:

- weapon integration;
- Rupture consumption;
- execution;
- heavy physical damage;
- attack sequencing;
- aggressive Biomass generation.

Signature node themes:

- bone edges inherit weapon affixes;
- overkill creates usable tissue;
- consecutive attacks reshape reach and angle;
- Rupture consumption changes finishers;
- weapon speed can be traded for tissue mass.

### Path B — Bastion Organism

Stable path ID: `PATH-GRA-BASTION`

Focus:

- layered armor growth;
- delayed damage;
- retaliation;
- organ redundancy;
- stability;
- body-zone repair.

Signature node themes:

- torso adaptations absorb specific damage families;
- blocked or delayed damage becomes Biomass;
- damaged armor plates can be fired outward;
- healing can be converted into structural reinforcement;
- low-life states enable emergency anatomy rather than generic damage bonuses.

### Path C — Carrion Architect

Stable path ID: `PATH-GRA-CARRION`

Focus:

- deployed organs;
- corpse use;
- corrosion;
- parasitic attachments;
- area control;
- resource ecosystems.

Signature node themes:

- Colony Heart variants;
- enemy remains become temporary tissue fields;
- parasites spread Rupture or siphon resistances;
- consumed enemies imprint traits onto organ skills;
- detached tissue remains temporary and tactical, never a permanent minion army.

### Path D — Archive Aberrant

Stable path ID: `PATH-GRA-ARCHIVE`

Focus:

- Trauma Imprints;
- damage conversion;
- incompatible adaptation combinations;
- self-damage;
- Lattice repair patterns;
- dangerous build experimentation.

Signature node themes:

- retain multiple damage memories;
- convert received damage into tailored offense;
- unlock cross-category anatomy;
- temporarily copy enemy defenses or attack properties;
- overgrowth increases output while destabilizing resource and health management.

## 13. Cross-path synergies

### Butcher + Bastion

**Siege Carcass**

A slow, heavily reinforced weapon platform that stores incoming force and releases it through massive Rupture-consuming swings.

### Butcher + Carrion

**Harvest Chain**

Executions seed organ fields, organ fields soften enemies, and softened enemies create faster executions.

### Butcher + Archive

**Counter-Evolution Predator**

Taking a damage family changes the next weapon sequence to exploit the attacker’s biological or structural weakness.

### Bastion + Carrion

**Living Redoubt**

Deployed organs reinforce the player’s territory, redirect damage, and convert corpses into armor replacement.

### Bastion + Archive

**Adaptive Bulwark**

Trauma Imprints specialize defensive growth against the most recent threat but create vulnerability to neglected damage families.

### Carrion + Archive

**Unlicensed Ecology**

Organs consume enemy traits, mutate their function, and create temporary biological rules inside their area.

## 14. Proposed keystones

Keystones are build-defining commitments, not mandatory upgrades.

### `KEY-GRA-001` — No Original Shape

- Major Adaptations no longer have category compatibility restrictions.
- Each incompatible pairing increases Biomass costs and self-damage.
- Visual overgrowth escalates clearly.

### `KEY-GRA-002` — The Wound Learns

- The largest damage family recently received grants a powerful tailored adaptation bonus.
- All other damage families penetrate more defense.
- Encourages encounter knowledge and deliberate risk.

### `KEY-GRA-003` — All Meat Is Instruction

- Eligible enemy remains can be consumed to temporarily copy one trait.
- Copied traits replace one current Trauma Imprint.
- Boss traits use curated versions to avoid uncontrolled power theft.

### `KEY-GRA-004` — Closed Circulation

- Life recovery beyond maximum Life becomes Biomass and adaptation reinforcement.
- External healing is reduced.
- Creates a self-sustaining bruiser without infinite party-healing abuse.

### `KEY-GRA-005` — Beautiful Failure

- Destroyed Major Adaptations trigger powerful rupture effects.
- Reabsorbing them manually grants little or no refund.
- Encourages intentional breakage and replacement.

### `KEY-GRA-006` — One Body, One Purpose

- Only one Major Adaptation may remain active.
- Its effects, scaling, visual size, and skill conversions are dramatically increased.
- Supports a readable low-complexity/high-commitment build.

### `KEY-GRA-007` — Distributed Heart

- Colony Heart becomes the primary life core while active.
- Damage can be redirected between player and organ.
- The organ’s destruction creates a severe vulnerability window.

### `KEY-GRA-008` — Feast Without End

- Biomass capacity is removed and replaced by escalating Overgrowth.
- Spending slows Overgrowth; failing to spend causes self-damage and forced tissue eruption.
- High-action build for expert players.

## 15. Ultimate

### `ULT-GRA-001` — Become the Answer

The Graftborn tears open the Morphogenic Archive and rapidly assembles a temporary combat anatomy based on the current build.

The ultimate must not be one fixed giant-monster form. It synthesizes the player’s equipped adaptations, path investment, weapon family, and recent Trauma Imprints.

### Baseline effects

- immediately grows or reinforces all selected Major Adaptations;
- temporarily removes growth-time penalties;
- grants aggressive Biomass generation;
- causes key skills to use their fully evolved variants;
- enables one cross-region synergy unique to the current loadout;
- ends with a forced Moulting event that must be managed.

### Build examples

- Butcher build becomes a many-jointed execution engine with extreme reach and chained finishers.
- Bastion build becomes a walking layered fortress that ejects destroyed armor as counter-projectiles.
- Carrion build turns its deployed organs into a connected circulatory territory.
- Archive build cycles tailored responses to incoming damage but risks lethal collapse.

### Ultimate design rule

The ultimate amplifies the build the player authored. It does not replace it with a separate minigame.

## 16. Endgame build archetypes

### Build 1 — Marrow Guillotine

Primary paths: Butchered Engine + Archive Aberrant

Core loop:

1. apply Rupture with fast bone-edged sequences;
2. take or deliberately trigger controlled physical Trauma;
3. convert the imprint into reach and counterforce;
4. consume Rupture with a massive execution strike;
5. reabsorb the weapon arm to trigger an exit burst;
6. regrow a different arm profile for the next pack or boss phase.

Identity: high-skill melee executioner with changing attack geometry.

### Build 2 — Cathedral of Meat

Primary paths: Bastion Organism + Carrion Architect

Core loop:

1. plant Colony Hearts and defensive organs;
2. redirect pressure into deployed anatomy;
3. create tissue fields from fallen enemies;
4. replace shattered armor using the field;
5. punish enemies for entering the organ network;
6. relocate the territory using Moulting Step and organ reabsorption.

Identity: territorial tank-controller without permanent pets.

### Build 3 — Counterfeit Apex

Primary paths: Archive Aberrant + Butchered Engine

Core loop:

1. deliberately expose the body to an enemy’s signature damage;
2. store the Trauma Imprint;
3. grow a tailored response;
4. copy a curated trait from an elite or boss add;
5. convert that trait into weapon or Organ Lance behavior;
6. abandon it before the encounter changes damage profile.

Identity: encounter-knowledge build that turns enemy identity into temporary anatomy.

### Build 4 — Rupture Migration

Primary paths: Butchered Engine + Carrion Architect

Core loop:

1. spread Rupture through hooks and tissue fields;
2. dash between marked enemies using modified Splinter Rush;
3. consume stacks in a chain of collisions;
4. leave parasitic organs behind each kill;
5. collapse the entire chain for a pack-wide structural failure.

Identity: aggressive pack-clearing skirmisher with brutal path planning.

### Build 5 — Last Organ Standing

Primary paths: Bastion Organism + Archive Aberrant

Core loop:

1. maintain Redundant Organ charges;
2. delay damage through Ossuary Guard;
3. convert near-lethal events into specialized anatomy;
4. cycle body regions as each one fails;
5. finish encounters in a dangerous low-life overgrown state.

Identity: reactive survival build where defense is an authored sequence, not passive invulnerability.

All names and slots are proposed.

### `UNI-GRA-001` — Motherless Pattern

Slot: Graft Core / class relic

Effect concept:

- removes the default visual and mechanical template for Major Adaptations;
- the first adaptation grown each combat becomes the body’s Prime Pattern;
- later adaptations inherit one property from it;
- replacing the Prime Pattern violently destroys all dependent growths.

Build impact: turns adaptation order into the central combat puzzle.

### `UNI-GRA-002` — The Mercy Cage

Slot: chest armor

Effect concept:

- Ossuary Guard traps a portion of damage that would hit nearby allies or deployed organs;
- stored damage becomes a visible cage around the torso;
- breaking or releasing the cage deals scaled counter-damage and heavily Ruptures the player.

Build impact: enables an active protector identity without mandatory co-op dependence; solo use protects Colony Hearts and converts environmental pressure.

### `UNI-GRA-003` — Knife That Remembers Every Hand

Slot: one-handed blade

Effect concept:

- each Major Adaptation changes the weapon’s moveset property rather than only its damage;
- arm growth changes reach;
- leg growth changes recovery movement;
- torso growth adds stored-force release;
- cranial growth adds targeted weak-point behavior;
- reabsorbing a region permanently changes the next attack in the sequence.

Build impact: creates a weapon-combo architecture driven by anatomy cycling.

### `UNI-GRA-004` — Saint’s Rejected Liver

Slot: anatomical off-hand

Effect concept:

- toxic and occult damage are filtered into separate Biomass pools;
- spending one pool empowers the other;
- reaching maximum in either causes organ revolt and a dangerous area effect;
- can support corrosion, void resistance conversion, or alternating-resource builds.

Build impact: turns damage intake and dual-resource management into offense.

### `UNI-GRA-005` — The Skin That Stayed Behind

Slot: boots

Effect concept:

- Moulting Step leaves behind a full false body retaining the shed adaptations;
- the husk repeats one tagged skill or draws enemy attention;
- reabsorbing the husk returns Biomass and merges any damage it suffered into a Trauma Imprint;
- only one husk can exist by default.

Build impact: enables positional feints, delayed echoes, and defensive damage sampling without becoming a clone-minion build.

### `UNI-GRA-006` — Crown of Unfinished Thoughts

Slot: head armor

Effect concept:

- cranial adaptation can host two Minor Adaptations;
- skill targeting and weak-point detection improve;
- taking dream or temporal damage can scramble which minor trait is active unless stabilized through play;
- Open the Archive fixes both traits temporarily.

Build impact: high-information precision build with controlled instability.

### `UNI-GRA-007` — Marrow Tithe

Slot: two-handed axe or cleaver

Effect concept:

- Biomass spending adds permanent mass to the weapon during combat;
- mass increases damage, reach, and recovery time;
- executions shed accumulated mass as bone shrapnel and reset the weapon;
- bosses allow controlled partial shedding through Rupture windows.

Build impact: creates a visible tempo cycle from fast light weapon to monstrous execution tool.

### `UNI-GRA-008` — Heart of the Empty Ward

Slot: amulet

Effect concept:

- Colony Heart is internalized and cannot be deployed;
- its area effects follow the player at reduced radius;
- each active Major Adaptation changes the pulse function;
- losing a Major Adaptation briefly stops the pulse.

Build impact: converts territorial organ builds into mobile aura-like bruisers while preserving body-state interaction.

## 18. Gear interaction rules

### Affix families

Proposed Graftborn-relevant affixes:

- Biomass capacity;
- Biomass gained from Rupture consumption;
- adaptation growth speed;
- adaptation durability;
- region-specific skill levels;
- physical damage converted through Organ skills;
- Trauma Imprint duration;
- self-damage mitigation that does not erase cost;
- reabsorption refund;
- deployed organ durability and pulse rate;
- Rupture application and consumption effect;
- Major Adaptation tag bonuses.

### Conversion rules

- Conversion chains must have a clear maximum depth.
- A single damage event should not generate Biomass repeatedly through circular conversion.
- Self-damage must be tagged so it cannot trigger every “when hit” effect without limits.
- Corpse and remains interactions need boss-safe fallback behavior.
- Adaptation effects should snapshot only where necessary; dynamic scaling is preferred when readable and performant.

### Saved builds

Saved build slots should preserve:

- skill loadout;
- class-tree allocation where respec rules permit;
- chosen Major Adaptations;
- controller bindings;
- gear set references;
- cosmetic anatomy preferences that do not alter combat readability.

---

## 19. Co-op identity

The Graftborn contributes through physical disruption, territory, and adaptive protection without requiring a party.

### Party value

- exposes Rupture weak points;
- pins or relocates enemies;
- creates organ fields with optional ally benefits;
- absorbs or delays selected pressure;
- adapts to neglected encounter threats;
- executes priority targets during vulnerability windows.

### Solo parity

Every support-facing effect must have a solo target:

- ally protection also protects deployed organs or the player’s own delayed-damage structures;
- shared healing also reinforces adaptations;
- exposed weak points improve the Graftborn’s own finishers;
- territorial effects remain useful for enemy control.

### Co-op restrictions

- no permanent ally body modification without explicit consent;
- no visual grafts attached to another player by default;
- no forced health sharing;
- no ability that requires an ally to make the build function.

## 20. Accessibility and readability

### Required options

- reduce gore density while preserving mechanical hit confirms;
- replace exposed-organ detail with armored or stylized tissue variants;
- high-contrast outlines for active Body Regions;
- icon-based adaptation state display;
- separate indicators for Biomass, Trauma Imprints, and adaptation durability;
- controller hold/tap customization for Reabsorb and adaptation replacement;
- screen-shake scaling;
- reduced camera movement during executions;
- optional simplified anatomy HUD showing four body-region slots.

### Cognitive load controls

A beginner preset can:

- recommend one adaptation per region;
- auto-select a compatible adaptation when using a growth skill;
- limit active Trauma Imprints to one;
- display explicit “grow / fight / reabsorb” prompts during early levels.

These assists must not alter underlying balance in competitive or leaderboard contexts unless the mode explicitly permits it.

### Enemy readability

The Graftborn’s gore and tissue effects must not hide:

- enemy telegraphs;
- ground hazards;
- boss weak points;
- ally effects;
- interactable objects.

## 21. Technical implementation risks

### Risk 1 — Visual combinatorics

Four regions with many adaptation combinations can create unsustainable model and animation complexity.

Mitigation:

- modular sockets with strict silhouette budgets;
- shared rig-compatible growth families;
- limited Major Adaptation visual archetypes with material and geometry variants;
- animation overrides only for build-defining changes;
- early prototype with placeholder region markers before high-detail assets.

### Risk 2 — Animation compatibility

Weapon families plus arm transformations can break attack readability and collision alignment.

Mitigation:

- define a small set of attack geometry profiles;
- map adaptations to profiles rather than unique animation sets for every combination;
- decouple damage arcs from decorative geometry while keeping them visibly aligned;
- automated tests for reach, arc, and root motion where possible.

### Risk 3 — Proc loops

Self-damage, delayed damage, healing conversion, Biomass gain, and adaptation destruction can produce circular triggers.

Mitigation:

- strict event tags;
- per-event and per-frame trigger limits;
- explicit source ownership;
- conversion-depth cap;
- deterministic combat logs.

### Risk 4 — Corpse dependence

Bosses and some enemies may not create usable remains.

Mitigation:

- Rupture and stagger produce synthetic tissue fragments;
- elite and boss encounters create curated harvest windows;
- corpse-consuming skills have non-corpse fallback effects.

### Risk 5 — Controller complexity

Managing four body regions could become a radial-menu burden.

Mitigation:

- skills grow predetermined region adaptations based on loadout;
- Reabsorb can target the most recently used or held-skill-linked region;
- optional radial menu exists for deliberate out-of-combat configuration, not constant combat interruption;
- adaptation replacement can be tied to skill variants and loadout slots.

### Risk 6 — Save compatibility

Persistent anatomy loadouts could break when skills or nodes change.

Mitigation:

- stable IDs for class, paths, skills, keystones, adaptations, and uniques;
- versioned migration tables;
- invalid loadouts fall back to refunded points and unequipped items rather than corrupted saves.

### Risk 7 — Excessive visual noise

Multiple organ fields, tissue husks, Rupture effects, and armor breakage could overwhelm packs.

Mitigation:

- per-player effect budgets;
- distance-based simplification;
- priority hierarchy for boss telegraphs;
- user-facing effect-density controls;
- hard cap on deployed major organs.

## 22. Non-negotiable design rules

1. Mutation is deliberate, not random.
2. The player remains recognizably the same character through adaptation.
3. The class’s normal combat loop must feel complete without its ultimate.
4. Body Regions create meaningful commitments rather than cosmetic toggles.
5. Biomass is generated through combat participation, not passive waiting.
6. Rupture is structural setup and consumption, not renamed Bleed.
7. No permanent minion army of detached body parts.
8. Conventional weapons remain meaningful loot.
9. Self-damage builds require visible cost and cannot become free proc engines.
10. The class cannot permanently alter allies without consent.
11. Strong defense must come from active anatomy management rather than passive immortality.
12. High-end mastery comes from sequencing, positioning, encounter knowledge, and build architecture.
13. The Graftborn’s story is about self-authorship, not curing ugliness or returning to purity.
14. Horror stays serious, physical, and purposeful.
15. All progression remains persistent and compatible with multiple saved builds.

## 23. Consistency check

### Shared campaign compatibility

- Uses the established shared six-act structure.
- Reinforces the Red Basilica and The Unfinished without creating a separate campaign.
- Connects the Binding Epoch flesh anchor to the World-Lattice premise.
- Preserves the Hollow King and Sunken Crypts as the opening anchor.
- Bridges into Fracture Hunts, Anchor Depths, and later optional Abyss Siege without requiring resets.

### Launch-class identity separation

- **Voidbringer:** manipulates gravity, portals, soul energy, and Corruption; Graftborn manipulates authored anatomy and stored tissue.
- **Penitent:** writes battlefield law through rites and geometry; Graftborn rewrites its own body through biological commitment.
- **Somnarch:** should control dream-state, perception, and sleeping sovereignty rather than physical mutation.
- **Relic Host:** negotiates with inherited dead and artifacts; Graftborn rejects external ownership of its body.
- **Gorgon:** should transform or fix external beings and matter through gaze/pattern mechanics; Graftborn primarily transforms itself.
- **Tidewrought:** should embody pressure, current, and abyssal sea identity; Graftborn may borrow aquatic repair traits but cannot own sea-control fantasy.
- **Anachron:** should manipulate sequence and chronology; Graftborn adapts in present combat and does not rewind mistakes.

### Reserved-class separation

- Plaguebringer remains available for true disease ecology, contagion, and epidemic control.
- Graftborn corrosion and parasites must stay secondary to anatomy and adaptation.
- Choirborn and Echo Thief mechanics are not used.

### Shared numerical framework compatibility

- Uses Life, Guard/Armor, damage families, statuses, persistent equipment, class-tree paths, keystones, and build slots.
- Adds Biomass, Body Regions, Trauma Imprints, and Rupture as class-specific systems.
- Requires explicit trigger tagging to stay compatible with shared proc and conversion rules.

## 24. Open owner decisions

These decisions are intentionally not locked:

1. Is the player-facing class name definitively **Graftborn**, or should that be the lineage while the combat class uses another title?
2. How visibly transformed should the default character appear outside combat?
3. Should character creation allow several origin presentations such as escaped patient, battlefield harvest, condemned fleshworker, or awakened lineage?
4. Does the Graftborn receive a dedicated Graft Core slot, share the proposed Class Relic slot, or use anatomical off-hands within normal equipment rules?
5. Should Body Regions be visible as four explicit HUD slots from level one, or introduced gradually?
6. How many Major Adaptations should exist at launch per region?
7. Should Trauma Imprints ship in the baseline class or unlock through the Archive Aberrant path to reduce early complexity?
8. How far can curated elite or boss trait copying go without diluting enemy identity?
9. Should executions be frequent pack-combat tools, rare elite finishers, or both through separate animations?
10. What level of gore reduction is required while preserving the class’s mechanical clarity and serious tone?
11. Is **Become the Answer** the right ultimate name and synthesis model?
12. Should the class have selectable body presentation options that preserve all gameplay silhouettes?
13. Which campaign NPC should embody the opposing belief that a body has one lawful original form?
14. Should the Graftborn’s late arc include a permanent cosmetic choice representing its self-authored body?
15. Which proposed unique items are strong enough to reserve for pinnacle/endgame rarity?

## 25. Recommended first playable slice

To validate identity before building the full class, prototype:

- Biomass resource;
- Arms and Torso as the first two Body Regions;
- two Major Adaptations per region;
- Marrow Cleave;
- Suture Hook;
- Ossuary Guard;
- Organ Lance;
- Reabsorb;
- Rupture application and consumption;
- one simple physical Trauma Imprint;
- one Butcher/Bastion cross-path synergy;
- one unique-item modifier using adaptation order.

The slice succeeds when:

- a new player understands how to grow and use an adaptation;
- an experienced player can deliberately cycle two body states for higher output;
- the class feels brutal without becoming visually unreadable;
- the same base skills support at least two clearly different playstyles;
- the Graftborn cannot be mistaken for the Penitent, Voidbringer, a werewolf, or a poison summoner.
