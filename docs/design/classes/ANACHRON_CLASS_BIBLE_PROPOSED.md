# Anachron Class Bible

> Status: **PROPOSED — owner review required.**
>
> This document extends the shared campaign and systems framework without changing gameplay code or locking unresolved narrative decisions as canon.

## 1. Class thesis

**The Anachron is a condemned temporal combatant who survives by carrying several mutually incompatible versions of the same moment and forcing enemies to suffer the consequences of paths they did not take.**

The class is not a generic time wizard, cooldown-reset machine, teleport spammer, clone summoner, or cosmetic clockwork caster. Its fantasy is violent chronology: attacks leave consequences before causes, wounds return at the worst possible instant, movement creates abandoned positions that can be weaponized, and defensive mastery comes from deciding which version of the self is allowed to become real.

A skilled Anachron follows a clear escalating loop:

1. **Imprint** actions, positions, wounds, or enemy states into the local sequence.
2. **Accumulate Disjunction** by maintaining incompatible outcomes without resolving them.
3. **Select or deny causality** through Reprise, Delay, Sever, and Convergence effects.
4. **Collapse the sequence** so stored consequences occur together, in reverse order, or on a chosen target.
5. **Stabilize** before unresolved contradictions consume resources, defenses, or control.

The first-minute understanding should be simple: mark what happened, move or strike again, then make the marked event happen a second time. Mastery comes from sequencing, choosing which state to preserve, planning safe return points, exploiting delayed consequences, and controlling when multiple timelines are forced into one outcome.

## 2. Locked identity boundaries

The Anachron must not become:

- a conventional robed chronomancer;
- a pure cooldown-reduction class;
- a permanent teleport-spam assassin;
- a clone or pet-management class;
- a random proc class whose effects feel disconnected from player intent;
- a save-scumming fantasy that erases every mistake for free;
- a class whose optimal play requires memorizing invisible timestamps;
- a visual-effects storm of clocks, gears, hourglasses, and blue portals;
- a boss-trivializing hard-stop class;
- an Echo Thief overlap built around copying enemy skills or identities;
- a Somnarch overlap built around alternate reality territory;
- a Voidbringer overlap built around portal geometry;
- a fragile caster whose entire defense is avoiding contact;
- a class that only becomes distinct during its ultimate.

The class should feel surgical, inevitable, fractured, cruel, precise, and physically present.

## 3. Lore and origin

### CLS-ANA-ORIGIN — The Arrested-Time Anchor

During the Binding Epoch, one World-Lattice principle governed **sequence integrity**. The wound below reality did not merely exert force; it caused events to occur without stable order. Walls cracked before impact. Blood left bodies before blades arrived. Entire settlements remembered futures that never became real.

The custodians of this principle were altered to carry contradictions without allowing them to spread. Their nervous systems learned to preserve several neighboring outcomes. Their scars could precede injury. Their bodies aged unevenly, with organs existing at different biological moments. They became living temporal anchors capable of deciding which event was permitted to count as the present.

Later civilizations converted fragments of this function into calendars, execution rites, royal succession law, disaster prophecy, and the doctrine of legally erased events. The Meridian Court rose from these remnants. Its chronologists did not merely record history; they prosecuted events considered too dangerous to have happened.

Some Anachrons were born near failed anchors. Some were survivors of erased wars. Some were deliberately manufactured as judicial weapons. Others are people whose deaths were revoked without restoring the life that originally followed.

The player Anachron is one such contradiction: a person who has already reached several incompatible endings and continues because none has yet obtained sole authority.

### Personal thematic question

The Anachron arc asks:

> Is identity the sum of what happened, what might have happened, or the consequence one chooses to carry forward?

The class must ultimately decide whether survival means preserving every possible self, selecting one true history, or accepting that personhood can remain incomplete without becoming false.

## 4. Shared campaign role and personal arc

The Anachron follows the same campaign timeline as every launch class.

### ACT-01 — Recognition: The Crown Remembers Tomorrow

The Hollow Crown does not merely reveal the remaining Lattice anchors. When held near the Anachron, it displays several incompatible routes at once, including roads not yet built and regions already destroyed in alternate outcomes.

Personal beat:

- The Anachron recognizes the crown-map as a sequence engine rather than prophecy.
- The Sunken Crypts briefly show the Hollow King alive, dead, crowned, and never born.
- A sealed inscription addresses the player by a title they have not yet earned.
- The class learns that its condition is related to the Lattice rather than private madness.

### ACT-02 — The Red Basilica: Guilt Before the Crime

The Covenant of Measure prosecutes several citizens for sins their bodies are predicted to commit during future ritual collapse.

Personal beat:

- The Anachron witnesses punishment being used as an attempt to create the crime it claims to prevent.
- The Covenant offers legal recognition in exchange for becoming an instrument of preemptive judgment.
- Penitent and Anachron lore intersect around responsibility: written law governs action, while temporal law governs sequence and consequence.
- The player can expose a forged future without changing the shared campaign outcome.

### ACT-03 — The Country Behind Sleep: Memories of the Unlived

The dead population dreams events that have not occurred. Some dreams are genuine neighboring timelines; others are emotional possibilities given form.

Personal beat:

- The Anachron encounters a version of themselves remembered by people they never met.
- Somnarch integration distinguishes dream possibility from temporal consequence.
- The Quiet Parliament attempts to vote one potential future into historical fact.
- The class learns that being remembered does not prove an event occurred.

### ACT-04 — The Reliquary March: The Saint Who Died in Every Procession

The Procession carries relic fragments from several mutually exclusive deaths of the same saint. Each relic insists it is the original.

Personal beat:

- The Anachron can perceive that all relics are authentic outcomes from a damaged sequence.
- Relic Host integration distinguishes inherited memory from temporal duplication.
- The Meridian Court arrives to destroy all but one version, claiming plurality itself is contamination.
- The player may preserve the saint's contradictions as testimony rather than selecting a convenient official history.

### ACT-05 — The Inverted Sea: The Drowned Day

The suspended ocean contains layers of time compressed like geological strata. Entire naval battles, migrations, extinctions, and coastlines exist inside its pressure.

Personal beat:

- The Anachron walks through moments stored as physical depth.
- Tidewrought integration distinguishes pressure memory from sequence control.
- The player discovers a future where the Ninth Tide survives the campaign by becoming a temporal current.
- The Meridian Court offers to erase that possibility by removing every cause connected to the player.

### ACT-06 — Definition: The Hour Without End

This is the Anachron's central act. The Lattice citadel exists across several ruined eras simultaneously. Other classes navigate fragments; the Anachron perceives the transitions between them.

The **Calendar Eater** is revealed as a failed temporal custodian that consumes dates, succession, and causal ownership to prevent contradiction from reaching the central anchor.

Personal beat:

- The Meridian Court reveals that the player has been executed, pardoned, recruited, and erased in different authorized histories.
- The class discovers its continued existence is not an accident; one branch of the old Lattice repeatedly preserved it as an emergency operator.
- The temptation is absolute authority over sequence: choose the one history everyone must inhabit.
- The player rejects, inherits, limits, or redistributes that authority without splitting into a separate campaign.

### Definition beat — The Consequence Chosen

The Anachron resolves the personal arc by defining stability as accountable continuity rather than enforced singularity.

- The class accepts that not every abandoned possibility must be preserved.
- It refuses the Meridian Court's doctrine that inconvenient lives can be erased for structural cleanliness.
- It treats memory, consequence, and testimony as different things rather than collapsing them into one official truth.
- It chooses which outcome to carry forward while acknowledging the cost of alternatives that were denied.

This remains a personal resolution inside the shared climax.

## 5. Visual identity and silhouette

### Silhouette rules

The Anachron should read instantly through:

- an asymmetrical body whose armor, age, and injuries appear drawn from neighboring moments;
- one arm or side of the torso slightly ahead or behind the rest of the motion;
- layered restraint bands, temporal sutures, and anchor pins driven through armor into flesh;
- a compact executioner's silhouette rather than a flowing wizard robe;
- weapons with repeatable geometric profiles suited to exact motion;
- one persistent “missing interval” in the silhouette, such as a gap where armor should connect;
- short afterpositions that remain anatomically recognizable rather than translucent clone armies;
- visible state scars that shift when the active sequence changes.

### Material language

- blackened execution steel;
- pale ceramic chronology plates marked by cuts rather than numbers;
- old leather repaired with future alloys;
- flesh showing injuries at different healing stages simultaneously;
- red-black causal filaments connecting impacts to delayed consequences;
- colorless frame tearing around unresolved movement;
- oxidized brass used sparingly as judicial hardware, not decorative clockwork;
- brittle glass seams containing aborted moments.

### Animation language

- attacks should preserve exact, readable poses that can later recur;
- some impacts occur a fraction before the weapon visually arrives, but never enough to damage readability;
- Reprise effects reuse authored motion fragments rather than spawning generic copies;
- movement changes position through omitted intervals, not portal traversal;
- defensive corrections should feel like selecting a different outcome, not rewinding a video;
- high Disjunction causes subtle disagreement between body parts, stance, and shadow;
- Convergence should violently remove excess afterpositions and leave one committed body.

### Readability rule

The player must always understand:

- current Disjunction;
- which personal state is Anchored;
- which enemies carry temporal Imprints;
- what consequence is Delayed;
- where a Reprise will occur;
- whether a return point is safe and valid;
- when Convergence is available;
- which effects alter order versus merely repeat damage.

No essential state may be communicated only through tiny timeline icons or timestamp text.

## 6. Combat role

### Primary role

Close-to-mid-range sequence duelist and delayed-burst controller.

### Secondary roles

- precision single-target setup;
- pack deletion through synchronized delayed consequences;
- positional correction and return routing;
- reactive defense through state selection;
- boss stagger through repeated authored impact;
- conditional debuff extension and compression;
- tactical ally protection through localized consequence delay.

### Intended strengths

- exceptional reward for deliberate sequencing;
- strong single-target damage without requiring permanent melee uptime;
- flexible engagement through Anchors and omitted-interval movement;
- meaningful defensive recovery that still preserves the cost of mistakes;
- high burst through Convergence;
- strong boss value through temporal strain rather than hard stop;
- broad build diversity between Reprise, Delay, Severance, Anchor, and Convergence play.

### Intended weaknesses

- weak output when using skills without planned order;
- careless Disjunction can reduce defenses or trigger destabilization;
- delayed damage can feel slow when immediate target selection is poor;
- Anchors require valid placement and can be punished by moving hazards;
- recovery is conditional and cannot erase every loss;
- visual clutter risk rises sharply when too many events are stored;
- the class is vulnerable during badly timed Convergence recovery;
- ranged coverage is tactical rather than effortless screen-wide casting.

## 8. Unique resource — Disjunction

### Definition

**Disjunction** measures how many incompatible events the Anachron is actively carrying without resolving them.

Proposed range: 0–100.

Disjunction is generated by:

- creating temporal Imprints;
- preserving an Anchor state;
- delaying damage or crowd-control consequences;
- maintaining abandoned positions;
- causing Reprises before earlier Imprints resolve;
- denying an enemy action through Sever effects;
- crossing specific threshold states through path investment.

Disjunction is spent or reduced by:

- resolving Reprises;
- collapsing stored consequences;
- returning to an Anchor;
- selecting one state during Convergence;
- allowing Delayed effects to occur naturally;
- using stabilization skills;
- triggering class-specific execution windows.

### Disjunction bands

#### 0–24 — Aligned

- stable defenses;
- low temporal potency;
- fast setup availability;
- ideal for recovery and repositioning.

#### 25–49 — Divergent

- improved Imprint and Reprise strength;
- stronger movement omissions;
- modest visual disagreement around the body.

#### 50–74 — Fractured

- significantly stronger Delay, Sever, and Convergence effects;
- reduced passive Guard unless offset by investment;
- greater Anchor instability;
- increased opportunity for advanced path mechanics.

#### 75–99 — Critical Sequence

- maximum offensive leverage;
- defensive state becomes dangerous;
- some skills gain alternate outcomes;
- invalid or overloaded Anchors may collapse.

#### 100 — Causal Rupture

Base behavior:

- active unresolved Imprints collapse automatically at reduced efficiency;
- the Anachron suffers a brief destabilization rather than a long stun;
- the current Anchor is consumed;
- Disjunction returns to a safe middle value rather than zero;
- major self-damage is avoided unless a dedicated high-risk build explicitly opts into it.

### Resource philosophy

Disjunction is not mana and should not encourage passive hoarding. It is controlled instability. High values must feel powerful, readable, and dangerous without turning every build into threshold babysitting.

## 9. Signature system — Authored Sequence

The Anachron's signature mechanic is the ability to deliberately author a short combat sequence from several event types.

### 9.1 Temporal Imprints

A **Temporal Imprint** records one concise gameplay fact for later interaction.

Supported foundational Imprints:

1. **Impact Imprint** — records a percentage of direct damage and stagger dealt by one authored hit.
2. **Position Imprint** — records a valid location and facing.
3. **Wound Imprint** — records a limited portion of damage recently taken.
4. **State Imprint** — records one eligible class-applied status or target condition.
5. **Motion Imprint** — records one movement vector or weapon sweep.

Imprints must never store unrestricted full game state. They use curated data structures and explicit eligibility rules.

### 9.2 Reprise

A **Reprise** causes an authored Imprint to recur.

- Impact Reprise repeats adjusted damage and stagger without rerolling every original proc.
- Position Reprise creates movement, return, or a localized strike from the recorded point.
- Wound Reprise does not simply heal; it selects how much of the recorded injury becomes present consequence.
- State Reprise extends, transfers, or consumes eligible class-applied states.
- Motion Reprise repeats geometry through authored phantom force, not a free autonomous clone.

### 9.3 Anchored Self

The Anachron may maintain one primary **Anchor** containing:

- position;
- facing;
- a capped Life reference;
- selected self-buff state;
- current weapon-mode context;
- creation time and validity rules.

Returning to an Anchor:

- cannot restore Life above the recorded capped reference;
- cannot resurrect the player;
- cannot recover consumed items or external cooldowns;
- cannot erase boss phase transitions or world state;
- cannot return into invalid collision or lethal unloaded space;
- always preserves at least part of damage taken after the Anchor unless a build pays a meaningful cost.

The fantasy is selecting a survivable branch, not free undo.

### 9.4 Delayed Consequence

Eligible damage, stagger, or debuff effects may be marked **Delayed**.

Delayed consequences:

- remain visible on the target or player;
- resolve after a fixed interval or trigger;
- can be accelerated, transferred, compressed, or partially severed;
- are capped independently for normal enemies, elites, bosses, allies, and the player;
- never postpone death indefinitely.

### 9.5 Severance

**Severance** removes the link between an eligible cause and consequence.

Examples:

- strip the repeated portion from an enemy's telegraphed follow-up attack;
- prevent one class-applied debuff from expiring normally and instead detonate it;
- convert part of delayed incoming damage into Disjunction;
- deny a normal enemy's movement continuation;
- reduce a boss's sequence stability without cancelling required mechanics.

Severance is not universal dispel, immunity, or boss stop.

### 9.6 Convergence

**Convergence** resolves multiple active Imprints into one selected outcome.

Possible outcomes:

- repeat all eligible Impact Imprints on one target at diminishing coefficients;
- cause Position Imprints to emit crossing attack lines;
- collapse Delayed consequences simultaneously;
- select the safest valid personal Anchor at a high resource cost;
- compress several State Imprints into Temporal Strain;
- erase unused Imprints for defense or recovery.

Convergence is the class's core payoff and should be mechanically available outside the ultimate.

## 10. Boss-safe temporal mechanics

Bosses and major enemies require temporal value without hard-control abuse.

### Temporal Strain

Bosses accumulate **Temporal Strain** when affected by:

- repeated authored impacts;
- compressed Delayed consequences;
- interrupted follow-up sequences;
- Convergence effects;
- position-line crossings;
- path-specific chronology attacks.

At thresholds, Temporal Strain may:

- increase stagger damage;
- expose a brief damage window;
- reduce repeated attack follow-up speed;
- amplify the next Reprise coefficient;
- create a removable defensive layer;
- reward precise ordering without freezing the boss.

Temporal Strain must not:

- pause mandatory boss mechanics;
- rewind phase transitions;
- duplicate loot or phase rewards;
- cause animation-state corruption;
- move bosses to invalid prior positions;
- create infinite stagger loops.

## 11. Weapons and equipment identity

### Primary weapon families

#### Duelling blades

- fast exact strikes;
- strong Impact Imprint generation;
- high precision and Reprise scaling;
- lower inherent area coverage.

#### Execution swords

- deliberate arcs and heavy authored impacts;
- stronger stagger and Convergence payoff;
- slower setup cadence.

#### Chronal polearms

- line control and Position Imprint interaction;
- strong crossing geometry;
- balanced range and commitment.

#### Judicial hammers

- delayed impact, Temporal Strain, and boss stagger;
- lower mobility but strong Anchor defense.

#### Thread bows or repeaters

- limited ranged identity based on marked trajectories;
- motion and line Reprises rather than generic projectile spam;
- should remain tactical, not off-screen dominant.

### Proposed class-specific equipment slot — Sequence Brace

A **Sequence Brace** is a restraint assembly attached to the spine, sternum, or weapon arm.

It can modify:

- Anchor capacity and validity;
- Imprint type limits;
- Disjunction thresholds;
- Reprise coefficients;
- Delayed consequence behavior;
- Convergence selection rules;
- stabilization after Causal Rupture.

Whether every launch class receives a dedicated class slot remains an owner-level shared-systems decision.

### Gear philosophy

Weapons determine attack geometry and cadence. Sequence Braces determine how chronology is stored and resolved. Armor supports survivability and build direction without replacing the class tree.

## 12. Movement identity

The Anachron moves by omitting, preserving, or reselecting intervals rather than opening portals.

### Baseline dodge — Missing Step

The Anachron removes a short segment from their visible movement and reappears at the end of a collision-checked trajectory.

Rules:

- travel path is still validated;
- the player cannot cross sealed geometry;
- invulnerability duration is shorter than the full visual omission;
- attack follow-ups may originate from the abandoned start point through upgrades;
- high Disjunction can extend distance but increases recovery risk.

### Advanced movement verbs

- **Return:** move to an Anchor.
- **Skip:** omit a short interval along current momentum.
- **Exchange:** swap which of two valid Position Imprints becomes current.
- **Recur:** repeat a prior movement vector from the present location.
- **Arrive First:** place an attack consequence at the destination before the body completes movement.

Movement should reward planning and geometry, not remove positioning as a concern.

## 13. Foundational active skill kit

The names are proposed. Stable IDs are provided for future implementation discussion.

### ANA-SK-01 — First Cut, Second Wound

A precise weapon strike that creates an Impact Imprint. Recasting within the window triggers a reduced Reprise from the original angle.

Purpose:

- level-one identity skill;
- immediate class comprehension;
- basic single-target and pack sequencing.

### ANA-SK-02 — Missing Step

Perform the baseline omitted-interval dodge. Leaves a short-lived Position Imprint at the starting point.

Purpose:

- mobility;
- Position Imprint teaching;
- later cross-path attack geometry.

### ANA-SK-03 — Deferred Ruin

Strike or project a narrow line that applies part of its damage immediately and stores the rest as Delayed consequence.

Purpose:

- delayed-damage foundation;
- burst setup;
- boss Temporal Strain interaction.

### ANA-SK-04 — Anchor the Living

Record a personal Anchor with position, facing, and capped Life reference. Recast to return under safety rules.

Purpose:

- signature defense;
- planned aggression;
- high-skill movement routing.

### ANA-SK-05 — Recurring Arc

Perform a sweeping attack. After a brief interval, the motion recurs from the same origin as authored phantom force.

Purpose:

- area clear;
- Motion Imprint teaching;
- crossing-line synergies.

### ANA-SK-06 — Sever the Following

A counter-like strike that marks an enemy's next eligible follow-up. Normal enemies lose the continuation; elites and bosses suffer Temporal Strain and reduced follow-up potency.

Purpose:

- reactive defense;
- sequence control;
- boss-safe counter identity.

### ANA-SK-07 — Borrowed Injury

Record a portion of recent damage as a Wound Imprint. Recast to divide the recorded amount between immediate retained damage, gradual recovery, and Disjunction according to build rules.

Purpose:

- active recovery;
- meaningful cost preservation;
- defensive build foundation.

### ANA-SK-08 — Crossed Outcomes

Create two Position Imprints around a target area. Attacks or movement between them generate cutting sequence lines.

Purpose:

- area control;
- positional build foundation;
- pack and boss geometry.

### ANA-SK-09 — History Refuses You

Apply a State Imprint that records class-applied debuffs on a target. On trigger, selected debuffs recur at reduced duration or collapse into damage and Temporal Strain.

Purpose:

- status hybrid support;
- build interaction without stealing enemy abilities;
- elite and boss setup.

### ANA-SK-10 — Execution Already Passed

Mark a wounded target with a future execution threshold. If the target crosses the threshold during the window, a delayed finishing strike occurs. Bosses instead suffer amplified stagger and stored damage.

Purpose:

- execute identity;
- priority-target pressure;
- dramatic delayed payoff.

### ANA-SK-11 — Unmake the Interval

Create a short field where the Anachron's own eligible attacks resolve their delayed portions faster and enemy follow-up chains are weakened.

Purpose:

- controlled local setup;
- not a global time stop;
- Convergence preparation.

### ANA-SK-12 — Convergent Sentence

Consume selected active Imprints and resolve them according to current path priorities.

Base priority:

1. collapse eligible Delayed enemy consequences;
2. trigger one Impact Reprise per target;
3. emit attack lines between Position Imprints;
4. reduce Disjunction based on successful resolutions.

Purpose:

- foundational payoff;
- build-defining skill outside the ultimate;
- visible mastery expression.

## 14. Ultimate — All Ends Are Present

### ANA-ULT-01 — All Ends Are Present

The Anachron temporarily carries several authored terminal outcomes without immediately resolving them.

During the ultimate:

- valid attacks automatically create limited Impact Imprints;
- Missing Step preserves both start and end Position Imprints;
- Delayed consequences cannot expire early;
- Causal Rupture is suspended but Disjunction continues accumulating;
- one visual terminal state appears for major enemies based on current health and Temporal Strain;
- recasting or expiration triggers a controlled mass Convergence.

The final Convergence:

- repeats selected authored impacts at diminishing coefficients;
- collapses Delayed consequences;
- emits crossing sequence lines;
- applies boss-safe Temporal Strain;
- selects one valid personal outcome for post-ultimate position and partial recovery;
- clears excess visual states immediately.

### Ultimate constraints

- It must not reset every cooldown.
- It must not make the player permanently invulnerable.
- It must not duplicate every proc or on-hit effect.
- It must not freeze bosses or cancel phase mechanics.
- It must not create autonomous clones.
- It must preserve weapon identity and authored movement.
- The class must remain complete without it.

## 15. Class-specific progression paths

### PATH-ANA-01 — The Repeating Blade

Fantasy: a duelist who perfects one action until reality accepts repetition as force.

Focus:

- Impact and Motion Imprints;
- weapon geometry;
- critical precision;
- Reprise chains;
- single-target execution;
- authored attack repetition.

Representative nodes:

- first Reprise gains increased stagger;
- critical authored hits store a larger Impact coefficient;
- Motion Reprises can inherit modified weapon width;
- repeating the same skill increases cost but improves final Convergence;
- changing attack direction creates crossing cuts;
- executions refund limited Disjunction.

Risk:

- must not become a one-button echo build;
- proc inheritance requires strict caps.

### PATH-ANA-02 — Meridian Fugitive

Fantasy: a hunted survivor who weaponizes abandoned positions and refuses the history chosen by the Court.

Focus:

- Position Imprints;
- Anchor play;
- Missing Step;
- movement attacks;
- evasion and return routing;
- line-crossing damage.

Representative nodes:

- returning to an Anchor emits a weapon arc;
- invalidated Anchors become retaliatory fields instead of disappearing;
- Missing Step can bank one Recur vector;
- crossing one's own sequence line grants Guard;
- Position Imprints persist longer at high Disjunction;
- Convergence may select the safest valid Position Imprint.

Risk:

- cannot allow unrestricted arena bypass;
- return safety must remain predictable.

### PATH-ANA-03 — Wound Accountant

Fantasy: an executioner-physician who decides when injury becomes real and forces every debt to be paid.

Focus:

- Wound Imprints;
- Delayed consequences;
- controlled recovery;
- execute thresholds;
- damage compression;
- defensive bruiser play.

Representative nodes:

- part of blocked damage becomes a Wound Imprint;
- Delayed incoming damage grants temporary Guard;
- healing while carrying a Wound Imprint increases its offensive conversion but not total recovery;
- executions transfer remaining delayed damage to nearby enemies at capped value;
- Causal Rupture becomes a damaging release instead of destabilization;
- Convergence can convert unresolved personal wounds into area impact at a real health cost.

Risk:

- must not create immortal self-healing loops;
- life accounting requires deterministic rules.

### PATH-ANA-04 — Last Historian

Fantasy: a keeper of forbidden outcomes who prosecutes enemies with the evidence of everything they attempted to become.

Focus:

- State Imprints;
- Temporal Strain;
- debuff recurrence;
- Severance;
- boss control;
- tactical support without dependence on allies.

Representative nodes:

- class-applied debuffs can be recorded as one compressed State Imprint;
- Severance applies additional Temporal Strain to elites and bosses;
- repeated status duration becomes damage instead of infinite extension;
- Convergence selects one dominant State Imprint per target;
- enemy follow-up attacks create evidence marks when they connect;
- consuming evidence grants party-readable but self-sufficient vulnerability windows.

Risk:

- cannot overlap Echo Thief by copying enemy abilities;
- status recursion must be bounded.

## 17. Proposed keystones

### KEY-ANA-01 — Only the Final Blow Was Real

All non-Reprise direct hits deal reduced immediate damage. Their stored Impact Imprints are stronger, and Convergence heavily amplifies the final authored hit.

Build effect:

- transforms steady damage into planned execution bursts.

### KEY-ANA-02 — I Refuse This Death

A lethal hit consumes the active Anchor, leaves the player at minimal Life, and converts most prevented damage into unavoidable Delayed consequence. Long internal cooldown.

Build effect:

- dramatic survival with debt rather than free cheat death.

### KEY-ANA-03 — No Event Stands Alone

Position Imprints automatically connect with sequence lines, but each additional line raises Disjunction upkeep.

Build effect:

- converts movement routing into persistent combat geometry.

### KEY-ANA-04 — The Body Keeps Both Accounts

Wound Imprints can exceed normal capacity and grant offensive power, but all recovery is reduced until the next Convergence.

Build effect:

- enables dangerous injury-storage bruiser builds.

## 20. Gear interactions and affix vocabulary

### Desirable class-affix families

- increased Impact Imprint coefficient;
- increased Reprise stagger;
- increased Position Imprint duration;
- reduced Anchor invalidation penalty;
- increased Delayed consequence capacity;
- increased Temporal Strain generation;
- reduced Disjunction gained from specific Imprint types;
- improved Convergence selection efficiency;
- increased Missing Step line damage;
- improved Wound Imprint recovery conversion;
- increased execution-threshold potency;
- stabilization after Causal Rupture;
- weapon-family-specific authored geometry.

### Affixes to avoid

- unrestricted cooldown reset chance;
- chance to fully rewind damage taken;
- infinite debuff-duration extension;
- unrestricted duplicate on-hit effects;
- global enemy time stop;
- random teleport on hit;
- automatic Convergence without clear player control;
- hidden timestamp math required for optimization;
- chance to duplicate unique-item triggers recursively.

### Proc inheritance rule

Reprises and Convergence echoes inherit only explicitly approved properties:

- base weapon damage contribution;
- damage type conversion;
- critical state where specified;
- stagger coefficient;
- class-tag scaling.

They do not freely retrigger:

- item explosions;
- resource-on-hit loops;
- additional Reprises;
- unique-item summons;
- execute chains beyond caps;
- global on-kill effects;
- external cooldown resets.

## 21. Party and co-op identity

The Anachron must be fully effective solo.

### Solo identity

- authors its own Imprints;
- generates its own Temporal Strain;
- maintains its own Anchors;
- resolves its own Delayed consequences;
- has complete damage, defense, and control loops without allies.

### Co-op contribution

- creates visible boss Temporal Strain windows;
- can delay a limited portion of ally damage with explicit consent or predictable rules;
- weakens enemy follow-up chains;
- creates crossing-line zones allies can exploit without being required to understand the full class system;
- can preserve one shared objective state only where encounter rules explicitly support it.

### Co-op prohibitions

- never rewind ally position without consent;
- never reset ally cooldowns as a baseline role;
- never erase another player's resource expenditure;
- never alter loot history;
- never restore failed objective states;
- never make optimal party play dependent on one Anachron.

### Same-class interaction

- each Anachron owns separate Imprints and Anchors;
- Temporal Strain contribution may aggregate through capped shared boss stacks;
- Position Imprints are visually keyed per player;
- one player's Convergence cannot consume another's Imprints;
- ally delayed-damage effects cannot recurse between Anachrons.

## 24. Technical implementation risks

### RISK-ANA-01 — Full-state rewind temptation

Risk:

- attempting to serialize arbitrary scene or actor state will create bugs and impossible test scope.

Mitigation:

- use curated Imprint payloads only;
- never promise universal rewind;
- keep encounter state authoritative and forward-only.

### RISK-ANA-02 — Proc recursion

Risk:

- repeated attacks retrigger repeated attacks, item effects, resource gains, or executions.

Mitigation:

- explicit authored-event tags;
- secondary-event inheritance whitelist;
- hard chain-depth limits;
- deterministic event ownership.

### RISK-ANA-03 — Anchor collision failure

Risk:

- return points become blocked, unloaded, hazardous, or outside encounter bounds.

Mitigation:

- validate at creation and return;
- store fallback nav-safe position;
- expose invalidation immediately;
- never teleport through sealed encounter geometry.

### RISK-ANA-04 — Life accounting exploits

Risk:

- Wound Imprints and Anchors create duplication, negative damage, immortal loops, or healing amplification.

Mitigation:

- capped references;
- damage ledger IDs;
- no event may be recovered twice;
- separate restoration, prevention, and delayed-damage categories.

### RISK-ANA-05 — Boss animation corruption

Risk:

- Severance or temporal effects interrupt required boss transitions.

Mitigation:

- bosses receive Temporal Strain and follow-up modifiers rather than animation rewind;
- explicit immunity and substitution tables;
- encounter-owned sequences remain authoritative.

### RISK-ANA-06 — Save/load persistence

Risk:

- saving during unresolved Imprints creates stale actor references or invalid Anchors.

Mitigation:

- combat-temporal state is transient;
- clear or safely resolve on checkpoint serialization;
- do not persist scene-local target references.

### RISK-ANA-07 — Multiplayer authority

Risk:

- clients disagree about event order, damage ownership, or valid return positions.

Mitigation:

- server-authoritative sequence IDs;
- deterministic ordering;
- compact Imprint replication;
- no client-only rewind assumptions.

### RISK-ANA-08 — Visual clutter

Risk:

- multiple afterpositions, lines, and delayed states obscure hazards.

Mitigation:

- strict global and per-player visual caps;
- priority rendering;
- fade non-actionable history;
- accessibility simplification.

### RISK-ANA-09 — Performance spikes

Risk:

- Convergence resolves too many events in one frame.

Mitigation:

- aggregate compatible damage events;
- cap per-target Imprints;
- batch visuals separately from authoritative damage;
- stagger noncritical presentation without changing outcome.

### RISK-ANA-10 — Hidden complexity

Risk:

- class is technically powerful but impossible to understand.

Mitigation:

- one visible resource;
- limited foundational Imprint types;
- plain-language payoff summaries;
- predictable path-specific selection.

### RISK-ANA-11 — Encounter bypass

Risk:

- Anchors and Missing Step cross doors, walls, phase boundaries, or objective gates.

Mitigation:

- shared movement validation;
- encounter volumes invalidate positions;
- no return across scene transitions.

### RISK-ANA-12 — Future content compatibility

Risk:

- every new status, item, and boss requires bespoke temporal behavior.

Mitigation:

- default non-recordable policy;
- explicit eligibility tags;
- shared event interfaces;
- automated tests for new recordable effects.

## 25. Non-negotiable design rules

1. The Anachron manipulates authored combat sequences, not unrestricted world state.
2. The class must be understandable as imprint, repeat, collapse.
3. Reprise is not a generic chance to cast twice.
4. Anchor is not a free full heal or resurrection.
5. Disjunction must reward controlled instability rather than passive waiting.
6. Bosses receive Temporal Strain instead of hard time stop or rewind.
7. The class must retain a physical weapon-combat identity.
8. Movement uses omitted intervals, not portals.
9. Afterpositions are authored effects, not autonomous clone pets.
10. No build may require exact timestamp memorization.
11. Convergence must exist outside the ultimate.
12. The ultimate must not reset every cooldown.
13. Temporal effects cannot duplicate loot, objective completion, or consumable use.
14. Wound accounting must preserve meaningful consequences.
15. Proc inheritance must be explicit and capped.
16. The class may record class-applied states, not steal enemy identities or skills.
17. Visual chronology must never obscure enemy telegraphs.
18. A low-complexity endgame build must remain viable.
19. Every path must support both normal enemies and bosses.
20. The class must function fully without co-op.
21. Returning to an Anchor must obey collision and encounter rules.
22. Multiple Anachrons cannot consume or recursively delay one another's events.
23. Narrative time manipulation cannot create a separate campaign timeline.
24. The personal arc must resolve inside the shared campaign.
25. Uncertain cosmology remains proposed until owner approval.
26. The Anachron must feel brutal and consequential, not whimsical or gadget-like.

## 26. Cross-class consistency audit

### Voidbringer

Potential overlap:

- spatial displacement;
- afterimages and unusual movement;
- event compression could resemble gravity compression.

Protected boundary:

- Voidbringer manipulates space, gravity, portals, and Corruption.
- Anachron manipulates authored order, repetition, and consequence.
- Missing Step never creates a traversable portal.
- Convergence is temporal accounting, not spatial collapse.

Result: compatible with clear effect language.

### Penitent

Potential overlap:

- sentence, law, judgment, and execution vocabulary.

Protected boundary:

- Penitent writes ritual law into the battlefield through Brands and Circles.
- Anachron determines event order and evidentiary consequence.
- Penitent's authority is devotional and sacrificial; Anachron's is chronological and judicial.

Result: compatible, but naming should avoid excessive shared “judgment” terminology.

### Graftborn

Potential overlap:

- altered body and injury adaptation.

Protected boundary:

- Graftborn authors anatomy and remembers trauma biologically.
- Anachron records curated event consequences and bodily states temporally.
- Wound Imprints do not mutate body regions.

Result: compatible.

### Somnarch

Potential overlap:

- unreal outcomes, alternate states, and repeated positions.

Protected boundary:

- Somnarch imposes dream law and hostile territory.
- Anachron remains in the waking world and manipulates sequence.
- Unlived memories are not dream manifestations.

Result: compatible with strong presentation distinction.

### Relic Host

Potential overlap:

- inherited memories and several identities occupying one body.

Protected boundary:

- Relic Host negotiates with discrete dead Presences.
- Anachron carries incompatible versions of one personal sequence.
- Afterpositions have no independent personality.

Result: compatible.

### Gorgon

Potential overlap:

- state recording and boss-safe control.

Protected boundary:

- Gorgon fixes attention, venom, movement, and structure through gaze and petrification.
- Anachron records and resolves event order.
- Temporal Strain never becomes stone, Carapace, or venom ecology.

Result: compatible.

### Tidewrought

Potential overlap:

- stored pressure, delayed release, and movement lines.

Protected boundary:

- Tidewrought stores physical pressure and shapes currents.
- Anachron stores authored consequences and creates sequence lines.
- Current Lines move force and bodies; sequence lines resolve temporal geometry.

Result: compatible.

### Echo Thief — reserved

Highest-risk overlap:

- repetition, echoes, and recorded actions.

Protected boundary:

- Echo Thief should appropriate identity, technique, voice, or enemy-derived patterns.
- Anachron may only repeat its own authored actions and curated class-applied states.
- Anachron never copies enemy skills.

Result: compatible only if this boundary remains locked.

### Choirborn — reserved

Potential overlap:

- rhythm, cadence, and action sequencing.

Protected boundary:

- Choirborn should operate through collective voice, resonance, harmony, or sonic authority.
- Anachron uses causality and event order, not music or resonance.

Result: compatible.

### Plaguebringer — reserved

Potential overlap:

- delayed damage and status compression.

Protected boundary:

- Plaguebringer should own infection ecology, spread, incubation, and disease transformation.
- Anachron's Delay is temporal and does not spread biologically.
- State Imprints do not create contagion.

Result: compatible.

### Overall audit result

No direct contradiction was found with the shared campaign, completed launch-class bibles, or reserved-class boundaries. The highest-risk future boundary is Echo Thief, followed by visual overlap between Position Imprints, Somnarch territory, and Voidbringer spatial effects.

## 29. Final class promise

The Anachron should let the player feel that combat is not a stream of attacks but a sentence whose order can be rewritten.

The fantasy is not escaping consequence. It is choosing which consequence becomes real, carrying the cost of every denied alternative, and forcing monsters, kings, and broken gods to arrive at the ending the player authored for them.
