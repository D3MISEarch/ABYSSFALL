# Gorgon Class Bible

> Status: **PROPOSED — owner review required.**
>
> This document extends the shared campaign and systems framework without changing existing gameplay code or locking unresolved narrative decisions as canon.

## 1. Class thesis

**The Gorgon is a condemned metamorphic hunter who weaponizes attention, venom, and irreversible bodily states.**

The class is not a conventional poison rogue and not a mythological statue gimmick. Its fantasy is control through exposure: enemies become more vulnerable the longer they look, pursue, strike, or remain inside the Gorgon's hunting geometry. The Gorgon marks prey through gaze, scent, reflected movement, and venomous pressure, then decides whether to preserve, rupture, harvest, or transform what has been marked.

A skilled Gorgon does not simply deal damage over time. They author an escalating relationship with enemies:

1. **Expose** prey through line of sight, proximity, movement trails, or deliberate provocation.
2. **Fix** prey into a readable state through Stare, Venom, or mineralized tissue.
3. **Exploit** that state with precise repositioning, ambush, shatter, execution, or self-transformation.
4. **Shed** the encounter's accumulated danger and convert it into the next hunt.

The initial loop should be understandable in seconds: mark enemies, poison them, reposition, cash out. Mastery comes from controlling who sees whom, deciding when not to petrify, manipulating thresholds, and sequencing transformations across packs and bosses.

## 2. Locked identity boundaries

The Gorgon must not become:

- a generic poison assassin;
- a medusa cosplay class defined only by turning enemies to stone;
- a permanent hard-control machine that trivializes elites or bosses;
- a pet snake summoner;
- a ranged class whose optimal play is kiting off-screen;
- a shapeshifter who abandons the base kit whenever transformed;
- a stealth class whose power disappears in boss arenas;
- a class dependent on enemies literally having eyes;
- a seduction archetype;
- a green-particle reskin of another caster.

The class should feel predatory, bodily, cruel, deliberate, and tactically intimate.

## 3. Lore and origin

### CLS-GOR-ORIGIN — The Venomous Anchor

During the Binding Epoch, one World-Lattice principle required reality to **retain a chosen state under pressure**. Where void displaced, penance inscribed, flesh repaired, dream overlaid, and relic remembered, the venomous anchor forced unstable matter to hold still long enough to be bound.

Its custodians were altered into living arrest mechanisms. Their blood carried mineralizing agents. Their nervous systems could identify structural weakness through reflected light, vibration, and chemical trace. Their gaze did not cast a spell; it imposed a biological and metaphysical instruction:

> Remain as you are. Become unable to flee your current truth.

Later kingdoms misunderstood these custodians as cursed monsters. Their lineages were hunted, harvested for antivenom, displayed as saints' trophies, or sealed beneath courts as living execution devices.

The player Gorgon is a surviving descendant, deliberate reconstruction, or escaped state weapon. Their body contains dormant anchor organs that awaken as the Lattice fails.

### Personal thematic question

The Gorgon's arc asks:

> Is preservation an act of protection, possession, punishment, or fear?

The class must ultimately define whether its power exists to freeze the world into obedience or to grant fragile things enough stability to survive change.

## 4. Shared campaign role and personal arc

The Gorgon follows the same campaign timeline as all launch classes.

### ACT-01 — Recognition: The Crown That Refuses to Decay

The Hollow Crown contains a layer that has not aged despite centuries underwater. When the Gorgon approaches, dormant scales along their neck and forearms mineralize in sympathy. The crown-map identifies the Gorgon as a surviving expression of the Lattice's arrest principle.

Personal beat:

- The Gorgon realizes their curse was engineered.
- Hollow King remains and preserved courtiers react differently to Gorgon abilities.
- Optional lore reveals that the crypt's dead were not merely buried; some were held in stable states to prevent deeper contamination.

### ACT-02 — The Red Basilica: The Harvested Bloodline

The Covenant of Measure uses diluted Gorgon blood to preserve confession scars and prevent ritual subjects from dying before judgment is complete.

Personal beat:

- The Gorgon encounters a sanctioned blood-harvesting order.
- The class may destroy, expose, or repurpose the order's archive.
- Graftborn and Gorgon lore intersect through medical exploitation but remain mechanically distinct: authored adaptation versus imposed arrest.

### ACT-03 — The Country Behind Sleep: The Unblinking District

A district trapped inside collective dream cannot wake because every sleeper is preserved at the instant before death.

Personal beat:

- The Gorgon learns that preservation can become a prison even without malice.
- Somnarch dream law and Gorgon fixation interact narratively without sharing mechanics.
- A class-specific encounter requires releasing some victims while stabilizing others long enough to escape.

### ACT-04 — The Reliquary March: Saint Veyra, the Preserved Witness

The Procession carries a saint whose gaze fossilizes identity rather than flesh. Those seen by the saint can no longer change allegiance, belief, name, or memory.

Personal beat:

- The Gorgon confronts an idealized version of their power used as religious certainty.
- The Procession offers elevation into an immortal judicial relic.
- This is the class's central temptation: perfect preservation in exchange for becoming an instrument.

### ACT-05 — The Inverted Sea: Salt That Remembers Shape

Abyssal pressure produces salt formations containing snapshots of drowned bodies, cities, and extinct sea life.

Personal beat:

- The Gorgon discovers that the arrest principle can preserve patterns without preserving suffering.
- Tidewrought integration emphasizes pressure and oceanic transformation, while the Gorgon governs state fixation.
- Class-specific choices shape later dialogue but do not create a separate campaign ending.

### ACT-06 — Definition: The Last Molt

Inside the Hour Without End, alternate versions of the Gorgon appear: trophy, tyrant, guardian, failed weapon, and motionless immortal.

Definition beat:

- Reject permanent identity as the price of stability.
- Rewrite the arrest principle so selected structures can endure without being denied future change.
- Preserve one truth while allowing the self to molt beyond it.

This remains a personal resolution inside the shared campaign climax.

## 5. Visual identity and silhouette

### Silhouette rules

The Gorgon should read instantly through:

- a low, forward hunting posture rather than upright regal posing;
- asymmetric scale fields spreading from jaw, spine, shoulders, and forearms;
- a crown-like arrangement of sensory tendrils, quills, or ossified filaments rather than literal hair-snakes;
- one visibly reinforced arm or shoulder used to brace, parry, or expose the gaze;
- hooked weapons carried close to the body;
- a cloak, mantle, or trailing shed skin that records movement;
- mineral growth concentrated at joints and along damaged tissue;
- reflective surfaces embedded into armor to redirect line of sight.

### Material language

- wet scales becoming matte stone at thresholds;
- bruised flesh under translucent mineral plates;
- venom as dark amber, black-green, pale gold, or owner-selected accent rather than neon slime;
- obsidian, salt crystal, fossil ivory, corroded bronze, preserved sinew;
- shed skins that look used, torn, and anatomical rather than decorative fabric.

### Animation language

- controlled stillness before explosive movement;
- head and shoulder orientation clearly communicates gaze direction;
- dodges resemble coiling, shedding, or abrupt lateral breaks;
- petrification appears as progressive structural locking, not instant gray recoloring;
- transformations preserve recognizable weapon and stance language.

### Readability rule

The player must always understand:

- which enemies are Exposed;
- who is currently Fixed;
- who is near a Petrification threshold;
- which direction the active gaze or reflection is operating;
- when a target is immune to hard control but still affected by pressure and damage amplification.

## 6. Combat role

### Primary role

Mobile control-hunter and execution specialist.

### Secondary roles

- damage-over-time pressure;
- close-range hybrid weapon combat;
- pack shaping through directional threat;
- conditional defense through mineralization;
- elite and boss debuff maintenance;
- threshold cash-out burst.

### Intended strengths

- excellent control of dangerous priority targets;
- strong damage against enemies that pursue, face, or remain near the Gorgon;
- high build variety between venom, shatter, counter, and transformation play;
- strong positional skill expression;
- reliable boss contribution without relying on full petrification;
- access to temporary durability through mineralized tissue;
- powerful conversion of enemy aggression into resources.

### Intended weaknesses

- lower baseline damage before prey is prepared;
- poor efficiency when marks are spread thoughtlessly;
- directional mechanics demand awareness;
- overcommitting to mineralization can reduce mobility;
- threshold cash-outs can leave the class temporarily exposed;
- weaker against scattered enemies until equipped for trail or reflection play;
- limited unconditional recovery.

## 8. Unique resource — Ichor

**Ichor** represents catalytic venom, shed tissue, and structural information harvested from prey.

### Generation

Ichor is generated by:

- applying Exposed to a new target;
- crossing Venom thresholds;
- dodging through or around an attacking enemy;
- breaking mineralized tissue;
- harvesting a defeated marked enemy;
- receiving a telegraphed hit while partially mineralized;
- maintaining pressure on an elite or boss.

### Spending

Ichor powers:

- advanced gaze commands;
- rapid venom detonation;
- Molt movement skills;
- temporary body-state changes;
- emergency mineralization;
- high-impact executions.

### Resource behavior

- Ichor has a moderate cap.
- It should be earned through active hunt behavior, not passive regeneration.
- It decays slowly outside combat but never vanishes instantly between nearby packs.
- Certain builds may bank Ichor inside Shed Skins, Fixed targets, or mineral plates.
- Boss encounters provide steady Ichor through pressure milestones even when adds are absent.

### Failure safeguard

A player who spends poorly should still recover through basic Expose and Venom interactions. The class must not enter a dead state where it has no resource and no meaningful action.

## 9. Signature system — Exposure and Fixation

### 9.1 Exposed

**Exposed** is a persistent prey designation showing that the Gorgon has learned a target's movement, chemistry, and structural weakness.

Targets become Exposed through:

- direct gaze contact;
- close-range weapon hits from the flank or front;
- crossing a Shed Trail;
- striking a reflected gaze surface;
- becoming poisoned by a Gorgon skill;
- attacking the Gorgon during a prepared counter window.

Exposed targets:

- generate more Ichor;
- reveal directional weakness cues;
- accumulate Venom and Fixation more efficiently;
- remain readable through clutter;
- interact with execution and harvest skills.

Exposed is not a generic damage vulnerability by default. Its primary purpose is enabling the class kit.

### 9.2 Fixation

**Fixation** is the degree to which a target is being forced to remain in its current state.

Fixation builds through:

- sustained gaze;
- repeated attacks from controlled angles;
- venom threshold crossings;
- forced facing or reflection skills;
- remaining inside mineral fields;
- attempting high-commitment attacks against the Gorgon.

At thresholds, targets suffer **Lock States**.

#### Lock I — Hesitation

- brief action-speed pressure;
- clearer weak-point cue;
- no hard control.

#### Lock II — Seizure

- interrupted movement or reduced turning;
- stronger venom and shatter interactions;
- elites receive reduced duration but full mechanical tag.

#### Lock III — Petrified

- normal enemies become temporarily immobilized and structurally mineralized;
- elites receive a short partial lock and Breakable Carapace;
- bosses never become fully immobilized, but enter **Monument Pressure**.

### 9.3 Monument Pressure — boss compatibility

Bosses and control-immune enemies convert Petrification thresholds into Monument Pressure.

Monument Pressure can:

- slow specific recovery windows rather than core animation speed;
- increase weak-point damage;
- cause the next committed attack to shed Breakable Carapace;
- amplify venom conversion;
- enable execution-style skills without instant kills;
- temporarily stabilize the boss's location for telegraph readability without preventing mechanics.

This ensures the class works in every major encounter.

### 9.4 Breakable Carapace

Petrified enemies and pressured bosses form mineral plates. Breaking plates:

- deals physical or mixed damage;
- generates Ichor;
- may spread shards or venom;
- removes some Fixation;
- creates a meaningful choice between preserving control and cashing out damage.

### 9.5 Gaze rules

Gaze must not require pixel-perfect facing.

- Use a forgiving cone.
- Support controller aim assist and target lock.
- Provide an accessibility option for stronger facing assistance.
- Reflections and area skills can maintain Fixation without direct facing.
- Enemies without conventional eyes remain valid because gaze acts on structural recognition, not visual anatomy.

## 10. Secondary system — Venom Families

The Gorgon uses one core Venom status with build-defining expressions rather than many unrelated poisons.

### VEN-01 — Black Ichor

- sustained damage;
- Ichor generation at thresholds;
- baseline family.

### VEN-02 — Calcifying Venom

- converts part of Venom buildup into Fixation;
- lower raw damage;
- favors control and shatter.

### VEN-03 — Fever Venom

- causes marked enemies to act more aggressively or commit harder;
- increases danger and counter opportunities;
- cannot override boss scripting.

### VEN-04 — Grave Antivenom

- consumes Venom to heal, cleanse, or reinforce the Gorgon;
- defensive conversion family;
- requires deliberate harvest timing.

### VEN-05 — Mirror Venom

- causes affected targets to relay gaze or reflect certain line attacks;
- advanced positioning family.

A build may specialize deeply, but enemies should not need five separate status icons. The UI uses one Venom meter with a clear family modifier.

## 11. Weapons and equipment identity

### Supported weapon families

#### Hook Blades

Fast close-range weapons emphasizing flank hits, counters, and Venom application.

#### Glaives and Forked Polearms

Longer reach, directional control, drag, pin, and mineral-shatter play.

#### Ritual Bows

Short-to-mid-range hunting tools used for tethering, reflecting, or exposing—not off-screen sniping.

#### Claw Gauntlets

Aggressive Ichor generation and body-state interaction.

#### Mirror Shields

Defensive and control-focused gear enabling reflected gaze, parry windows, and fixed angles.

### Proposed class-specific slot — Shed Mantle

A **Shed Mantle** stores one body-state modifier and alters Molt behavior.

Examples:

- leave a decoy skin;
- preserve current Venom family during transformation;
- gain movement through mineral enemies;
- bank excess Ichor;
- turn Molt into a damaging rupture.

Whether every class receives a dedicated relic-style slot remains an owner-level shared systems decision. If the project rejects class-specific slots, Shed Mantle functions migrate to class-only uniques or passive nodes.

## 12. Movement identity

The Gorgon moves through abrupt lateral breaks, coils, vaults, and skin-shedding displacements.

### Baseline evade — Serpent Break

- fast lateral or diagonal dodge;
- gains bonus Ichor when crossing an enemy's attack line;
- briefly widens the gaze cone after a successful near-miss;
- must remain collision-safe and readable.

### Movement rules

- no unrestricted permanent invisibility;
- no teleport spam that erases positional commitment;
- movement should reward crossing lines of danger;
- transformations modify movement but do not replace all base controls;
- the class can escape body-blocking through Molt tools but pays Ichor or cooldown.

## 13. Foundational active skill kit

The following names and values are proposed. IDs should remain stable even if names change.

### GOR-A01 — Hunter's Regard

**Type:** Gaze, Mark

Project a controlled gaze cone that applies Exposed and builds Fixation. Releasing after a short hold sends a narrow pulse through the highest-Fixation target.

Build hooks:

- mobile channel;
- instant counter gaze;
- reflected cone;
- venom conversion;
- single-target beam variant.

### GOR-A02 — Forked Mercy

**Type:** Weapon, Melee, Execution

A two-part hooked strike. First hit pulls or turns lesser enemies; second hit deals bonus damage to Fixed or poisoned targets.

Build hooks:

- polearm sweep;
- dual-blade rapid chain;
- plate shatter;
- Ichor refund on execution.

### GOR-A03 — Calcifying Spit

**Type:** Projectile, Venom

Launch a short-to-mid-range venom bolt that splashes on impact and applies Calcifying Venom.

Build hooks:

- fan spread;
- bouncing mirror shot;
- ground pool;
- converts to physical shard projectile after plate break.

### GOR-A04 — Shed Trail

**Type:** Movement, Zone

Dash and leave behind a strip of discarded skin. Enemies crossing it become Exposed and poisoned. The trail records the Gorgon's facing direction for reflection effects.

Build hooks:

- decoy skin;
- explosive shed;
- healing molt;
- multiple short trails;
- trail becomes mineral barricade.

### GOR-A05 — Unblinking Counter

**Type:** Counter, Guard, Gaze

Enter a brief prepared stance. A frontal hit partially mineralizes the Gorgon, negates or reduces damage, and violently applies Fixation to the attacker.

Build hooks:

- perfect counter shatter;
- all-angle reduced version;
- venom retaliation;
- prolonged bastion stance;
- boss weak-point exposure.

### GOR-A06 — Quarry Lash

**Type:** Weapon, Tether

Throw a hooked chain, tongue-blade, or weapon tether that binds an Exposed target. Recast pulls the Gorgon, pulls lesser prey, or tightens Fixation depending on relative mass.

Build hooks:

- chain multiple prey;
- leave mineral anchor;
- consume Venom for rupture;
- mobility-focused recast.

### GOR-A07 — Fossil Bloom

**Type:** Area, Mineral, Shatter

Cause mineral growths to erupt from Fixed enemies and nearby terrain. Existing plates grow larger; subsequent weapon hits can break them for area damage.

Build hooks:

- defensive cover;
- shard shotgun;
- delayed chain bloom;
- preserve corpses as traps;
- Monument Pressure scaling.

### GOR-A08 — Venom Communion

**Type:** Harvest, Sustain

Consume part of the Venom on nearby Exposed enemies. Gain Ichor and a benefit based on active Venom family.

Build hooks:

- healing antivenom;
- attack-speed fever;
- armor from calcification;
- spread remaining venom;
- execute low-health normal enemies.

### GOR-A09 — Mirror of the First Crime

**Type:** Construct, Reflection

Plant or raise a reflective mineral plane. Gaze and selected projectiles redirect through it. Enemies seeing their reflection gain Fixation.

Build hooks:

- rotating mirror;
- mirror shield follow mode;
- shatter into blades;
- multiple weaker mirrors;
- reflect enemy telegraphs visually without returning prohibited boss attacks.

### GOR-A10 — Basilisk Rush

**Type:** Transformation, Movement, Melee

Partially transform and charge in a low predatory stance, trampling lesser enemies and carving a line through elites. Ends with a braced gaze burst.

Build hooks:

- steerable charge;
- leap finisher;
- plate armor;
- trail of venom;
- repeated short rushes.

### GOR-A11 — Preserve the Wound

**Type:** Curse, Control

Fix an enemy's current injuries in place. For a duration, a portion of damage dealt is recorded and reapplied when the effect ends or the target's plate breaks.

Build hooks:

- DoT preservation;
- party-safe damage ledger;
- early manual detonation;
- defensive version on self;
- boss-focused single target.

### GOR-A12 — Crown of Shed Teeth

**Type:** Aura, Offensive Stance

Manifest a ring of sensory quills and reflected fangs. Nearby Exposed enemies build Fixation when they face, target, or attack the Gorgon.

Build hooks:

- taunt-like aggression without hard AI override;
- ranged reflection links;
- defensive scale crown;
- venom aura;
- execute pulse on threshold crossings.

## 14. Ultimate — The World Holds Its Breath

### GOR-U01 — The World Holds Its Breath

The Gorgon fully awakens the arrest organ for a brief duration.

Effects:

- all Exposed enemies become linked to the Gorgon's gaze network;
- Fixation builds from movement, attacks, and Venom ticks;
- normal enemies reaching threshold Petrify immediately;
- elites form repeated Breakable Carapace layers;
- bosses gain accelerated Monument Pressure windows;
- the Gorgon gains partial mineral armor but retains movement;
- breaking plates extends the duration up to a cap;
- ending the ultimate triggers a controllable choice: preserve remaining control or shatter all active plates.

### Ultimate design rule

The ultimate amplifies the core loop rather than replacing it with a disconnected monster form. Weapon choice, Venom family, mirrors, and build nodes remain relevant throughout.

## 15. Class progression paths

### PATH-GOR-01 — Basilisk Butcher

**Fantasy:** Aggressive close-range hunter using hooks, rushes, Fever Venom, and brutal plate shatter.

Focus:

- weapon combos;
- pursuit;
- forced enemy commitment;
- execution;
- Ichor through risk;
- repeated partial transformation.

Risks:

- highest exposure to melee danger;
- can overconsume Fixation before control pays off.

Signature upgrades:

- successful near-miss dodges grant Feral Ichor;
- Fever Venom causes heavier attacks from marked prey;
- breaking a plate resets part of Basilisk Rush;
- executions leave a predatory aura on the next target.

### PATH-GOR-02 — Monument Warden

**Fantasy:** Deliberate control bruiser who mineralizes self, enemies, and terrain.

Focus:

- Unblinking Counter;
- Mirror Shields;
- defensive plates;
- area denial;
- boss pressure;
- controlled shatter timing.

Risks:

- slower clear without good setup;
- excessive self-mineralization can reduce mobility.

Signature upgrades:

- self-plates store prevented damage;
- mirrors count as mineral terrain;
- fixed enemies grant Guard to nearby allies;
- shattering self-plates emits directional retaliation.

### PATH-GOR-03 — Venom Archivist

**Fantasy:** Alchemical predator who records enemy chemistry and changes venom purpose mid-fight.

Focus:

- Venom family swapping;
- DoT;
- harvesting;
- sustain;
- spreading structural information;
- elite adaptation.

Risks:

- lower immediate burst;
- family management can create cognitive load.

Signature upgrades:

- first threshold reveals an enemy-specific Archive Trait;
- harvested Venom can be stored for the next pack;
- changing families preserves a percentage of buildup;
- boss phases can unlock temporary antivenom benefits.

### PATH-GOR-04 — Mirror Tyrant

**Fantasy:** A battlefield sovereign using redirected sight, reflected geometry, and controlled exposure.

Focus:

- mirrors;
- gaze relays;
- directional fields;
- ranged utility;
- pack shaping;
- high mastery positioning.

Risks:

- dependent on placement quality;
- weaker when mirrors are deployed thoughtlessly.

Signature upgrades:

- mirrors inherit active Venom family;
- gaze can fork at reduced strength;
- enemies between mirrors build Fixation while moving;
- shattered mirrors become temporary cutting lanes.

## 17. Proposed keystones

### KEY-GOR-01 — Mercy Is Movement

Moving through an enemy's attack line grants major Ichor and Exposed, but standing still reduces Ichor generation.

### KEY-GOR-02 — No Statue Lasts Forever

Petrification duration is reduced, but shattering a target deals greatly increased damage and spreads Fixation.

### KEY-GOR-03 — The Witness Becomes the Crime

Enemies facing the Gorgon build Fixation rapidly; enemies attacking from behind deal increased damage to the Gorgon.

### KEY-GOR-04 — Venom Has Memory

Venom consumed from one target is partially transferred to the next newly Exposed target. Direct Venom application is weaker.

### KEY-GOR-05 — Flesh of the Courthouse

Self-mineralization grants major Armor and Guard, but each plate reduces movement speed until shattered or shed.

### KEY-GOR-06 — Refuse the Final Shape

Reaching maximum self-mineralization triggers an empowered Molt instead of immobilizing the Gorgon. Molt consumes all stored Ichor.

### KEY-GOR-07 — One Perfect Specimen

Only one enemy can be Exposed at a time. That target receives greatly amplified Fixation, Venom, and weak-point effects.

### KEY-GOR-08 — A Garden of Witnesses

Exposed enemies relay gaze to nearby enemies, but the Gorgon's direct gaze is weaker.

## 18. Endgame build archetypes

### BUILD-GOR-01 — Basilisk Redline

**Core:** Hook Blades, Fever Venom, Basilisk Rush, Crown of Shed Teeth.

Loop:

1. Expose a dangerous target.
2. Encourage or exploit its committed attack.
3. Serpent Break through the line.
4. Rush back through the pack.
5. Shatter plates with Forked Mercy.
6. Chain executions into the next target.

Identity: fastest, riskiest melee build.

### BUILD-GOR-02 — Monument Engine

**Core:** Mirror Shield, Unblinking Counter, Fossil Bloom, Preserve the Wound.

Loop:

1. Brace against a major telegraph.
2. Generate self and enemy plates.
3. preserve accumulated damage;
4. selectively break enemy carapace;
5. shed self-plates for retaliation and recovery.

Identity: counter-bruiser with deliberate burst windows.

### BUILD-GOR-03 — Black Ichor Plague Hunt

**Core:** Calcifying Spit, Venom Communion, Shed Trail, Venom Has Memory.

Loop:

1. seed Venom across a pack;
2. cross thresholds;
3. harvest the weakest target;
4. transfer stored Venom forward;
5. use Molt to maintain momentum.

Identity: persistent DoT and pack propagation without overlapping Plaguebringer's disease ecology.

### BUILD-GOR-04 — Hall of a Thousand Eyes

**Core:** Mirror of the First Crime, Hunter's Regard, Mirror Venom, Fossil Bloom.

Loop:

1. establish reflective geometry;
2. redirect gaze through multiple lanes;
3. force movement across Fixation lines;
4. shatter mirrors and plates together;
5. rebuild the hall around the next threat.

Identity: highest positional mastery build.

### BUILD-GOR-05 — Grave Antivenom Warden

**Core:** Monument Warden, Grave Antivenom, Venom Communion, Unblinking Counter.

Loop:

1. absorb danger into plates;
2. apply defensive Venom;
3. harvest at controlled timings;
4. heal or cleanse through prey chemistry;
5. retain enough Fixation for boss pressure.

Identity: durable solo and co-op protector without becoming mandatory support.

### BUILD-GOR-06 — The Single Specimen

**Core:** One Perfect Specimen, Quarry Lash, Preserve the Wound, Forked Mercy.

Loop:

1. designate one elite or boss;
2. maintain tether and directional pressure;
3. store damage in the wound;
4. time Monument Pressure and plate break;
5. release a massive controlled execution window.

Identity: dedicated boss hunter.

### UNI-GOR-05 — The Last Antivenom

**Slot:** Belt

- Venom Communion can target the Gorgon.
- Consumes self-applied Venom to heal and grant temporary immunity to the consumed damage family.
- Enemy Venom generation is reduced.

Transforms the class into self-poison resource management.

### UNI-GOR-06 — Crown of Thirty-Seven Verdicts

**Slot:** Helm

- Crown of Shed Teeth marks nearby attackers with individual Verdicts.
- Each distinct attacker increases counter damage.
- Repeating attacks from the same target grant less benefit.

Transforms pack aggression into escalating retaliation.

### UNI-GOR-07 — The Courthouse Molt

**Slot:** Boots

- Serpent Break leaves all self-mineralization behind as a stationary shell.
- Shells block lesser enemies and can be shattered.
- Evade cooldown increases.

Transforms defensive plates into terrain control.

### UNI-GOR-08 — Hook of the Preserved Wound

**Slot:** Polearm

- Quarry Lash attaches Preserve the Wound automatically.
- Recast no longer pulls either target.
- Recast instead replays a portion of recorded movement as tearing damage.

Transforms tether into a boss-damage ledger.

### UNI-GOR-09 — Black Transit Looking Glass

**Slot:** Off-hand

- Mirrors become paired portals for gaze and approved Gorgon projectiles only.
- The Gorgon may Serpent Break through a mirror pair once before they shatter.
- Does not grant general Voidbringer portal functionality.

Transforms reflection into limited route planning while protecting class boundaries.

### UNI-GOR-10 — Fossil of the Self Not Chosen

**Slot:** Class relic or Ring

- The World Holds Its Breath creates an alternate mineral copy of the Gorgon at activation.
- Recast swaps current Life percentage, position, and plate state with the copy.
- The copy cannot attack independently.

Transforms the ultimate into temporal-feeling state exchange without using Anachron mechanics.

## 20. Gear interaction rules

### Preferred affix families

- Ichor generation from counters, thresholds, or plate breaks;
- Fixation rate under specific conditions;
- Venom threshold effectiveness;
- plate health and shatter damage;
- gaze cone, relay count, or reflection efficiency;
- Molt cooldown and trail behavior;
- Exposed target bonuses;
- self-mineralization conversion;
- weapon-specific follow-ups;
- Monument Pressure effectiveness within boss-safe caps.

### Affix restrictions

- no generic affix should provide permanent full Petrification;
- hard-control duration must have elite and boss conversion rules;
- reflection may not return arbitrary boss mechanics;
- taunt-like effects may influence targeting weights but cannot break scripted encounters;
- repeated trigger loops require internal cooldowns or trigger budgets;
- plate shatter must not recursively create infinite plates;
- Venom transfer must preserve damage ownership and multiplayer attribution.

### Damage conversion rules

Supported build directions may convert:

- Venom damage into physical shatter;
- prevented damage into plate retaliation;
- excess Fixation into critical or weak-point pressure;
- Ichor spending into temporary self-poison;
- mineral damage into defensive Guard;
- weapon hits into gaze pulses.

Conversions should create tradeoffs rather than free multiplication.

## 21. Solo and co-op identity

### Solo

The Gorgon must be fully self-sufficient in marking, controlling, harvesting, and executing prey. No core mechanic depends on allies facing enemies or supplying status effects.

### Co-op contribution

The Gorgon offers:

- readable priority-target designation;
- temporary control on dangerous normal enemies;
- boss weak-point and Monument Pressure windows;
- mineral cover or collision-safe terrain where supported;
- damage storage and coordinated plate-break moments;
- optional defensive antivenom effects.

### Co-op safeguards

- allies should not accidentally consume Gorgon Fixation unless the build explicitly enables shared shatter;
- plate visuals must not obscure enemy telegraphs;
- Preserve the Wound records only eligible damage with clear ownership rules;
- multiple Gorgons track Exposure independently but may share a capped public Monument Pressure layer;
- one Gorgon cannot permanently lock a target for the party.

### Same-class interaction

Proposed rule:

- personal Exposed and Venom data;
- target-level public Petrification immunity timer;
- shared visual shell with owner-colored or symbol-coded internal layers;
- each Gorgon can break only their own plates by default;
- special co-op nodes may permit intentional shared shatter.

## 22. Mastery curve

### First ten minutes

Player learns:

- Hunter's Regard marks prey;
- poisoned or marked enemies are easier to control;
- Forked Mercy hits harder after setup;
- Serpent Break rewards crossing danger.

### Early campaign

Player learns:

- Fixation thresholds;
- preserving versus breaking plates;
- one Venom family;
- basic Ichor economy.

### Mid campaign

Player adds:

- counters;
- reflections;
- body-state tradeoffs;
- target priority;
- boss Monument Pressure.

### Endgame mastery

Advanced play includes:

- deliberately holding targets below Petrification;
- pre-positioning mirrors before enemy commitment;
- swapping Venom family without wasting thresholds;
- breaking only selected plates;
- using movement to manipulate facing;
- storing Ichor across pack transitions;
- coordinating damage ledgers and boss windows;
- choosing when preservation is more valuable than burst.

The mastery ceiling comes from decisions and sequencing, not from excessive button count.

## 24. Technical implementation risks

### RISK-GOR-01 — Facing and gaze precision

Risk: camera angle, controller aim, and enemy orientation make gaze unreliable.

Mitigation:

- forgiving cones;
- lock-on support;
- orientation smoothing;
- accessibility assist;
- alternate reflection and zone methods.

### RISK-GOR-02 — Control immunity invalidates the class

Risk: elites and bosses ignore the signature mechanic.

Mitigation:

- Monument Pressure;
- plate generation;
- weak-point windows;
- guaranteed Ichor milestones;
- no build relies solely on immobilization.

### RISK-GOR-03 — Status and plate state explosion

Risk: every enemy tracks too many timers and shell objects.

Mitigation:

- one Venom meter with family metadata;
- threshold events rather than continuous per-frame checks where possible;
- pooled plate visuals;
- aggregate pack behavior for minor enemies;
- strict shell caps.

### RISK-GOR-04 — Mirror recursion

Risk: projectiles or gaze effects bounce indefinitely.

Mitigation:

- explicit relay count;
- immutable cast lineage IDs;
- no reflection of already reflected events beyond budget;
- deterministic ordering.

### RISK-GOR-05 — Counter abuse

Risk: frequent weak attacks produce excessive Ichor or invulnerability.

Mitigation:

- telegraph weight classes;
- per-attacker cooldowns;
- perfect versus partial counter distinction;
- no full immunity outside narrow timing.

### RISK-GOR-06 — Terrain obstruction

Risk: mineral shells block navigation or hide telegraphs.

Mitigation:

- collision only where explicitly designed;
- boss arenas use nonblocking shells by default;
- automatic shell decay;
- ally opacity controls;
- nav-safe placement validation.

### RISK-GOR-07 — Damage ledger exploits

Risk: Preserve the Wound duplicates prohibited damage, snapshots buffs incorrectly, or scales recursively.

Mitigation:

- record post-mitigation eligible damage;
- fixed attribution;
- no self-recording of replay damage;
- cap by target category;
- deterministic expiration.

### RISK-GOR-08 — Venom overlap with Plaguebringer

Risk: reserved Plaguebringer loses identity.

Mitigation:

- Gorgon Venom is intimate, prey-specific, and tied to Fixation;
- no epidemic simulation, corpse ecology, disease mutation, or autonomous spreading plague;
- transfers are deliberate and capped.

### RISK-GOR-09 — Transformation scope

Risk: partial forms require duplicate animation and equipment sets.

Mitigation:

- transformations layer onto the base skeleton;
- preserve weapon rig and core locomotion;
- use modular appendages and material changes;
- prototype one partial form before broader variants.

### RISK-GOR-10 — Multiplayer state ownership

Risk: competing Exposure, Venom, and plates create ambiguity.

Mitigation:

- personal ownership data;
- shared capped public boss layer;
- explicit break permissions;
- consistent UI symbols.

### RISK-GOR-11 — Off-screen control play

Risk: mirror and projectile builds become passive artillery.

Mitigation:

- limited mirror range;
- Ichor generation favors proximity and danger crossing;
- gaze relay loses power with distance;
- executions require engagement.

### RISK-GOR-12 — Visual monotony

Risk: every effect becomes gray stone and green poison.

Mitigation:

- mineral materials vary by region, venom family, and build;
- use fossil, salt, obsidian, bronze, amber, and flesh states;
- preserve strong weapon-impact language.

## 25. Non-negotiable design rules

1. The Gorgon is a hunter-controller, not a generic poison rogue.
2. Petrification is a threshold decision, not the only source of power.
3. Bosses remain fully valid targets through Monument Pressure.
4. The class must work against enemies without conventional eyes.
5. Gaze uses forgiving directional logic.
6. Exposure enables the kit rather than serving as a generic vulnerability icon.
7. Fixation creates a choice between control and shatter.
8. Venom remains prey-specific and deliberate, preserving Plaguebringer space.
9. Transformations amplify the base kit rather than replacing it.
10. Mirrors use deterministic relay budgets.
11. The Gorgon cannot permanently hard-lock elites or bosses.
12. Weapon combat remains central in multiple viable builds.
13. Movement rewards crossing danger rather than fleeing indefinitely.
14. Mineralization must have both offensive and defensive meanings.
15. Self-mineralization always includes a cost or release decision.
16. The class is brutal and bodily, not glamorous or seductive by default.
17. Every core mechanic has readable non-color cues.
18. Co-op utility is valuable but never mandatory.
19. Unique items change loops rather than adding flat damage only.
20. No class-specific story branch creates a separate campaign universe.
21. Unapproved narrative choices remain proposed.
22. Optional run modifiers remain outside this core bible.

## 26. Cross-class consistency audit

### Voidbringer

Potential overlap: spatial control and reflective route tricks.

Boundary:

- Voidbringer bends gravity, portals, and pressure.
- Gorgon controls exposure, state fixation, venom, and reflected sight.
- Black Transit Looking Glass permits only narrow Gorgon-specific routing and never general portal mastery.

### Penitent

Potential overlap: battlefield rules and judgment themes.

Boundary:

- Penitent writes ritual law through Brands, Circles, and Fervor.
- Gorgon imposes bodily and structural arrest through gaze, venom, and mineralization.
- Gorgon Verdict language should remain target pressure, not formal Rite Pattern grammar.

### Graftborn

Potential overlap: body horror and biological transformation.

Boundary:

- Graftborn authors anatomy through Biomass, Body Regions, Adaptations, and Trauma Imprints.
- Gorgon cycles predatory states and forces existing forms to hold.
- Gorgon transformations are limited hunt states, not body-region loadout reconstruction.

### Somnarch

Potential overlap: territorial control and Witness concepts.

Boundary:

- Somnarch overlays dream sovereignty and clauses.
- Gorgon uses physical sight lines, mirrors, and mineral fields.
- Gorgon does not rewrite reality through dream law.

### Relic Host

Potential overlap: preserved identity and inherited memory.

Boundary:

- Relic Host negotiates with dead intelligences and Presences.
- Gorgon preserves states and identity patterns without hosting autonomous dead minds.
- Saint Veyra remains a narrative foil, not a Presence system.

### Tidewrought

Potential overlap: pressure, salt, and bodily transformation.

Boundary:

- Tidewrought governs current, depth, pressure, and abyssal sea states.
- Gorgon uses salt and pressure only as preservation materials tied to arrest.
- Tidewrought should retain broad fluid movement and environmental flow.

### Anachron

Potential overlap: preserving moments and replay-like effects.

Boundary:

- Anachron manipulates sequence, delayed causality, and alternate temporal states.
- Gorgon records damage or bodily state without changing time.
- Fossil copies and wound ledgers must be framed as preserved structure, not time travel.

### Reserved Plaguebringer

Potential overlap: poison and spreading damage.

Boundary:

- Gorgon Venom is deliberate, marked, intimate, and threshold-based.
- Plaguebringer retains epidemic growth, disease ecosystems, corpse propagation, and autonomous contamination.

### Reserved Echo Thief

Potential overlap: reflected actions or repeated effects.

Boundary:

- Gorgon reflects lines and preserves damage records.
- Echo Thief retains appropriation of identities, abilities, sounds, or memories.

### Reserved Choirborn

Potential overlap: attention, facing, and group behavior.

Boundary:

- Gorgon manipulates individual prey relationships.
- Choirborn retains collective resonance, voices, and multi-entity harmony.

### Contradiction result

No direct contradiction found with the campaign framework or completed class bibles. The highest-risk future boundaries are Plaguebringer poison ecology, Graftborn transformation scope, and Anachron state-recording language.

## 28. Recommended first playable prototype slice

Prototype only the smallest loop needed to prove identity.

### Required abilities

- Hunter's Regard;
- Forked Mercy;
- Serpent Break;
- Unblinking Counter;
- Fossil Bloom.

### Required systems

- Ichor resource;
- one Exposed target marker;
- three Fixation thresholds;
- normal-enemy Petrification;
- elite Monument Pressure substitute;
- one Breakable Carapace object/state;
- one Venom family: Calcifying Venom.

### Test enemy set

- slow melee attacker;
- fast multi-hit attacker;
- shielded elite;
- ranged enemy;
- control-resistant boss dummy.

### Validation targets

The slice succeeds when:

1. a new player understands mark, build threshold, and shatter without reading a long tutorial;
2. counter and dodge builds feel different but feed the same loop;
3. Petrification helps without trivializing enemies;
4. the boss dummy remains satisfying despite immunity to hard control;
5. gaze works reliably on controller;
6. preserving a plate and breaking it are both valid decisions;
7. the class feels distinct from Graftborn, Penitent, and a standard poison rogue.

### Explicitly deferred

- multiple mirrors;
- full Venom family swapping;
- multiplayer shared plates;
- all transformation variants;
- ultimate extension logic;
- advanced unique items;
- optional Abyss Siege modifiers.
