# Tidewrought Class Bible

> Status: **PROPOSED — owner review required.**
>
> This document extends the shared campaign and systems framework without changing gameplay code or locking unresolved narrative decisions as canon.

## 1. Class thesis

**The Tidewrought is a pressure-forged amphibious executioner who carries an impossible ocean inside the body and weaponizes current, depth, drowning, and violent phase change.**

The class is not a water mage, pirate, fish-person novelty, or generic frost caster. Its fantasy is the bodily authority of the deep sea: force accumulating with depth, movement becoming current, armor behaving like a pressure hull, wounds flooding from within, and enemies being dragged into positions where the Tidewrought becomes strongest.

A skilled Tidewrought controls combat through a simple escalating loop:

1. **Soak** enemies and terrain with Abyssal Brine.
2. **Build Depth** by remaining committed, taking pressure, moving through threats, or compressing Brine into marked targets.
3. **Shape Current** to gather, redirect, separate, or carry enemies.
4. **Breach** the accumulated pressure through rupture, implosion, undertow, or transformation.
5. **Surface** before the body exceeds safe pressure and converts strength into self-destruction.

The first-minute understanding should be immediate: make enemies Wet, pull them together, build pressure, detonate the pack. Mastery comes from pressure routing, movement lines, deciding when to remain submerged in danger, managing Breach windows, and converting defensive commitment into offensive control.

## 2. Locked identity boundaries

The Tidewrought must not become:

- a conventional elemental water wizard;
- a pirate, sailor, or nautical comedy archetype;
- a generic frost caster with blue particles;
- a permanent giant-monster transformation class;
- a passive thorns tank whose best action is being hit;
- a pure pull-and-group support with weak solo damage;
- a class that requires literal water in the environment;
- a minion class built around fish, drowned sailors, or sea creatures;
- a ranged kiter whose optimal play avoids pressure;
- a copy of Voidbringer gravity control with water dressing;
- a damage-over-time class that overlaps Plaguebringer;
- a stance class with excessive mode maintenance.

The class should feel dense, violent, tidal, bodily, oppressive, and physically committed.

## 3. Lore and origin

### CLS-TID-ORIGIN — The Abyssal Pressure Anchor

During the Binding Epoch, one World-Lattice principle governed **distributed pressure**. The wound below reality could not be closed, so its force had to be carried, redirected, and released across a vast system without allowing any single point to rupture.

The custodians of this principle were remade as living pressure vessels. Their bones developed layered cavities. Their lungs learned to exchange more than air. Their blood could become brine, vapor, or dense black fluid depending on stress. They walked the deepest maintenance trenches beneath the old world and carried catastrophic pressure differences inside their own bodies.

Later civilizations remembered only fragments. Coastal kingdoms called them drowned saints, trench devils, storm-blooded kings, or the first shipwrecked. Some Tidewrought lineages became hereditary harbor weapons. Others were chained beneath seawalls to calm storms. Many were harvested for organs believed to prevent drowning.

The player Tidewrought is a surviving descendant, an intentionally reconstructed pressure bearer, or someone transformed by contact with a failed abyssal anchor. Their body contains an internal sea that should not fit inside human anatomy.

### Personal thematic question

The Tidewrought arc asks:

> Is belonging determined by origin, adaptation, obligation, or the place one chooses to protect?

The class must ultimately decide whether it is a citizen of land, servant of the sea, vessel of the Abyss, or something capable of defining a boundary that none of those powers own.

## 4. Shared campaign role and personal arc

The Tidewrought follows the same campaign timeline as all launch classes.

### ACT-01 — Recognition: Water Beneath the Dead

The Sunken Crypts contain burial chambers flooded from below despite being sealed from every known water source. When the Tidewrought approaches the Hollow Crown, their blood answers with a deep internal tide. The crown-map identifies the class as a surviving expression of the Lattice's pressure-distribution principle.

Personal beat:

- The Tidewrought learns the sea inside them is engineered rather than accidental.
- Hidden channels in the crypt respond to Tidewrought movement and reveal that the Hollow King's node was venting pressure through burial infrastructure.
- Optional encounters show drowned dead preserved by pressure rather than water.

### ACT-02 — The Red Basilica: Confession by Immersion

The Covenant of Measure uses sealed confession cisterns where subjects are submerged in sacramental fluid until guilt is forced into physical form.

Personal beat:

- The Tidewrought encounters victims altered by ritual drowning.
- The Covenant offers to classify the Tidewrought as a sacred instrument rather than a condemned aberration.
- Penitent and Tidewrought lore intersect through ritual pressure, while mechanics remain distinct: written law versus fluid force.

### ACT-03 — The Country Behind Sleep: The Dreaming Floodline

A dead population dreams of the night their city drowned. The remembered water rises each time the dream repeats, but it cannot escape the dream's border.

Personal beat:

- The Tidewrought enters water composed of memory rather than matter.
- Somnarch integration distinguishes dream territory from physical pressure control.
- The class learns that an ocean can be inherited as trauma without existing geographically.

### ACT-04 — The Reliquary March: The Saint in the Diving Bell

The Procession carries a sealed saint whose body has remained under impossible pressure for centuries. The saint's reliquary is used to crush hostile relics into obedience.

Personal beat:

- The Tidewrought recognizes the saint as a failed pressure custodian.
- Relic Host and Tidewrought lore intersect through inherited duty and bodily imprisonment.
- The player may free the saint's identity without destabilizing the shared campaign.

### ACT-05 — The Inverted Sea: The Ninth Tide

This is the Tidewrought's central act. The ocean has risen above the land as a suspended mass. Exposed seabed cities reveal that the old sea concealed a Lattice anchor beneath the world.

The **Ninth Tide** is not merely a monster. It is a migrating pressure intelligence assembled from drowned armies, extinct currents, and damaged anchor behavior.

Personal beat:

- The Drowned Compact identifies the player as a possible vessel for union with the Ninth Tide.
- The player discovers their transformation came from both the sea and the anchor beneath it; neither explanation alone is complete.
- The central temptation offers perfect belonging through dissolution of the individual self into a collective oceanic intelligence.
- The player rejects, negotiates, wounds, or temporarily channels the Ninth Tide without creating a separate campaign branch.

### ACT-06 — Definition: The Shore That Chooses Itself

Inside the Hour Without End, alternate Tidewrought outcomes appear: chained seawall, drowned sovereign, empty pressure suit, weaponized flood, and human body that never transformed.

Definition beat:

- The class rejects the idea that adaptation creates permanent debt to the environment that caused it.
- The pressure principle is rewritten from passive burden into chosen circulation.
- The Tidewrought defines a shore not as the place where sea defeats land, but as a negotiated boundary capable of moving without erasing either side.

This remains a personal resolution inside the shared climax.

## 5. Visual identity and silhouette

### Silhouette rules

The Tidewrought should read instantly through:

- a broad, forward-heavy torso resembling a living pressure hull;
- layered rib plates, valve-like scars, and sealed openings along the neck and flanks;
- one enlarged forearm, shoulder, or weapon housing used as a pressure chamber;
- heavy lower-body stance with feet that appear to grip terrain against current;
- hanging straps, chains, or membrane folds that move as though underwater even on land;
- weapons with dense, blunt profiles rather than decorative tridents;
- controlled leaks of brine, vapor, or black pressure-fluid at high Depth;
- skin or armor compression becoming visible as pressure rises.

### Material language

- salt-crusted iron;
- dark wet leather and compressed membrane;
- whale-bone-like structural ribs without direct real-world animal mimicry;
- abyssal glass formed by pressure;
- corroded bronze valves and anchor hardware;
- bruised flesh beneath translucent pressure sacs;
- pale foam, black brine, and colorless distortion instead of bright blue water magic.

### Animation language

- attacks begin heavy and end with sudden release;
- movement carries momentum like a current rather than weightless dashing;
- high-Depth idle poses show the body bracing against internal force;
- Breach attacks momentarily decompress the silhouette;
- pulls should feel hydraulic and bodily, not telekinetic;
- transformation preserves weapon recognition and player posture.

### Readability rule

The player must always understand:

- current Depth;
- which enemies are Soaked;
- where active Current Lines travel;
- which targets contain compressed Brine;
- whether the next Breach will rupture enemies, terrain, or the player;
- when the class is protected by pressure and when pressure has become dangerous.

## 6. Combat role

### Primary role

Close-to-mid-range pressure bruiser and forced-movement controller.

### Secondary roles

- pack compression and grouping;
- committed frontline durability;
- burst through stored pressure;
- area denial through Brine fields;
- movement-line damage;
- boss stagger and structural pressure;
- conditional self-recovery through controlled surfacing.

### Intended strengths

- exceptional pack gathering without sacrificing solo damage;
- strong damage and defense while maintaining deliberate commitment;
- excellent conversion of movement and incoming force into offense;
- distinct burst windows through Breach;
- meaningful boss contribution through Pressure Fracture rather than hard displacement;
- strong environmental presence without requiring environmental water;
- high build diversity between bruiser, current, implosion, brine, and transformation play.

### Intended weaknesses

- lower damage at zero Depth;
- careless overpressure can create self-stagger or resource collapse;
- poor sequencing can scatter packs instead of compressing them;
- mobility is directional and momentum-based rather than freely corrective;
- ranged enemies require deliberate current routing;
- unconditional healing is limited;
- high-impact skills are weaker when used without setup.

## 8. Unique resource — Depth

### Resource definition

**Depth** represents internal pressure carried by the Tidewrought. It ranges from 0 to 100 unless later tuning supports a different scale.

Depth is not mana and should not primarily regenerate while disengaged.

### Depth generation

Depth is gained by:

- striking Soaked enemies;
- moving through enemies with Current skills;
- blocking, bracing, or taking controlled damage;
- compressing Brine inside targets;
- remaining near multiple hostile bodies;
- traversing active Current Lines;
- specific gear and passive interactions.

### Depth bands

#### DEPTH-00 — Surface: 0–24

- safest state;
- lowest pressure scaling;
- improved recovery and movement correction;
- ideal for setup and repositioning.

#### DEPTH-01 — Descent: 25–49

- moderate damage and Guard gains;
- Current skills gain improved carry force;
- Breach skills become efficient.

#### DEPTH-02 — Crushing: 50–79

- strong damage, stagger, and mitigation;
- Brine compression accelerates;
- movement becomes more committed;
- some skills gain altered forms.

#### DEPTH-03 — Black Depth: 80–100

- maximum power and highest risk;
- the body leaks pressure and receives reduced external healing unless modified;
- Breach skills gain major effects;
- remaining capped too long builds **Hull Strain**.

### Hull Strain

Hull Strain is a short-term danger meter generated while at extreme Depth or by using pressure-forcing skills.

At maximum Hull Strain, the Tidewrought suffers **Forced Surface**:

- Depth rapidly drops;
- Guard temporarily weakens;
- the character is briefly vulnerable but not hard-stunned by default;
- nearby Brine may erupt unpredictably unless protected by build choices.

This is a readable failure state, not a random punishment.

### Resource rule

The best Tidewrought play should involve moving between pressure bands intentionally. Permanent maximum Depth must require a major build commitment with real tradeoffs.

## 9. Signature mechanic — Abyssal Brine and Pressure Routing

### Abyssal Brine

Many Tidewrought skills apply **Soaked** and deposit **Abyssal Brine**.

Brine may exist as:

- a status carried by enemies;
- a field on terrain;
- a compressed charge inside a target;
- a trail left by movement;
- a temporary coating on the Tidewrought.

Brine is not ordinary water. It is pressure-bearing fluid generated by the class's internal sea.

### Soaked

Soaked is the basic class setup state.

Soaked enemies:

- are easier to carry with Current effects;
- accumulate compressed pressure from Tidewrought hits;
- interact with Breach skills;
- remain fully functional against bosses even when displacement is reduced.

Soaked should be visually readable without coating enemies in bright blue gloss.

### Current Lines

Current skills create directional movement paths called **Current Lines**.

A Current Line can:

- move the Tidewrought;
- carry normal enemies;
- bend projectiles where explicitly supported;
- drag Brine fields;
- amplify attacks traveling with the current;
- oppose movement traveling against it.

Current Lines must use collision-safe navigation and cannot pull enemies through solid geometry.

### Compression

Repeated Tidewrought pressure against a Soaked target builds **Compression**.

Compression is target-local and has three proposed stages:

1. **Loaded** — target stores Brine and takes increased Current interaction.
2. **Crushed** — target suffers reduced stability and increased Breach damage.
3. **Fractured** — normal enemies are vulnerable to implosion or rupture; bosses gain Pressure Fracture stacks instead of hard control.

### Boss-safe Pressure Fracture

Bosses and displacement-resistant elites convert forbidden pull, carry, or implosion effects into **Pressure Fracture**.

Pressure Fracture may grant:

- increased stagger damage;
- increased Tidewrought damage from Breach skills;
- partial resistance reduction;
- threshold detonation at a stack cap;
- skill-specific payoff without moving the boss.

This ensures the class remains functional in boss encounters.

### Breach

Breach skills consume some combination of Depth, Brine, Compression, or Hull Strain to release stored pressure.

Breach outcomes include:

- outward rupture;
- inward implosion;
- directional jet;
- protective decompression;
- current inversion;
- temporary transformation.

Breach should be the satisfying punctuation of the class, not every button.

## 10. Weapons and equipment identity

### Supported weapon families

The Tidewrought should support conventional equipment rather than anatomy replacing the entire loot game.

Proposed families:

- heavy cleavers;
- mauls;
- hooked polearms;
- chain anchors;
- pressure gauntlets;
- short brutal spears;
- broad shields or hull plates;
- paired boarding-hook shapes without pirate styling.

### Weapon behavior

Weapons serve as pressure-routing tools:

- heavy weapons increase Compression and Breach force;
- hooked weapons improve carry, pin, and Current shaping;
- shields improve bracing, Depth retention, and protective decompression;
- gauntlets support close-range implosion and body-first movement;
- polearms extend Current Lines and directional control.

### Proposed class-specific equipment slot — Pressure Organ

A **Pressure Organ** is a removable regulator, vestigial organ, valve cage, internal reservoir, or grafted chamber that modifies Depth behavior.

Possible categories:

- regulator;
- ballast heart;
- brine bladder;
- abyssal lung;
- rupture valve;
- current keel.

This slot is proposed and depends on the shared decision about universal class-specific equipment slots.

### Gear rule

No weapon family should be mandatory for the class's core loop. Gear should change how pressure is expressed, not determine whether the class functions.

## 11. Movement identity

The Tidewrought moves like a current with mass.

### Baseline dodge — Undertow Step

A grounded directional surge that:

- gains slightly more distance when moving along an active Current Line;
- can pass through normal enemies only when sufficient Depth is available;
- leaves a brief Brine wake;
- does not ignore walls or collision;
- becomes harder to redirect at Black Depth unless modified.

### Movement principles

- movement should carry force into the next attack;
- the class should create lines rather than teleport;
- dodging through danger may generate Depth but must remain capped;
- movement skills should reposition enemies only when collision and crowd-control rules permit;
- boss immunity converts carry force into Pressure Fracture or stagger.

### Accessibility rule

Current direction must use clear ground arrows, wake orientation, and optional high-contrast indicators. Camera movement should remain stable even during large wave effects.

## 12. Foundational active skill kit

These skills establish vocabulary rather than final production balance.

### TID-SKL-01 — Hullbreaker

A heavy weapon strike that applies Soaked and adds Compression.

- At Crushing Depth, the final hit emits a short pressure cone.
- Against Fractured targets, it performs a small inward rupture.
- Serves as the foundational generator and combo anchor.

### TID-SKL-02 — Undertow Chain

Throw a hook, chain, or compressed tether that drags normal enemies toward a chosen point.

- Collision-respecting.
- Bosses gain Pressure Fracture instead of displacement.
- Recast may pull the Tidewrought toward an anchored heavy target.

### TID-SKL-03 — Brinewake

Drive forward, leaving a Current Line and Brine trail.

- Enemies caught in the wake are carried slightly.
- Following attacks traveling with the wake gain speed and Compression.
- Core movement and topology skill.

### TID-SKL-04 — Pressure Bell

Brace and create a dense field around the Tidewrought.

- Reduces incoming damage for a short window.
- Converts blocked force into Depth.
- Releases a pulse when the brace ends.
- Proper timing avoids excessive Hull Strain.

### TID-SKL-05 — Salt the Wound

Force Brine into a target's injuries.

- Converts Soaked into a persistent pressure reservoir.
- Repeated hits increase Compression.
- Does not use disease, rot, or infection language.

### TID-SKL-06 — Current Shear

Create two crossing currents that slice enemies moving through their intersection.

- Excellent pack shaping.
- Can invert to push outward with path investment.
- Bosses take repeated Pressure Fracture buildup while standing at the crossing.

### TID-SKL-07 — Drown the Air

Create a temporary zone where atmosphere behaves like deep water.

- Slows enemy projectiles and movement within strict caps.
- Increases Soaked application.
- Does not silence or hard-disable enemies.
- Visuals use pressure distortion and suspended debris rather than a literal bubble.

### TID-SKL-08 — Implosion Mark

Mark a Soaked target as a pressure sink.

- Nearby Current Lines bend slightly toward it.
- On expiry or Breach, normal enemies collapse inward.
- Bosses trigger a focused Pressure Fracture detonation.

### TID-SKL-09 — Surface Violence

Rapidly shed Depth in a forward blast.

- Deals damage based on Depth consumed.
- Clears part of Hull Strain.
- May launch normal enemies away or convert to stagger on bosses.
- Main emergency decompression tool.

### TID-SKL-10 — Keelhaul

Rush along a Current Line, dragging captured normal enemies and striking them through terrain-safe contact points.

- Ends in a heavy slam.
- Cannot move enemies through walls.
- Boss version becomes a committed pass that applies Fracture and stagger.

### TID-SKL-11 — Blackwater Mantle

Coat the body in dense abyssal fluid.

- Grants Guard and retaliation pressure.
- Converts some incoming elemental damage into Depth.
- At excessive use, increases Hull Strain.
- Should not become passive thorns gameplay.

### TID-SKL-12 — Trench Maw

Open a compressed rupture beneath a target area.

- Pulls normal enemies toward the center.
- Consumes Brine fields for additional implosion damage.
- Bosses remain stationary and take Pressure Fracture.
- Visual language is a pressure collapse, not a summoned sea monster.

## 13. Ultimate — The Sea Remembers Its Weight

### TID-ULT-01 — The Sea Remembers Its Weight

The Tidewrought opens the internal sea and temporarily becomes a living regional pressure system.

Effects:

- enters a controlled maximum-Depth state without immediately gaining Hull Strain;
- all nearby enemies become Soaked;
- movement creates powerful Current Lines;
- Breach skills may be used repeatedly with modified costs;
- normal enemies are carried by large-scale currents;
- bosses rapidly accumulate Pressure Fracture;
- the Tidewrought's silhouette expands through pressure membranes, armor separation, and internal fluid displacement without becoming an unrelated giant creature.

Finale:

- recasting or expiration triggers **Continental Breach**, collapsing all active Current Lines toward the Tidewrought before releasing them outward;
- the player returns at low Depth rather than remaining permanently transformed;
- skill expression comes from arranging currents and Brine before the finale.

Ultimate rule:

The ultimate amplifies the established kit. It must not replace the class with a separate moveset that makes the base class feel incomplete.

## 16. Proposed keystones

### KEY-TID-01 — No Surface Remains

Depth can no longer fall below 50 during combat.

Tradeoff:

- increased damage and Guard;
- Hull Strain begins earlier;
- external healing is less effective;
- Forced Surface becomes more dangerous.

### KEY-TID-02 — Empty Hull

Depth no longer grants defense.

Benefit:

- every point of Depth grants increased Breach damage and Current speed;
- Surface Violence consumes all Depth for exceptional burst.

### KEY-TID-03 — The Shore Moves

Current Lines slowly drift in their original direction.

Tradeoff:

- stronger arena control;
- lower duration;
- requires active repositioning to maintain useful topology.

### KEY-TID-04 — Pressure Finds the Weak

Compression spreads from a Fractured enemy to the nearest Soaked target.

Tradeoff:

- exceptional pack chaining;
- reduced single-target Compression generation.

### KEY-TID-05 — Drowned Without Water

Soaked becomes permanent on enemies while they remain in combat with the Tidewrought.

Tradeoff:

- Brine applications no longer deal direct damage;
- setup becomes reliable but less explosive.

### KEY-TID-06 — Breach Inward

All outward Breach effects become implosions.

Tradeoff:

- superior grouping and single-point damage;
- loses knockback and emergency space creation.

### KEY-TID-07 — The Ninth Has Room

The Tidewrought may store up to half of current Depth in an active Current Line.

Tradeoff:

- stored Depth is unavailable for personal defense;
- destroying or expiring the line releases the stored pressure.

### KEY-TID-08 — A Body Is a Coastline

Forced Surface no longer weakens Guard.

Tradeoff:

- it ruptures nearby Brine immediately;
- poor positioning can harm control plans and consume setup prematurely.

Enables dangerous overcap builds with staged decompression.

### UNI-TID-04 — Salt Crown of Vey

Type: Helm.

Effect:

- Soaked enemies leave Brine statues or pressure silhouettes when they die.
- Breach skills may consume those silhouettes as if they were living targets.

Build change:

Allows pack setup to persist briefly after enemy death without becoming a minion mechanic.

### UNI-TID-05 — Hull Without a Door

Type: Chest armor.

Effect:

- Forced Surface is disabled.
- At maximum Hull Strain, the Tidewrought instead takes escalating damage and gains escalating Breach power.

Build change:

Creates a true permanent-pressure archetype with severe survival risk.

### UNI-TID-06 — The Dry Drowning

Type: Amulet.

Effect:

- Drown the Air follows the Tidewrought.
- It no longer slows enemies.
- Enemies inside gain Compression whenever they attack.

Build change:

Converts control into aggression-fed boss and bruiser pressure.

### UNI-TID-07 — Current Eater

Type: Heavy cleaver.

Effect:

- Attacking across a Current Line consumes that line.
- The attack inherits its length, stored Depth, and carry force as damage.

Build change:

Turns persistent arena control into single-hit execution.

### UNI-TID-08 — Brineglass Heart

Type: Pressure Organ.

Effect:

- Internal Brine becomes brittle crystal at Black Depth.
- Compression detonations deal immediate shatter damage rather than implosion.
- Current effects lose most pull strength.

Build change:

Creates a direct burst variant that sacrifices grouping.

### UNI-TID-09 — Wake of the Unburied City

Type: Boots.

Effect:

- Brinewake creates parallel Current Lines based on movement speed.
- The Tidewrought cannot turn sharply during the skill.

Build change:

Creates high-speed lane-clearing builds with significant steering commitment.

### UNI-TID-10 — Treaty With the Ninth

Type: Class relic or Pressure Organ.

Effect:

- The Sea Remembers Its Weight no longer ends in Continental Breach.
- Instead, one persistent autonomous current remains after the ultimate.
- Only one may exist, and it follows deterministic path rules rather than pet AI.

Build change:

Trades ultimate burst for sustained regional control without becoming a summoner.

## 19. Gear interactions and affix vocabulary

### Supported affix families

- maximum Depth;
- Depth gained from movement, guarding, or striking Soaked targets;
- Hull Strain threshold;
- Hull Strain decay;
- Soaked duration or persistence;
- Compression generation;
- Pressure Fracture effectiveness;
- Current Line width, length, duration, and intersection power;
- Brine field area and transfer;
- Breach damage and Depth efficiency;
- Guard while at Crushing or Black Depth;
- movement along Current Lines;
- external healing penalty mitigation;
- Surface recovery effects.

### Conversion rules

Gear may convert:

- pull force into stagger;
- Brine duration into Breach damage;
- Guard into Compression;
- movement speed into Current Line length;
- excess healing into Depth reduction;
- Hull Strain into temporary damage;
- outward Breach into inward Breach;
- stored Depth into elemental or physical damage according to shared damage rules.

### Trigger safeguards

- triggered Breaches require internal cooldowns or resource costs;
- Current intersections cannot recursively spawn more intersections;
- enemy movement caused by one Current Line cannot repeatedly retrigger the same contact event without a lockout;
- corpse and death effects must have strict ownership and chain limits;
- boss Pressure Fracture detonations cannot recursively generate full new stacks;
- maximum persistent Current Lines must be capped and clearly displayed.

## 20. Party and co-op identity

The Tidewrought must remain complete in solo play.

### Natural co-op contribution

- gathers normal enemies into allied area attacks;
- creates Current Lines allies can read but do not need to use;
- applies Pressure Fracture to bosses;
- redirects threats and projectiles where supported;
- creates defensive pressure zones;
- controls pack shape without permanently disabling enemies.

### Co-op safeguards

- allies cannot be involuntarily moved by Tidewrought currents by default;
- optional ally movement benefits require explicit opt-in or passive rules;
- excessive enemy dragging must respect crowd-control diminishing returns;
- visual opacity scales down for allies;
- current ownership remains clear when multiple Tidewrought players are present;
- one player's Breach cannot consume another player's Brine unless a specific co-op rule allows it.

### Same-class interaction

Multiple Tidewrought players may overlap Soaked but maintain separate Compression ownership by default.

Proposed shared interaction:

- Current Lines from different players may form a **Confluence** once per interval;
- Confluence grants a capped effect and cannot recursively trigger;
- each player retains control over personal Depth and Brine.

## 21. Mastery curve

### First ten minutes

The player learns:

- Soaked makes skills interact;
- Depth rises through engagement;
- high Depth increases power;
- Surface Violence releases pressure;
- Undertow Chain gathers enemies.

### Early campaign

The player learns:

- Current Lines influence movement and attacks;
- Compression creates Breach payoff;
- Black Depth is powerful but risky;
- guarding can be proactive resource generation.

### Mid campaign

The player learns:

- not every Breach should consume all setup;
- boss Pressure Fracture replaces forbidden displacement;
- current topology matters more than raw area size;
- gear can redefine pressure flow.

### Endgame mastery

Expert play involves:

- entering exact Depth bands before skill sequences;
- routing enemies through multiple current intersections;
- controlling Hull Strain without losing offensive tempo;
- choosing between outward and inward Breach;
- staging boss Fracture detonations around vulnerability windows;
- using movement as both positioning and resource engineering;
- deciding which pressure remains in the body, enemies, or arena.

## 23. Technical implementation risks

### RISK-TID-01 — Forced movement and collision

Pull, carry, and line movement can create clipping, tunneling, or navigation exploits.

Mitigation:

- use validated motion paths;
- stop at collision-safe points;
- convert invalid displacement into stagger or damage;
- test narrow corridors and uneven terrain early.

### RISK-TID-02 — Current Line topology cost

Persistent lines and intersections may become expensive.

Mitigation:

- hard cap active lines;
- use pooled deterministic geometry;
- avoid per-frame navmesh recomputation;
- cache intersections.

### RISK-TID-03 — Recursive trigger chains

Breach, death, Compression spread, and Current intersections can recurse.

Mitigation:

- source IDs;
- generation limits;
- internal cooldowns;
- non-recursive tags;
- deterministic event ordering.

### RISK-TID-04 — Boss invalidation

If displacement is central, bosses may make the class feel broken.

Mitigation:

- Pressure Fracture conversion is mandatory;
- each displacement skill needs an explicit boss result;
- boss payoff must remain competitive without movement.

### RISK-TID-05 — Visual clutter

Brine, wakes, currents, pressure distortion, and implosions may obscure telegraphs.

Mitigation:

- terrain effects remain low and transparent;
- enemy telegraphs render above class fields;
- ally effects use reduced opacity;
- line count remains capped.

### RISK-TID-06 — Water simulation scope

Real fluid simulation is unnecessary and risky.

Mitigation:

- use authored meshes, decals, particles, and vector fields;
- simulate combat rules, not fluid physics;
- visuals follow deterministic gameplay geometry.

### RISK-TID-07 — Resource snowball

Dense packs may generate too much Depth and make bosses feel starved.

Mitigation:

- normalize generation by source category;
- add boss-specific Fracture generation;
- cap gain per event;
- avoid linear scaling with every nearby enemy.

### RISK-TID-08 — Permanent high-Depth dominance

Players may solve the resource by staying capped forever.

Mitigation:

- Hull Strain;
- healing and mobility tradeoffs;
- meaningful Surface bonuses;
- high-Depth builds require keystones or uniques.

### RISK-TID-09 — Control grief in co-op

Excessive pulls can disrupt ally targeting.

Mitigation:

- diminishing returns;
- elite resistance;
- predictable center points;
- ally-facing current indicators;
- configurable effect visibility.

### RISK-TID-10 — State ownership

Multiple players applying Soaked and Compression may create ambiguity.

Mitigation:

- shared basic status, personal payload ownership;
- deterministic UI priority;
- explicit same-class Confluence rules.

### RISK-TID-11 — Movement skill steering

Momentum movement can feel unresponsive.

Mitigation:

- preserve initial commitment while allowing limited correction;
- expose steering values to tuning;
- support controller aim and mouse targeting separately;
- never sacrifice input clarity for animation fidelity.

### RISK-TID-12 — Environmental assumptions

The class may look strange in dry regions.

Mitigation:

- Brine originates from the body;
- pressure effects use distortion, salt, vapor, and debris;
- no skill requires ambient water.

1. The Tidewrought is not a generic water mage.
2. The core fantasy is pressure, current, depth, and bodily containment.
3. The class remains fully functional in dry environments.
4. Soaked is setup, not the entire build identity.
5. Depth must reward engagement and intentional cycling.
6. Black Depth must remain powerful and dangerous.
7. Bosses convert forbidden displacement into Pressure Fracture.
8. Every major pull or carry skill must respect collision.
9. Real fluid simulation is not required.
10. The class cannot become a passive thorns tank.
11. Current Lines must remain visually readable.
12. The base kit must remain meaningful during the ultimate.
13. Transformation cannot replace the class with an unrelated creature.
14. Brine Anatomy must remain distinct from plague, infection, and poison.
15. Movement must feel forceful without becoming uncontrollable.
16. Pack control cannot erase enemy threat indefinitely.
17. Co-op currents cannot involuntarily move allies by default.
18. Persistent progression remains the foundation.
19. No mandatory in-combat upgrade-card interruptions.
20. Seasonal content cannot require character resets.
21. Optional run modifiers remain deferred to modes such as Abyss Siege.
22. Unresolved narrative and system choices remain proposed.
23. The Tidewrought receives a personal arc inside the shared campaign, never a separate campaign.
24. Gear changes pressure expression without being required for baseline function.

## 25. Cross-class consistency audit

### Voidbringer boundary

Potential overlap: displacement and spatial control.

Protected distinction:

- Voidbringer manipulates gravity, portals, absence, and spatial pressure differentials.
- Tidewrought creates physical and metaphysical fluid pressure, momentum, and current.
- Voidbringer repositions through discontinuity; Tidewrought moves along continuous force lines.

### Penitent boundary

Potential overlap: zones and battlefield control.

Protected distinction:

- Penitent inscribes rules through ritual geometry.
- Tidewrought creates directional physical fields through current and pressure.

### Graftborn boundary

Potential overlap: altered body and anatomy.

Protected distinction:

- Graftborn authors biological adaptation and body regions.
- Tidewrought manages a pressure-vessel body whose fundamental anatomy remains recognizable.

### Somnarch boundary

Potential overlap: environmental overlay.

Protected distinction:

- Somnarch imposes dream territory and clauses.
- Tidewrought creates physical pressure topology and Brine fields.

### Relic Host boundary

Potential overlap: inherited custodial burden.

Protected distinction:

- Relic Host negotiates with dead intelligences.
- Tidewrought carries force and an oceanic collective temptation, not a council of personified presences.

### Gorgon boundary

Potential overlap: setup statuses and bodily states.

Protected distinction:

- Gorgon fixes and transforms enemy state through gaze, venom, and mineralization.
- Tidewrought loads and ruptures pressure through Soaked, Compression, and Current.

### Anachron boundary

Potential overlap: stored force and delayed payoff.

Protected distinction:

- Anachron manipulates sequence, timing, and recorded events.
- Tidewrought stores physical pressure in bodies and arena geometry.

### Reserved Plaguebringer boundary

Potential overlap: internal target payload and damage over time.

Protected distinction:

- Plaguebringer should own infection, contagion, rot, ecology, and spread.
- Tidewrought owns Brine, compression, implosion, and pressure rupture.
- Tidewrought Brine does not reproduce, infect, mutate, or behave as disease.

### Reserved Echo Thief boundary

Potential overlap: using enemy force.

Protected distinction:

- Echo Thief appropriates actions, identities, or echoes.
- Tidewrought converts physical aggression into pressure without copying skills.

### Reserved Choirborn boundary

Potential overlap: group resonance.

Protected distinction:

- Choirborn should own voice, chorus, resonance, and collective expression.
- Tidewrought currents are directional force networks rather than sonic harmony.

### Contradiction result

No direct contradiction is identified with the proposed campaign framework or completed launch-class bibles.

The highest-risk implementation boundaries are:

- forced movement versus Voidbringer spatial control;
- Brine payloads versus Plaguebringer infection;
- bodily transformation versus Graftborn adaptation;
- persistent arena fields versus Somnarch territory.

These boundaries are explicit and should be tested during future implementation reviews.
