# Relic Host Class Bible

> Status: **PROPOSED — owner review required.**
>
> Stable class ID: `CLS-REL`
>
> This document defines the Relic Host as a launch class inside the shared AbyssFall campaign. It preserves the locked persistent-ARPG direction and treats all unapproved mechanics, names, values, and lore as proposals.

---

## 1. Class thesis

The Relic Host is not a holy paladin, conventional summoner, necromancer, or generic possessed warrior.

The Relic Host is a **living reliquary carrying several inherited dead intelligences whose unfinished functions can be invoked, negotiated with, layered, and forcibly overruled in combat**.

Its fantasy is weaponized inheritance: carrying the remains of saints, executioners, failed machines, oath-breakers, and extinct custodians inside the body or armor, then deciding which presence receives control, which memories become power, and which dead authority must be denied.

### Player promise

The Relic Host lets the player say:

> “I carry the dead because burial failed. Their miracles are mine to command, but their purpose is not.”

### Core identity pillars

1. **Inherited presences** — each bound relic contains continuity, motive, and a combat function rather than behaving like a disposable pet.
2. **Possession as stancecraft** — the player deliberately grants limited control to one presence at a time, changing attacks, defense, movement, and skill behavior.
3. **Concord versus domination** — power grows through negotiated alignment or violent suppression, with distinct build paths for each philosophy.
4. **Material sanctity** — relic effects emerge from bone, metal, cloth, teeth, bells, engines, seals, and preserved organs rather than abstract holy light.
5. **Persistent buildcraft** — chosen relics, host sockets, class-tree paths, unique-item rules, and saved loadouts define long-term identity.

### Explicit anti-pillars

The Relic Host must not become:

- a clean armored crusader with generic radiant attacks;
- a necromancer whose relics are merely skeleton minions;
- a permanent AI companion-management class;
- a random-proc possession casino;
- a class whose strongest mode removes player control for long periods;
- a simple aura support that depends on co-op;
- a lore-heavy class with weak moment-to-moment combat;
- a single-relic class where every build uses the same saint;
- a visual pile of floating trinkets without clear hierarchy.

## 2. Lore and campaign role

### Origin

**PROPOSED:** During the Binding Epoch, the World-Lattice required living custodians capable of carrying failed components away from collapsing nodes. These custodians became mobile reliquaries. They housed preserved command organs, dead operators, sainted engineers, execution protocols, and fragments of pre-human intelligences inside specially prepared bodies.

The first Hosts were not priests. They were emergency infrastructure.

Over centuries, maintenance practice became religion. The surviving mobile custodians were renamed saints, their procedures became miracles, and their bound components were split among kingdoms as holy remains. The Procession of Last Things eventually formed around a brutal belief: anything sacred left buried would be reclaimed by the Abyss, so all relics must remain in motion.

Modern Relic Hosts arise when a prepared or catastrophically compatible body becomes inhabited by more than one preserved continuity. Some Hosts inherit relics through family duty. Some are forcibly installed. Some awaken after touching an object that has rejected every prior bearer.

The player begins with a fractured **Reliquary Frame** embedded through the sternum, spine, or armor harness. It contains several dormant cavities and one active presence whose identity may be selected as origin flavor without creating separate campaigns.

### What counts as a relic

A relic is not valuable because it was worshipped. A relic is mechanically alive because it retains at least one of the following:

- a preserved command;
- a stable memory pattern;
- a repeated miracle or procedure;
- an unfinished oath;
- an extinct sensory system;
- a dead intelligence capable of preference;
- a machine function that mistakes the Host for its original operator.

Relics may be sacred, criminal, mechanical, anatomical, royal, or pre-human. “Holy” is a social label, not a damage alignment.

### Shared campaign integration

The Relic Host follows the same six-act campaign as every launch class.

#### ACT-01 — Recognition

The Hollow Crown map causes the Host’s active relic to speak in a voice it has never used before. It recognizes the crown not as royal regalia, but as a portable Lattice authorization key.

Personal mission proposal: recover the **Tongue of the Empty Court**, a strip of preserved command tissue hidden beneath the Hollow King’s throne machinery. When installed, it lets the Host understand fragments of old custodian speech but also awakens a second presence.

#### ACT-02 — The Red Basilica

The Covenant of Measure classifies unlicensed possession as stolen sainthood. It seeks to inventory, purify, and legally assign every intelligence inside the Host.

The Host discovers that several celebrated Basilica saints were not divine figures at all, but dismembered Lattice operators whose procedures were converted into religious doctrine.

Personal mission proposal: enter the **Gallery of Approved Miracles**, where relics have been stripped of identity and reduced to repeatable public punishments. The Host can free, silence, or rebind one surviving continuity.

#### ACT-03 — The Country Behind Sleep

Relics dream differently from living minds. A bound presence may preserve a memory so completely that the Quiet Parliament mistakes it for a sovereign territory.

The Host encounters dream copies of former bearers who insist they are the true owners of the body. This act establishes the line between inherited continuity and Somnarch-authored manifestation.

Personal mission proposal: cross the **Dormitory of Former Flesh**, where every previous bearer of one relic appears simultaneously and demands the return of a different body part.

#### ACT-04 — The Reliquary March

This is the Relic Host’s primary personal-arc region.

The Procession of Last Things identifies the player as an illegal, unfinished mobile shrine. Its leaders offer elevation into a **Many-Bodied Saint**, a distributed consciousness housed across weapons, pilgrims, siege engines, and devotional objects.

Saint Orison, the Many-Bodied, claims individuality is merely a temporary storage failure. The player is promised perfect survival through distribution, but would cease to possess a private self.

Personal mission proposal: climb the moving fortress-shrine **Vesper Ossuary**, confront the relic that originally designed the Host lineage, and choose whether to inherit its mandate, rewrite its terms, or break its authority.

#### ACT-05 — The Inverted Sea

The suspended ocean contains relics from drowned civilizations that never became part of terrestrial religion. These objects retain pressure-born memories, extinct languages, and predatory maintenance protocols.

The Ninth Tide attempts to use the Host as a land-compatible reliquary for oceanic intelligence.

Personal mission proposal: extract the **Keel Saint**, a preserved navigation engine worshipped by a city that drowned before its construction was complete. Installing it offers exceptional movement control but creates a route through which the sea can observe the Host.

#### ACT-06 — Definition

The Eightfold Custodian recognizes the Relic Host as portable storage, not a person. It attempts to revoke the Host’s ownership and reclaim every bound continuity as system property.

The Procession offers its final doctrine: no single self deserves priority over thousands of preserved dead. The Host must define whether coexistence means democracy, stewardship, command, sacrifice, or permanent separation.

The defining statement is:

> “Memory grants a voice. It does not grant a body.”

### Personal-arc temptation

The Host can become almost impossible to erase by distributing identity across many relics and bearers. The cost is the death of private continuity: the player survives, but no longer as a singular person.

Late-game mechanics should reflect this temptation through distributed echoes, multiple active presences, and powerful surrender effects without making loss of player agency mandatory.

## 3. Visual language and silhouette

### Default silhouette

The Relic Host should read as a mobile shrine, condemned carrier, and battlefield executor without resembling a polished knight.

- a visible Reliquary Frame entering the sternum, ribs, spine, or shoulder girdle;
- asymmetrical armor assembled around different preserved objects;
- one dominant carried relic with a readable combat silhouette;
- sealed cavities, lock plates, hooks, devotional clamps, and extraction handles;
- cloth strips, labels, chain tags, or legal seals recording prior owners;
- posture that changes subtly depending on the active presence;
- evidence that armor protects the relics as much as the wearer;
- no permanent angel wings, spotless plate, or generic glowing halo.

### Material palette

- blackened iron;
- old bone and tooth enamel;
- tarnished brass;
- funeral cloth;
- dark wax seals;
- oxidized silver;
- dried blood used as a binding medium;
- ceramic saint masks;
- restrained relic-white and funeral gold for active authority;
- class-path accents that remain secondary to material identity.

### Presence animation rules

1. Each presence must have one unmistakable posture, sound, icon, and material response.
2. Activation changes the Host’s center of gravity and attack grammar, not only particle color.
3. Relics may move slightly within clamps, rotate, breathe, vibrate, or attempt escape.
4. Bound presences should not constantly manifest full humanoid ghosts.
5. A major surrender effect may temporarily overlay another body shape, but the Host silhouette remains readable.
6. Conflicting presences create visible strain at attachment sites rather than random screen noise.

### Readability accents

- **Bone white:** stable inherited authority.
- **Funeral gold:** Concord and willing alignment.
- **Iron red:** suppression, forced control, and Host strain.
- **Ash gray:** exhausted or silenced presence.
- **Cold blue:** machine or navigation continuity.
- **Black fracture:** Abyssal contamination or identity collapse.

Critical states must use icons, sound, animation, posture, and UI framing in addition to color.

## 4. Combat role

### Primary role

Hybrid melee-caster and stance sequencer built around deliberate possession windows, relic combinations, and inherited skill transformations.

### Secondary roles

Depending on build, the Relic Host can become:

- heavy executioner bruiser;
- defensive ward-and-guard anchor;
- short-range relic caster;
- multi-presence combo engine;
- companion-adjacent echo specialist;
- ailment purifier and conversion tank;
- mobile bell-and-chain controller;
- sacrifice-driven burst class;
- weapon-focused duelist borrowing brief relic authority.

### Baseline strengths

- high build flexibility through relic loadout combinations;
- strong access to Guard, Ward, recovery conversion, and retaliation;
- clear burst windows during possession;
- excellent interaction with marks, echoes, zones, and enemy break states;
- strong resistance to silence, fear, and identity-control effects;
- multiple viable damage families depending on equipped relics;
- naturally rich unique-item space.

### Baseline weaknesses

- poor sequencing can create internal conflict and resource lockout;
- strongest effects often require preparing a presence before surrendering control;
- less mobile than agile launch classes without investment;
- vulnerable during forced ejection or Reliquary overload;
- visually and cognitively demanding if relic hierarchy is not carefully constrained;
- hybrid scaling can become unfocused without deliberate gear choices;
- cannot maintain every relic at full authority simultaneously.

### Difficulty target

- **Initial understanding:** medium.
- **Mechanical execution:** medium-high.
- **Buildcraft ceiling:** very high.
- **Failure punishment:** moderate, rising sharply only in domination-focused builds.

A new player should understand: build Accord, invoke one presence, use its altered skills, then discharge or rotate before conflict becomes dangerous.

An expert should manage multiple presence memories, planned ejections, cross-relic completions, selective suppression, and item-driven rule changes.

### Attribute lean

- **Might:** heavy relic weapons, stagger, armor, execution effects.
- **Will:** Accord stability, relic damage, possession duration, control resistance.

No attribute should be mandatory for every Relic Host build.

### Damage identity

The Relic Host primarily uses:

- Physical;
- Relic;
- Ritual;
- secondary conversion into Decay, Tidal, Oneiric, Void, or Temporal through specific relics.

The class must not gain unrestricted access to every damage family without opportunity cost. Conversion should come from equipped presences, keystones, or transformative uniques.

## 6. Unique resource — Accord

### Definition

**Accord** measures the current operating agreement between the Host and the equipped relic presences.

Accord is not mana, holiness, or friendship. It represents functional alignment: the Host is performing actions a presence recognizes as compatible with its retained purpose.

### Proposed range

- baseline: `0–100 Accord`;
- stable threshold: `30–69`;
- aligned threshold: `70–100`;
- fractured threshold: `0–29` after active conflict or forced suppression.

### Generation

Accord is gained by:

- using a relic’s favored skill tags;
- completing that relic’s **Memory Condition**;
- blocking, executing, binding, healing, moving, or sacrificing in ways matching its identity;
- rotating away from a presence after completing its purpose;
- using neutral Host skills that explicitly mediate conflict;
- interacting with class-tree nodes and gear affixes.

### Spending

Accord is spent to:

- enter **Investiture**, granting limited control to a selected presence;
- empower relic-specific skill variants;
- trigger **Last Testament** discharge effects;
- prevent forced ejection;
- maintain multiple partially awakened presences in advanced builds;
- overwrite a relic’s refusal through domination mechanics.

### High and low states

At high Accord:

- possession transitions are faster;
- presence skill variants gain full behavior;
- conflict generation is reduced;
- Last Testament effects become stronger;
- certain defensive benefits persist briefly after rotation.

At low Accord:

- the Host can still use baseline skills;
- presence variants become weaker or unavailable;
- forced invocation generates more Conflict;
- the active relic may resist specific commands;
- recovery tools remain available so the class is not trapped in failure.

### Persistence rules

**PROPOSED:**

- Accord persists between ordinary encounters but decays toward 40 outside combat.
- Town entry normalizes Accord to 40 and clears Conflict.
- Death resets Accord to 25 rather than zero.
- Boss phase transitions do not clear Accord or Memory Conditions.

### Resource design rule

The class must function at low Accord. High Accord creates authority and sequencing depth; it cannot be the price of basic usability.

## 7. Signature mechanic — The Reliquary Council

### Overview

The Relic Host equips a small set of named **Presences** into the Reliquary Frame. Each Presence provides:

- one passive inheritance;
- one Memory Condition;
- one Investiture transformation package;
- one Last Testament discharge;
- one conflict behavior;
- one visual and audio identity.

The launch target should support many collectible Presences, but the active loadout remains constrained.

### Proposed loadout structure

- **Primary Presence:** full Investiture access and strongest passive.
- **Secondary Presence:** full Memory Condition and limited skill transformation.
- **Witness Presence:** passive inheritance and trigger access; cannot normally take full control.

Advanced keystones and uniques may alter this structure.

### Investiture

Investiture is the deliberate act of allowing one Presence to partially operate the Host.

During Investiture:

- posture and core attack grammar change;
- selected active skills gain a presence-specific variant;
- one passive inheritance intensifies;
- Accord drains slowly or by action depending on the Presence;
- Conflict rises when the Host acts against the Presence’s retained purpose;
- the player retains full movement and targeting control.

Baseline Investiture duration target: 6–12 seconds depending on spending and actions.

### Memory Conditions

A Memory Condition is a readable combat task that reactivates a Presence’s retained pattern.

Examples:

- block a heavy hit;
- execute an enemy under a threshold;
- strike several enemies with one chain sweep;
- remain inside a consecrated ruin while taking damage;
- spend Life voluntarily;
- complete a full weapon combo without being interrupted;
- cleanse an ailment;
- bind an elite or boss during a break window.

Memory Conditions must encourage class-appropriate play rather than arbitrary chores.

### Last Testament

Ending Investiture willingly after completing its Memory Condition triggers the Presence’s Last Testament.

A Last Testament may:

- echo the last empowered attack;
- leave a relic zone;
- grant Guard or Ward;
- bind nearby enemies;
- transfer a stored wound;
- summon a brief autonomous strike;
- convert remaining Accord into a class-specific payoff.

Last Testaments reward clean rotation rather than permanent stance uptime.

### Conflict

Conflict is a secondary pressure meter generated when:

- the Host forces Investiture below the Accord threshold;
- incompatible Presences are awakened together;
- the player repeatedly violates a Presence’s behavior;
- certain domination skills or uniques suppress refusal;
- enemy effects target identity or possession.

At maximum Conflict, **Ejection** occurs unless prevented.

### Ejection

Ejection forcibly ends Investiture and creates a short penalty determined by the expelled Presence:

- temporary skill lock for that Presence;
- Host stagger;
- Ward collapse;
- a hostile relic echo;
- Accord loss;
- temporary self-mark or vulnerability.

Ejection must be brief, readable, and survivable. It is a consequence, not a prolonged loss-of-control cutscene.

### Player-control guarantee

No baseline mechanic may:

- reverse player input;
- make the character attack allies;
- remove control for more than a brief stagger;
- force random skill casts;
- change equipped skills without preview.

Possession changes rules and posture while preserving player agency.

## 8. Foundational Presence roster

These are proposed mechanical archetypes, not necessarily the final launch acquisition list.

### PRE-01 — The Iron Confessor

- Identity: executioner-priest built to extract truthful final statements.
- Passive: increased stagger and damage against marked or low-life enemies.
- Memory Condition: execute an enemy shortly after breaking it.
- Investiture: heavy strikes become slower, wider, and able to consume marks.
- Last Testament: repeats the final execution arc as a delayed Relic echo.
- Conflict trigger: abandoning a marked enemy before completion.

### PRE-02 — Saint Veyra of the Closed Gate

- Identity: custodian who held a failed evacuation route shut from the inside.
- Passive: Guard effectiveness and displacement resistance.
- Memory Condition: prevent or absorb a heavy hit while protecting a bound zone.
- Investiture: shield, ward, and anchoring skills intensify; movement becomes slower.
- Last Testament: leaves a gate-shaped barrier that blocks projectiles and grants allies Ward.
- Conflict trigger: voluntarily leaving an active protected zone too early.

### PRE-03 — The Bell Without a Tower

- Identity: alarm intelligence severed from a dead city.
- Passive: increased radius and detection of concealed or ambushing enemies.
- Memory Condition: hit several different enemies with one cast or sweep.
- Investiture: attacks emit directional tolls that Daze and build Break Pressure.
- Last Testament: a final bell wave pulls marked enemies inward.
- Conflict trigger: repeatedly striking only one target while unthreatened.

### PRE-04 — Mother Calcine

- Identity: plague-burner who preserved survivors by carrying their sickness.
- Passive: ailment tolerance and conversion of cleansed ailments into Accord.
- Memory Condition: cleanse or endure a threshold of ailment damage.
- Investiture: stores ailments and can transfer or burn them into Decay zones.
- Last Testament: purges stored ailments in a controlled ash burst.
- Conflict trigger: using recovery while no ailment or wound is present.

### PRE-05 — The Keel Saint

- Identity: drowned navigation engine that remembers routes as pressure gradients.
- Passive: movement resistance and increased speed after Displace effects.
- Memory Condition: cross through enemies or terrain hazards without being hit.
- Investiture: grants a heavy, pressure-driven movement skill and Tidal conversion.
- Last Testament: leaves a current that repeats the Host’s path and drags enemies along it.
- Conflict trigger: remaining stationary too long during active danger.

### PRE-06 — The Unnamed Standard

- Identity: battlefield banner that retained the command instinct of an erased army.
- Passive: self-buff strength and companion/echo durability.
- Memory Condition: maintain pressure near multiple enemies or allies.
- Investiture: creates a moving command radius centered on the Host.
- Last Testament: plants a temporary standard that preserves the final active buff package.
- Conflict trigger: retreating while healthy and enemies remain nearby.

### PRE-07 — The Pale Artificer

- Identity: dead machine-saint that repairs systems by replacing damaged material with itself.
- Passive: Ward recovery and object/relic cooldown rate.
- Memory Condition: restore Ward after it is fully broken.
- Investiture: weapon attacks manifest mechanical extensions and consume Ward for damage.
- Last Testament: rebuilds part of the Host’s Ward and creates a repairing construct.
- Conflict trigger: overcapping Ward without spending or converting it.

### PRE-08 — The Witness in Salt

- Identity: fossilized observer that records cause without interpretation.
- Passive: stores a small portion of recently received and dealt damage.
- Memory Condition: witness a boss or elite completing a major attack without being struck.
- Investiture: can echo one stored enemy pattern as a Host-safe Relic effect.
- Last Testament: releases a non-identical replay of the stored pattern.
- Conflict trigger: attempting to replay an effect before fully observing it.

### Presence identity rule

Every Presence needs a retained function, behavior, and cost. A Presence cannot exist solely to grant “fire damage,” “critical chance,” or another generic stat package.

## 9. Weapons and Class Relic equipment

### Supported weapon families

- great hammers and execution mauls;
- polearms and processional blades;
- chain censers and hooked flails;
- shields and gate slabs;
- one-handed blades with reliquary off-hands;
- bells, standards, and staff-like machine relics;
- heavy firearms or launchers only if later world direction supports them;
- unarmed frame-strike builds through specific relics or uniques.

### Weapon philosophy

The weapon remains physically important. Investiture transforms its grammar but should not replace it with generic magical casting.

Examples:

- a maul becomes an execution instrument under the Iron Confessor;
- a shield becomes a gate segment under Saint Veyra;
- a chain censer becomes a bell-clapper under the Bell Without a Tower;
- a polearm becomes a keel or rudder under the Keel Saint.

### Class Relic slot — Reliquary Frame

The proposed Relic Host Class Relic is the **Reliquary Frame**.

It determines:

- Presence capacity;
- Accord baseline and thresholds;
- Conflict tolerance;
- which body region visually houses the primary relic;
- frame-specific affix pools;
- Investiture transition behavior;
- interaction with Ejection and Last Testament.

Possible frame bases:

- Sternum Cage — balanced Accord and armor;
- Spinal Procession — stronger movement and rotation effects;
- Cranial Basilica — stronger casting and Memory Conditions, higher Conflict risk;
- Shoulder Ossuary — heavy weapon and Guard focus;
- Distributed Sutures — advanced multi-presence support with lower individual authority.

### Equipment visibility

At least the Primary Presence and Reliquary Frame must be visibly represented on the character. Secondary and Witness Presences may use smaller but recognizable attachments.

## 10. Movement identity

### Baseline movement skill — Pallbearer Step

The Host drives the active relic forward and follows its momentum through a short, collision-respecting displacement.

- heavy rather than agile animation;
- damages or shoves enemies at the endpoint depending on weapon;
- gains a Presence variant during Investiture;
- cannot pass through closed geometry;
- preserves facing or retargets based on input settings;
- baseline cooldown target: 4.5 seconds.

### Presence variants

- Iron Confessor: shoulder-check into execution range.
- Saint Veyra: short anchored advance that leaves a barrier edge.
- Bell Without a Tower: tolling step that Dazes nearby enemies.
- Mother Calcine: ash-shed movement that leaves stored ailment behind.
- Keel Saint: pressure surge along a steerable route.
- Pale Artificer: mechanical pull toward a placed construct.

### Movement rules

- The class must have reliable hazard escape without a mobility Presence.
- Heavy silhouette cannot justify sluggish input.
- Investiture may change movement behavior but never remove escape access.
- Movement echoes must remain collision-safe.
- Enemy displacement resistance cannot make the movement skill feel broken; resisted movement should convert into impact or Break Pressure.

## 11. Foundational active skill kit

All values and names are proposed. Skills are designed as a launch vocabulary rather than a final hotbar requirement.

### REL-S01 — Reliquary Strike

**Role:** low-cost core weapon action and Accord builder.

A grounded weapon sequence that changes its third hit according to the active Presence.

- builds Accord when the third hit fulfills a Presence preference;
- remains usable outside Investiture;
- supports heavy, reach, shield, and chain weapon variants;
- final hit provides strong animation and sound confirmation.

### REL-S02 — Invoke Presence

**Role:** signature stance activation.

Selects a loaded Presence and enters Investiture by spending Accord.

- tap activates the last selected Presence;
- hold or radial input selects another Presence;
- animation can be canceled into defensive timing at a cost;
- unavailable Presence states are clearly shown before activation.

### REL-S03 — Seal the Vessel

**Role:** defense, mediation, and emergency recovery.

The Host clamps the Reliquary Frame shut, gaining Guard and suppressing Conflict generation briefly.

- converts part of prevented damage into Accord;
- ends hostile relic echoes;
- does not cleanse every ailment by default;
- can be specialized into Ward restoration, retaliation, or forced domination.

### REL-S04 — Processional Chain

**Role:** Bind, pull, grouping, and close-range control.

Throws or sweeps a chain carrying seals and relic fragments.

- Binds standard enemies to the Host or terrain;
- against bosses, creates Break Pressure and a short movement-tension effect;
- Presence variants alter endpoint behavior;
- chain path must remain visually readable in dense packs.

### REL-S05 — Borrowed Miracle

**Role:** Presence-specific tactical cast.

Executes the equipped Presence’s designated Miracle skill.

Examples:

- Confessor execution cone;
- Veyra barrier plane;
- Bell directional toll;
- Calcine ailment transfer;
- Keel pressure wake;
- Artificer repair construct.

This shared slot reduces hotbar burden while preserving strong relic variety.

### REL-S06 — Exhumed Authority

**Role:** mark and setup.

Brands an enemy, object, or zone as disputed property of the Reliquary Council.

- marked enemies grant Accord when struck according to the active Memory Condition;
- marked objects or zones can become Last Testament anchors;
- bosses cannot remove the mark arbitrarily;
- same-class marks coexist through owner identity.

### REL-S07 — Pallbearer Step

**Role:** movement.

Performs the class movement action defined above.

- gains a distinct Investiture variant;
- produces Break Pressure when displacement is resisted;
- can complete certain Memory Conditions through traversal or impact.

### REL-S08 — Litany of Refusal

**Role:** anti-control, cleanse, and domination tool.

The Host rejects both enemy influence and internal relic command.

- removes or reduces Dread, Silence, Bind, and possession-type effects;
- suppresses the active Presence’s conflict behavior briefly;
- generates Conflict when used repeatedly;
- domination builds convert refusal into damage or extended Investiture.

### REL-S09 — Testament Cut

**Role:** rotation payoff.

Ends Investiture manually with a committed weapon or relic strike.

- triggers Last Testament if the Memory Condition is complete;
- partial completion gives a weaker discharge rather than nothing;
- can be aimed and timed;
- becomes a central skill for clean rotation builds.

### REL-S10 — Ossuary Ward

**Role:** renewable barrier and stored-wound interaction.

Builds Ward from bound fragments around the Host.

- fragments break visibly as Ward is lost;
- broken fragments may feed Accord or a selected Presence;
- some builds consume Ward offensively;
- recovery has a clear delay or action requirement.

### REL-S11 — Council in Chains

**Role:** multi-presence setup.

Briefly awakens Secondary and Witness Presences without granting full control.

- creates one limited trigger from each available Presence;
- sharply increases Conflict;
- high-level buildcraft tool, not mandatory baseline rotation;
- presentation uses symbols and partial manifestations rather than multiple full ghosts.

### REL-S12 — Last Bearer

**Role:** sacrifice, recovery, and emergency comeback.

The Host destroys or temporarily seals one loaded Presence to survive lethal pressure.

- grants Guard, Life recovery, or Ward depending on the Presence;
- disables that Presence for a meaningful duration;
- can trigger a unique sacrifice testament;
- never permanently deletes collected progression.

### REL-U01 — The Dead Take Office

**Role:** ultimate.

The Host opens every loaded Reliquary chamber and establishes a temporary Council state in which multiple Presences operate through one body under player command.

Proposed behavior:

- Primary Presence enters full Investiture without Accord drain;
- Secondary Presence contributes its Borrowed Miracle transformation;
- Witness Presence triggers its passive and Last Testament at reduced strength;
- Conflict cannot cause Ejection during the ultimate but is accumulated as **Debt**;
- weapon attacks inherit a composite relic grammar;
- ending the ultimate releases a chosen final Testament;
- unresolved Debt becomes post-ultimate Conflict or a build-dependent cost.

Baseline duration target: 10–14 seconds.

Baseline cooldown target: 75–105 seconds, or partially event-driven through completed Memory Conditions.

The ultimate must feel like a dangerous council occupying one body, not a generic golden super mode.

## 12. Class-specific progression paths

The launch tree should begin with four recognizable paths while supporting cross-path builds.

### PATH-REL-A — Concordant Bearer

**Fantasy:** achieve precise cooperation with inherited dead without surrendering personal authority.

**Mechanical focus:**

- Accord generation and retention;
- clean Investiture rotations;
- completed Memory Conditions;
- empowered Last Testaments;
- reduced Conflict through correct sequencing;
- multiple Presence flexibility;
- self and party defensive residue.

Representative major nodes:

- **Terms Accepted:** completing a Memory Condition pauses Accord drain briefly.
- **Shared Hands:** first transformed skill after Investiture gains both Host and Presence tags.
- **Courteous Exit:** Testament Cut grants Guard when ending an aligned Investiture.
- **No Voice Forgotten:** rotating through all loaded Presences empowers the next Last Testament.
- **Witnessed Agreement:** Witness Presence gains limited Accord from the Primary Presence’s completed condition.

Playstyle: deliberate multi-stance sequencing with high consistency and low failure volatility.

### PATH-REL-B — Iron Usurper

**Fantasy:** dominate the relics, force their miracles into service, and use internal conflict as fuel.

**Mechanical focus:**

- forced Investiture at low Accord;
- Conflict generation and spending;
- domination damage;
- suppression windows;
- Ejection manipulation;
- sacrifice and recovery;
- high-risk burst.

Representative major nodes:

- **The Body Has One Throne:** only the Primary Presence may trigger Ejection; Secondary conflict becomes damage.
- **Refusal Is Fuel:** Litany of Refusal converts suppressed Conflict into Relic damage.
- **Break the Saint:** forced Ejection creates a hostile echo that can be struck and consumed.
- **Command Through Pain:** voluntary Life spending grants temporary Accord and authority.
- **Unwilling Miracle:** Borrowed Miracle can be cast below its normal Accord threshold at increased Conflict.

Playstyle: aggressive possession control, self-imposed danger, and explosive discharge.

### PATH-REL-C — Processional Bastion

**Fantasy:** become a moving fortress-shrine that carries protection, boundaries, and inherited law through hostile ground.

**Mechanical focus:**

- Armor, Ward, Guard, and Anchor;
- barriers, standards, and moving zones;
- shield and heavy weapon play;
- ally-compatible self-benefiting defenses;
- retaliation and stored damage;
- Saint Veyra, Unnamed Standard, and Artificer synergies.

Representative major nodes:

- **Walk the Wall:** Guard remains partially active during Pallbearer Step.
- **Burden Shared:** self-targeted barriers also grant nearby allies or constructs reduced Ward.
- **Shrine on Foot:** Host-centered zones move with the player at reduced radius.
- **Every Blow Catalogued:** blocked heavy hits empower the next Testament Cut.
- **No Ground Surrendered:** resisting displacement produces a Relic shockwave.

Playstyle: heavy frontline control that remains fully viable solo.

### PATH-REL-D — Testament Reclaimer

**Fantasy:** extract the dead’s final patterns and weaponize their release rather than maintaining long possession.

**Mechanical focus:**

- short Investiture windows;
- Last Testament frequency;
- echoes and stored actions;
- mark consumption;
- Presence sacrifice and reawakening;
- critical sequencing;
- hybrid cast/weapon builds.

Representative major nodes:

- **Nothing Dies Once:** Last Testaments can Echo at reduced effect after a delay.
- **Final Words First:** entering Investiture begins with partial Memory completion but shortens duration.
- **Inheritance Tax:** consuming a mark restores Accord to a different Presence.
- **Empty Chamber Resonance:** disabled or sacrificed Presences empower remaining Testaments.
- **Return to Dust:** Testament Cut against a broken enemy Ruptures all Host-owned marks for a composite effect.

Playstyle: rapid stance-entry, discharge, mark management, and relic echo chaining.

## 14. Proposed keystones

### KEY-REL-01 — One Body, Many Graves

You may equip one additional Witness Presence.

- Witness passives operate at reduced strength.
- Council in Chains generates significantly more Conflict.
- Each distinct Memory Condition completed empowers the ultimate.

Build purpose: broad multi-relic sequencing.

### KEY-REL-02 — Sainthood by Force

You may enter Investiture at any Accord value.

- Accord cannot reduce Conflict while Investiture is active.
- Forced transformed skills gain damage and stagger.
- Ejection penalties are stronger.

Build purpose: domination burst.

### KEY-REL-03 — The Host Is the Relic

Disable the Primary Presence slot.

- Reliquary Frame affixes and Host skills are greatly empowered.

Build purpose: relicless or anti-possession bruiser.

### KEY-REL-04 — Procession Without End

Host-centered zones and standards move with you.

- Their radius is reduced.
- Leaving a zone no longer ends relevant Memory Conditions.
- Movement speed is reduced while multiple zones are active.

Build purpose: mobile fortress.

### KEY-REL-05 — Last Testament Is Law

Ending Investiture consumes all remaining Accord.

- Last Testament scales strongly with Accord consumed.

Build purpose: large planned discharge.

Conflict no longer causes Ejection automatically.

Build purpose: sustained danger state.

Build purpose: distributed survival and sacrifice builds.

### KEY-REL-08 — Unanimous Possession

The Dead Take Office requires all loaded Memory Conditions to be complete.

- Ultimate duration and composite effects are greatly increased.
- It cannot be activated early.

Build purpose: high-preparation ultimate build.

## 15. Endgame build archetypes

### BUILD-REL-01 — Iron Confessor Execution Loop

**Core:** Iron Confessor, Testament Reclaimer, Last Testament Is Law.

**Loop:**

1. mark priority enemies with Exhumed Authority;
2. build stagger through heavy weapon sequences;
3. enter short Confessor Investiture;
4. break and execute the target;
5. use Testament Cut to repeat the execution arc;
6. consume remaining marks to fund the next Presence or rotation.

**Strengths:** elite deletion, boss break windows, satisfying heavy cadence.

**Weaknesses:** limited ranged coverage, setup-dependent against scattered packs.

**Gear priorities:** Might, stagger, mark effect, Testament scaling, heavy weapon reach.

### BUILD-REL-02 — Saint Veyra Walking Bastion

**Core:** Saint Veyra, Processional Bastion, Procession Without End.

**Loop:**

1. establish a moving barrier or protected radius;
2. absorb heavy hits to complete Veyra’s Memory;
3. advance with Pallbearer Step while preserving Guard;
4. end Investiture to leave a gate barrier;
5. rotate into Artificer or Standard to restore Ward and continue forward.

**Strengths:** exceptional survivability, dense-pack stability, strong solo and co-op utility.

**Weaknesses:** lower burst, movement and zone management demands.

**Gear priorities:** Armor, Guard, Ward recovery, displacement resistance, moving-zone support.

### BUILD-REL-03 — Bell Procession Crowd Breaker

**Core:** Bell Without a Tower, chain weapon, Concordant Bearer.

**Loop:**

1. group enemies with Processional Chain;
2. strike multiple targets to complete the Bell memory;
3. use tolling weapon sweeps and Borrowed Miracle;
4. rotate cleanly to pull marked survivors into a final bell wave;
5. repeat through dense packs.

**Strengths:** pack control, Daze, break pressure, excellent audiovisual identity.

**Weaknesses:** weaker single-target output without cross-path investment.

**Gear priorities:** radius, chain reach, multi-hit Accord, Daze and break scaling.

### BUILD-REL-04 — Crown of Nails Ejection Engine

**Core:** Iron Usurper, Conflict of Interest or Sainthood by Force, hostile echo consumption.

**Loop:**

1. force Investiture below the safe Accord threshold;
2. violate the active Presence deliberately;
3. trigger or approach Ejection;
4. create a hostile relic echo;
5. bind, strike, or consume the echo for burst, Accord, and recovery;
6. vent remaining Conflict through Seal the Vessel.

**Strengths:** high-risk burst, unique internal-enemy gameplay, strong comeback potential.

**Weaknesses:** execution-heavy, dangerous during boss mechanics, cognitively demanding.

**Gear priorities:** Conflict gain, Ejection effect, self-damage mitigation, echo damage.

### BUILD-REL-05 — Archive of Final Motions

**Core:** Concordant Bearer, Testament Reclaimer, Witness in Salt.

**Loop:**

1. complete several different Memory Conditions;
2. perform brief ordered Investitures;
3. store each Last Testament as a queued echo;
4. observe and record a major enemy pattern;
5. release the archive during a boss vulnerability window.

**Strengths:** massive sequencing ceiling, flexible damage profile, boss adaptation.

**Weaknesses:** setup time, poor recovery after a mistimed release.

**Gear priorities:** echo retention, Accord on rotation, Memory speed, stored-pattern scaling.

### BUILD-REL-06 — Empty Reliquary Framebreaker

**Core:** The Host Is the Relic, Iron Usurper, heavy frame and weapon scaling.

**Loop:**

1. fight without a Primary Presence;
2. use baseline Host skills and empowered Reliquary Frame effects;
3. consume empty chambers as defensive and offensive thresholds;
4. trigger Frame Rupture for large close-range impact;
5. sacrifice optional Witness utility for raw personal authority.

**Strengths:** simple surface loop, strong bruiser identity, low stance-management burden.

**Weaknesses:** loses much of the class’s flexible Presence utility and unique rotation depth.

**Gear priorities:** frame base effects, weapon damage, Armor, Ward, empty-slot bonuses.

### UNI-REL-01 — The Mouth That Outlived the Saint

**Slot:** Reliquary Frame attachment or amulet.

**Rule change:** The Primary Presence’s Last Testament is spoken before Investiture begins, placing a delayed effect that completes only when Investiture ends.

**Slot:** head.

**Rule change:** Ejected Presences remain as targetable hostile relic echoes. Defeating an echo permanently suppresses that Presence for the encounter but grants its full passive to the Host.

**Enables:** Crown of Nails domination builds.

**Cost:** repeated ejections can remove all Presence access during a long boss fight.

**Readability:** distinct relic effigies tear free and retain the Presence’s icon.

### UNI-REL-04 — Pallbearer’s Empty Harness

**Slot:** Class Relic.

**Rule change:** supports The Host Is the Relic play even without its keystone. Each empty Presence slot changes Pallbearer Step into an additional chained impact.

**Enables:** movement-impact bruiser and empty-frame builds.

**Cost:** lower Accord capacity and no Witness slot.

**Readability:** unused harness clamps slam shut one after another during movement.

### UNI-REL-05 — The Bell Buried in Flesh

**Slot:** chest.

**Rule change:** Bell Without a Tower tolls whenever the Host takes a heavy hit. The toll deals no direct damage but repeats all Break Pressure applied recently.

**Enables:** retaliation tank and boss stagger builds.

### UNI-REL-06 — Keel of the Landlocked Dead

**Slot:** polearm or Class Relic.

**Rule change:** Keel Saint movement paths remain as temporary solid currents. The Host and allies can travel along them; enemies are displaced toward their end.

**Enables:** route-builder, mobile zone, and co-op traversal builds.

**Cost:** fewer charges and increased Pallbearer Step cooldown.

**Readability:** a compressed water-and-bone track remains anchored to terrain.

### UNI-REL-07 — Ash Ledger of Mother Calcine

**Slot:** off-hand.

**Rule change:** cleansed ailments are recorded as named entries. Borrowed Miracle may spend a selected entry to apply a transformed version to enemies.

**Enables:** ailment archivist and adaptive Decay builds.

**Cost:** ailments deal increased damage to the Host before being cleansed; ledger capacity is limited.

**Readability:** physical pages or wax tablets fill with distinct symbols.

### UNI-REL-08 — Standard of the Army That Never Was

**Rule change:** The Unnamed Standard records self-buffs and defensive states that expire within its radius, then replays one when a Last Testament triggers.

**Enables:** buff sequencing and moving-fortress hybrids.

**Cost:** recorded buffs have reduced effect and cannot be replayed twice consecutively.

**Readability:** the banner physically gains and loses stitched emblems representing recorded states.

### UNI-REL-09 — Salt Witness Aperture

**Slot:** ring or eye relic.

**Rule change:** Witness in Salt can record one enemy telegraph shape and convert it into a player-owned zone after successful avoidance.

**Enables:** reactive boss-learning and pattern-reversal builds.

**Cost:** recording replaces the Witness passive until discharged; copied effects are simplified and never exact boss attacks.

**Readability:** a salt-white outline of the observed telegraph remains in the UI and on the Host.

### UNI-REL-10 — The Procession’s Last Vacancy

**Slot:** Class Relic.

**Rule change:** Secondary Presence becomes a second Primary Presence. Both gain full Investiture transformations, but every skill favors one and angers the other.

**Enables:** dual-possession conflict builds.

**Cost:** Conflict cannot naturally decay in combat and Investiture always awakens both.

**Readability:** two dominant relic silhouettes pull the Host’s posture in opposing directions.

## 17. Gear interactions and affix vocabulary

### General affix groups

- maximum Accord;
- Accord gained from Memory Conditions;
- Accord retained after Testament Cut;
- Investiture duration;
- Investiture transition speed;
- Conflict generation and venting;
- Ejection recovery;
- Last Testament effect and radius;
- Presence passive effect;
- Borrowed Miracle cooldown and conversion;
- Reliquary Frame Armor or Ward;
- mark duration and ownership;
- chain reach and Bind strength;
- Guard, Ward, and sacrifice thresholds;
- Memory Condition progress from bosses and elites.

### Presence-specific affixes

Presence-specific affixes should usually appear on Class Relics, named bases, or uniques rather than flooding the universal gear pool.

Examples:

- Iron Confessor execution threshold;
- Veyra barrier integrity;
- Bell multi-target count forgiveness;
- Calcine stored ailment capacity;
- Keel path duration;
- Standard recorded-state limit;
- Artificer Ward-to-damage conversion;
- Witness observation window.

### Conversion rules

- Conversion must name a source and destination damage family.
- A Presence cannot grant universal conversion to all skills without substantial cost.
- Converted Borrowed Miracles retain the `Relic` tag for class interactions.
- Physical weapon portions and Relic effect portions must be separately inspectable in tooltips.
- Echoes and Last Testaments cannot recursively trigger themselves unless a unique explicitly creates a capped exception.

### Same-item interaction rules

- Multiple Testament echoes require a hard repetition cap.
- Multiple death-prevention effects resolve in a documented order.
- A Presence disabled by sacrifice cannot be re-enabled by generic cooldown reset.
- Two effects that change Primary Presence capacity must declare precedence.
- No item may make Conflict, Accord, Memory Conditions, and Ejection all irrelevant simultaneously.

## 18. Party and co-op identity

### Solo guarantee

Every group-facing effect must retain a self-use function.

Examples:

- Veyra barrier protects the Host even with no allies;
- Standard buffs affect the Host first;
- Processional zones move with or benefit the Host;
- ally-triggered Accord clauses have self, companion, construct, or enemy alternatives;
- sacrifice and recovery never require another player.

### Co-op contribution

The Relic Host can contribute:

- temporary barriers and Ward;
- Bind and Break Pressure;
- moving command zones;
- ailment cleansing and transfer;
- controlled enemy grouping;
- defensive residue after Presence rotation;
- emergency body-blocking and displacement resistance.

The class should not become mandatory because it carries the only viable party defense.

### Same-class interaction

- each Host owns its marks, Memory Conditions, and Accord;
- barriers may overlap visually but use capped mechanical stacking;
- one Host cannot trigger another Host’s Last Testament by default;
- different Presence auras may coexist when their effects differ;
- identical standards refresh only their owner’s record state;
- hostile relic echoes are owned by the creating Host for rewards and triggers.

### Narrative co-op rule

Multiple Relic Hosts are not assumed to carry copies of the exact same consciousness. Shared Presence archetypes may represent fragments, offices, masks, copied procedures, or separate relics belonging to the same cult lineage.

## 19. Mastery curve

### First-hour understanding

The player learns:

1. basic weapon attacks build Accord;
2. one Presence can be invoked;
3. the Presence changes several skills;
4. a clear Memory Condition powers the exit effect;
5. ending Investiture willingly is better than being ejected.

### Campaign mastery

The player learns:

- switching between Primary and Secondary Presence;
- matching Memory Conditions to encounter types;
- using Seal the Vessel and Litany of Refusal;
- preserving Accord between packs;
- creating defensive or offensive Last Testament residue;
- choosing gear that supports a coherent Presence package.

### Endgame mastery

The player optimizes:

- ordered Presence rotations;
- boss-specific Memory completion;
- Conflict as a controlled resource;
- Ejection timing and echo consumption;
- item-driven loadout-rule changes;
- exact Testament release windows;
- multi-presence ultimate preparation;
- damage-family and defensive conversion.

### Failure readability

When a Relic Host fails, the player should understand whether the cause was:

- insufficient Accord;
- incomplete Memory Condition;
- excessive Conflict;
- wrong Presence for the encounter;
- forced Ejection;
- Ward or Guard collapse;
- a disabled Presence;
- poor gear scaling.

The UI must never reduce failure to “the ghosts got angry” without mechanical explanation.

## 20. Accessibility and readability requirements

### Presence hierarchy

- Primary, Secondary, and Witness Presence use different frame shapes.
- Active Investiture must be readable without color.
- Memory Condition progress appears near the relevant Presence, not in a distant generic buff row.
- Conflict uses directional or fracture animation distinct from Accord fill.
- disabled, exhausted, and sacrificed states have unique silhouettes and sounds.

### Input and selection

- tap-to-reinvoke and hold/radial selection must both be configurable;
- controller selection must pause neither combat nor the world unless the game’s global design allows it;
- optional direct Presence bindings should be supported;
- skill transformation previews must be available in the loadout screen;
- PlayStation-family, Xbox-family, and keyboard/mouse prompts update dynamically.

### Visual density

- no more than one full Presence manifestation at baseline;
- Council in Chains uses partial limbs, symbols, weapon overlays, or localized figures;
- friendly barriers and enemy barriers use different edge language;
- Last Testament echoes must be visually subordinate to current player actions;
- party visibility settings may reduce other players’ relic manifestations without hiding danger or utility.

### Audio

Each Presence requires:

- an activation cue;
- Memory completion cue;
- conflict warning cue;
- Last Testament cue;
- Ejection cue.

Voice lines must be separately adjustable and cannot be the sole communication channel.

### Cognitive-load options

- simplified Presence HUD;
- reduced voice frequency;
- stronger Memory Condition iconography;
- optional automatic radial return to Primary Presence;
- preview of post-ultimate Debt;
- configurable screen shake and internal body-horror intensity.

## 21. Technical implementation risks

### RISK-REL-01 — Combinatorial skill transformation

Multiple Presences transforming multiple skills can create an unmanageable matrix.

**Mitigation:** use data-driven transformation packages with fixed supported hooks. Borrowed Miracle carries the most unique behavior; other skills use constrained modifier interfaces.

### RISK-REL-02 — State-machine explosion

Accord, Conflict, Investiture, Memory Conditions, Ejection, sacrifice, disabled states, and ultimate Debt can produce brittle transitions.

**Mitigation:** define one authoritative Relic Host state service with explicit transition events and deterministic tests.

### RISK-REL-03 — Player agency during possession

Animation or AI-driven overlays could accidentally steal input.

**Mitigation:** all Investiture behavior remains player-controlled. Possession modifies action data, movement parameters, and animation layers rather than assigning an autonomous controller.

### RISK-REL-04 — Save-data complexity

Collected Presences, loadouts, frame sockets, progress, and item-modified capacity require stable persistence.

**Mitigation:** use stable Presence IDs, versioned loadout data, explicit migration, and no serialized scene-node references.

### RISK-REL-05 — Visual clutter

Several relics, zones, chains, barriers, echoes, and party Hosts can overwhelm combat.

**Mitigation:** hard manifestation hierarchy, effect budgets, owner filtering, simplified remote-player visuals, and deterministic culling rules that preserve telegraphs.

### RISK-REL-06 — Voice-content scope

Many sentient Presences could require enormous voice budgets.

**Mitigation:** communicate primarily through sound motifs, short barks, text fragments, posture, and item descriptions. Full dialogue is reserved for major campaign Presences.

### RISK-REL-07 — Universal best Presence

One Presence may dominate every build if its Memory Condition and passive are too broadly useful.

**Mitigation:** encounter-profile testing, opportunity costs, damage-family commitment, loadout slot pressure, and per-Presence balance telemetry.

### RISK-REL-08 — Boss compatibility

Memory Conditions based on executions, displacement, or multiple enemies may fail on bosses.

**Mitigation:** every condition has a boss translation such as break windows, phase objectives, hazard avoidance, add interaction, or damage thresholds.

### RISK-REL-09 — Recursive echoes

Last Testaments, Witness patterns, marks, and item effects may create infinite trigger loops.

**Mitigation:** event provenance tags, per-action trigger caps, explicit `cannot trigger itself` defaults, and automated recursion tests.

### RISK-REL-10 — Relic acquisition overload

Too many collectible Presences could feel like a second inventory game.

**Mitigation:** separate Presence archive from normal item inventory; allow search, favorites, build assignment, clear source hints, and duplicate conversion rather than clutter.

### RISK-REL-11 — Hybrid scaling traps

Players may mix weapon and relic effects without understanding scaling.

**Mitigation:** skill breakdowns, suggested tags, path-specific gear examples, and clear damage-source display.

### RISK-REL-12 — Narrative contradiction

A Presence appearing in multiple players, items, or quests can confuse continuity.

**Mitigation:** distinguish unique named continuities from repeatable relic offices, fragments, procedures, and masks in data and narrative tags.

## 22. Non-negotiable design rules

1. The Relic Host retains player control during possession.
2. Relics are inherited continuities or functions, not generic spell gems.
3. Each Presence has a motive, Memory Condition, passive, Investiture package, Last Testament, and conflict behavior.
4. Baseline combat works at low Accord.
5. Clean rotation is rewarded; permanent stance uptime is not the only viable goal.
6. Conflict is readable and controllable rather than random punishment.
7. Ejection is brief, survivable, and mechanically explained.
8. The class remains viable solo.
9. Party utility cannot make the class mandatory.
10. The weapon remains physically meaningful during Investiture.
11. Relic effects use material, bodily, architectural, and mechanical evidence.
12. The class does not become a polished holy knight.
13. The class does not become a conventional minion commander.
14. Bosses support translated Memory Conditions.
15. Presence loadouts are persistent and saveable.
16. Unique items change rules, sequencing, costs, or targeting rather than only granting larger numbers.
17. No build may erase Accord, Conflict, Memory Conditions, and Ejection simultaneously.
18. Multiple Relic Hosts remain readable in co-op.
19. New Presences must protect class identity rather than importing another class wholesale.
20. The campaign remains shared; the Relic Host receives a personal arc inside it, not a separate story mode.

## 23. Cross-class consistency audit

### Voidbringer boundary

- Voidbringer manipulates gravity, spatial pressure, portals, soul energy, and Corruption.
- Relic Host may equip a void-associated relic, but cannot gain broad portal traversal or gravity control as a baseline identity.
- A void Presence should provide a narrow inherited procedure, not replace Voidbringer buildcraft.

**Finding:** no direct contradiction.

### Penitent boundary

- Penitent openly writes ritual law into the battlefield through Brands, Circles, Rite Patterns, Fervor, and sacrifice.
- Relic Host invokes inherited procedures retained inside physical objects and dead continuities.
- Relic Host marks indicate disputed ownership or memory conditions, not ritual guilt grammar.

**Finding:** thematic adjacency is healthy if law-writing remains exclusive to Penitent.

### Graftborn boundary

- Graftborn authors living anatomy through Biomass, Body Regions, Adaptations, Trauma Imprints, and Rupture.
- Relic Host installs or carries preserved objects and continuities through a Reliquary Frame.
- Relic Host body horror should emphasize attachment, occupation, and preservation rather than freeform mutation.

**Finding:** no direct contradiction.

### Somnarch boundary

- Somnarch authors or sustains dream territory and manifestations through oneiric sovereignty.
- Relic Presences possess inherited continuity, material history, and retained function.
- Dream copies of former bearers are not mechanically equivalent to actual Presences.

**Finding:** boundary is explicit and compatible.

### Gorgon boundary

- Gorgon should own transformative gaze, pattern exposure, petrification, venomous alteration, and identity fixation.
- Relic Host may carry fossilized or gaze-related relics, but cannot broadly transform enemies through sight.

**Finding:** preserve the Witness in Salt as observation and replay, not petrification.

### Tidewrought boundary

- Tidewrought should own abyssal pressure, brine, Drown, currents, and sea-body identity.
- Keel Saint grants narrow Tidal conversion and movement-route behavior.

**Finding:** acceptable cross-class flavor with clear opportunity cost.

### Anachron boundary

- Anachron should own chronology, delayed and repeated action, Desync, and timeline manipulation.
- Relic Host echoes are retained memories or procedures, not time travel.
- Witness replay must be mechanically tagged as `Echo`, not `Temporal`, unless a specific item converts it.

**Finding:** no direct contradiction.

### Reserved class boundary — Choirborn

- Choirborn should own collective voice, harmonic body, resonance, and chorus identity.
- Relic Host may contain multiple voices, but combat is a council of distinct inherited Presences rather than a fused choir.

**Finding:** avoid sound-based Presence rosters dominating the class beyond the Bell archetype.

### Reserved class boundary — Echo Thief

- Echo Thief should appropriate enemy or ally actions, identities, or techniques.
- Relic Host uses pre-equipped inherited continuities and cannot freely steal live enemy skills.
- Witness in Salt records a simplified observed pattern through a committed Presence slot.

**Finding:** bounded observation is acceptable; broad live appropriation remains reserved.

### Reserved class boundary — Plaguebringer

- Plaguebringer should own epidemic spread, contagion networks, disease ecology, and plague propagation.
- Mother Calcine stores, cleanses, transfers, or burns ailments but does not create self-propagating epidemics.

**Finding:** no direct contradiction if contagion chains remain reserved.

## 24. Explicit owner decisions

### OQ-REL-01 — Presence voice level

Should major Presences speak conversationally during campaign scenes, use sparse combat barks and text memories, or remain mostly nonverbal?

### OQ-REL-02 — Starting Presence choice

Does the player select one of several starting Presences as background flavor and early mechanics, or begin with one canonical Presence?

### OQ-REL-03 — Loadout size

Is the baseline `Primary + Secondary + Witness` structure the right cognitive limit, or should launch begin with only two active Presences?

### OQ-REL-04 — Reliquary body placement

Should the default Frame be visibly fused into the chest and spine, or should body placement vary strongly with equipped frame base?

### OQ-REL-05 — Procession morality

Is the Procession of Last Things primarily tragic, authoritarian, necessary, predatory, or regionally divided?

### OQ-REL-06 — Saint Orison identity

Was Saint Orison originally a willing distributed custodian, a punishment victim, a failed survival project, or a later counterfeit saint?

### OQ-REL-07 — Accord terminology

Does **Accord** fit the desired tone, or should the resource use a harsher term such as Compliance, Authority, Concordance, or Office?

### OQ-REL-08 — Conflict UI

Should Conflict be one shared meter, individual per-Presence strain values, or a shared meter with individual warning states?

### OQ-REL-09 — Relic collection scope

Should Presences be a large collectible roster similar to skill gems, a smaller curated class progression set, or primarily item-bound?

### OQ-REL-10 — Named versus repeatable Presences

Which Presences are unique characters in canon, and which represent repeatable relic offices or fragments for co-op continuity?

### OQ-REL-11 — Empty Host viability

Should The Host Is the Relic remain a keystone niche, or be promoted into a full supported class path for players who dislike stance switching?

### OQ-REL-12 — Ultimate control scheme

### OQ-REL-13 — Religious vocabulary

How strongly should names such as saint, miracle, reliquary, litany, and testament remain, versus shifting toward stranger machine-custodian language?

### OQ-REL-14 — Body-horror intensity

How visibly should relic installation damage the Host: restrained scar-and-harness language, exposed preserved organs, or highly visceral insertion points?

### OQ-REL-15 — Class Relic slot confirmation

Should every launch class use the proposed Class Relic slot, and is the Reliquary Frame the correct Relic Host implementation?

### OQ-REL-16 — Presence acquisition in campaign

Should major personal missions always reward a new Presence, or should they sometimes alter an existing Presence’s identity and rules?

### Validation questions

1. Does each Presence change combat grammar enough to feel inherited rather than cosmetic?
2. Can a new player understand Accord, Memory completion, and voluntary discharge within ten minutes?
3. Is Conflict readable before Ejection?
4. Does the player retain a sense of full control during Investiture?
5. Is voluntary Testament Cut consistently more satisfying than passive expiration?
6. Do both Presence conditions function against a boss?
7. Does the class remain functional when Accord is low?
8. Can the UI communicate two Presences without overwhelming controller players?
9. Are heavy weapon and relic effects visually distinct but coherent?
10. Does the class feel meaningfully separate from Penitent, Somnarch, and a conventional summoner?

### Prototype success condition

The slice succeeds when players describe the class as carrying and negotiating with dangerous inherited authorities, can explain why they rotate Presences, and can identify the cause of Ejection without external explanation.

## 26. Final identity statement

The Relic Host is a person turned into contested sacred infrastructure.

Its depth comes from selecting which inherited dead authority takes office, fulfilling or violating its retained purpose, and deciding whether power is earned through cooperation, extracted through domination, or rejected in favor of the Host’s own body.

The class should feel brutal, material, and morally unstable: every miracle is evidence that someone or something never finished dying.
