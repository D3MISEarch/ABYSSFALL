# Somnarch Class Bible

> Status: **PROPOSED — owner review required.**
>
> Stable class ID: `CLS-SOM`
>
> This document defines the Somnarch as a launch class inside the shared AbyssFall campaign. It preserves the locked persistent-ARPG direction and treats all unapproved mechanics, names, values, and lore as proposals.

---

## 1. Class thesis

The Somnarch is not a conventional sleep mage, illusionist, psychic, or pet summoner.

The Somnarch is a **waking sovereign of hostile dream-territory** who imposes a private nightmare over real space, then weaponizes the contradiction between what enemies perceive and what the world permits.

Its fantasy is controlled oneiric dominion: planting impossible landmarks, forcing enemies to reenact fear, moving through spaces that only exist while unobserved, and deciding when a dream remains suggestion versus when it becomes physically binding.

### Player promise

The Somnarch lets the player say:

> “This battlefield belongs to the dream I brought with me, and you are already obeying its laws.”

### Core identity pillars

1. **Territorial dreaming** — the class creates a legible dream-state over real terrain rather than casting disconnected purple spells.
2. **Perception becomes setup** — enemies can be made to see, fear, remember, or anticipate things that later become mechanically real.
3. **Lucid sequencing** — skills change based on whether the Somnarch is Waking, Veiled, or in full Dominion.
4. **Fear with rules** — horror effects are readable, purposeful, and buildable; they are not random confusion.
5. **Persistent buildcraft** — class trees, equipment, dream-construct choices, saved loadouts, and oneiric conversion define long-term identity.

### Explicit anti-pillars

The Somnarch must not become:

- a generic shadow caster;
- a hard crowd-control class that permanently disables enemies;
- a sleep-themed necromancer with dream pets;
- a confusion casino where enemies act randomly;
- a purple projectile wizard with nightmare names;
- a fragile glass cannon that has no solo defensive plan;
- a class whose strongest mechanics disappear against bosses;
- a pure damage-over-time archetype with little positional play.

## 2. Lore and campaign role

### Origin

**PROPOSED:** During the Binding Epoch, one Lattice lineage was tasked with sealing routes that could not be closed physically. These custodians learned to move dangerous corridors, memories, and possible events into a shared sovereign dream where they could be contained without being destroyed.

Their descendants became known in fragmented folklore as bedroom saints, sleep tyrants, grave listeners, and monarchs without courts. Most modern Somnarchs do not know their inheritance. They experience recurring places that do not exist, wake with injuries earned in dreams, or discover that other people remember serving them in a kingdom neither party has visited.

A true Somnarch carries a dormant **Sovereign Dream**, a self-consistent territory nested inside the mind but partially anchored to the World-Lattice. When the Lattice fails, this territory begins leaking into waking space.

The class begins after surviving an event in which everyone nearby fell asleep, disappeared into a shared nightmare, or awakened with the same impossible memory. The exact background may remain selectable flavor rather than separate campaigns.

### Shared campaign integration

The Somnarch follows the same six-act campaign as every launch class.

#### ACT-01 — Recognition

The Hollow Crown map does not appear as geography. The Somnarch dreams the missing corridors between its symbols and realizes the crown is mapping routes hidden outside waking space.

Personal mission proposal: recover the **Unwoken Cartouche**, a funerary plate that records the Hollow King’s attempts to imprison a collapsing route inside royal dreams.

#### ACT-02 — The Red Basilica

The Covenant of Measure treats dreams as unaccountable testimony and therefore unlawful. Beneath the Basilica, the Somnarch discovers confession chambers designed to extract nightmares and use them as sacrificial geometry.

The class sees that ritual law and dream law can both rewrite behavior, but through opposite methods: the Penitent declares a rule openly; the Somnarch makes reality act as though the rule was always feared.

#### ACT-03 — The Country Behind Sleep

This act is the Somnarch’s primary personal-arc region.

The Quiet Parliament claims the Somnarch’s Sovereign Dream is not personal at all, but a vacant throne inside the collective sleep of a dead civilization. The Parliament offers recognition, legitimacy, and an eternal court in exchange for allowing waking bodies to become governed property of the dream-state.

Personal mission proposal: enter the **Palace of the Last Sleeper**, confront several contradictory versions of the Somnarch’s origin, and identify which memories were implanted by the Parliament.

#### ACT-04 — The Reliquary March

Relics retain dreams from former hosts. The Somnarch encounters devotional objects that continue dreaming extinct saints into partial existence.

This act establishes the boundary between Somnarch constructs and Relic Host presences: dream figures are authored or sustained through oneiric law, while relic entities possess inherited continuity and material history.

#### ACT-05 — The Inverted Sea

The suspended ocean dreams of every body it has drowned. The Somnarch can walk through these pressure-memories as temporary corridors but risks allowing the Ninth Tide to enter the Sovereign Dream.

Personal mission proposal: close the **Drowned Nursery**, a dream-region where the sea rehearses new land-dwelling bodies.

#### ACT-06 — Definition

The Eightfold Custodian identifies the Somnarch as a containment environment rather than a person. The Quiet Parliament offers one final bargain: abandon waking limitation and let all surviving people live inside a painless, permanent kingdom.

The class arc resolves by defining the ethical border of dream sovereignty.

The defining statement is:

> “A refuge entered without consent is only a prison that learned to feel merciful.”

### Personal-arc temptation

The Quiet Parliament offers a perfect collective dream where hunger, death, guilt, and time can be edited away. The cost is that waking reality becomes disposable and every sleeping mind becomes territory.

The temptation is mechanically reflected through late-game keystones that increase Dominion uptime while threatening to erase the distinction between player-authored zones and uncontrolled dream spread.

## 3. Visual language and silhouette

### Default silhouette

The Somnarch should read as a ruler, sleepwalker, and battlefield architect without becoming an ornate high-fantasy wizard.

- layered burial cloth, severe court garments, or restraint wrappings;
- a crown-like frame, halo, veil rig, or cranial apparatus that never resembles a clean royal crown;
- asymmetrical posture suggesting part of the body is still asleep;
- long weapon or focus silhouette used like a surveying instrument;
- small suspended objects that orient toward nearby dream zones;
- face partially obscured by veil, closed-eye mask, scar seals, or sleep sutures;
- no constant cloud of random eyes and tentacles.

### Material palette

- soot-black cloth;
- pale skin or porcelain mask surfaces;
- tarnished silver;
- bruised violet used sparingly;
- moonless blue-black;
- faded funeral gold;
- chalk-white dream architecture;
- translucent surfaces only at zone boundaries and impossible geometry.

### Dream geometry rules

1. Dream effects should create recognizable architecture, paths, thresholds, furniture, windows, stair fragments, hanging doors, or court markers.
2. Geometry may be impossible, but each construct must communicate function before spectacle.
3. Primary player-owned dream zones use stable edges and inward motion.
4. Enemy oneiric effects use broken edges and outward leakage.
5. Dream entities should not clutter the screen with fully independent bodies unless a build intentionally specializes in manifestations.
6. The battlefield should look invaded by a coherent place, not covered by unrelated spell decals.

### Readability accents

- **Pale white:** lucid, player-controlled dream law.
- **Deep indigo:** Veil state, uncertainty, hidden routes.
- **Muted gold:** sovereign control, completed dream clauses, Dominion authority.
- **Bruised red:** fear, nightmare pressure, failed resistance.
- **Black fracture:** uncontrolled leakage, hostile collective dream influence.

Critical states must use symbols, sound, animation, and zone shape in addition to color.

## 4. Combat role

### Primary role

Oneiric zone-controller and setup-payoff caster with strong positional manipulation and build-dependent melee or manifestation options.

### Secondary roles

Depending on build, the Somnarch can become:

- mobile nightmare duelist;
- layered zone-control caster;
- fear-and-break-pressure specialist;
- delayed burst architect;
- dream-echo combo engine;
- defensive territory keeper;
- close-range sovereign using a long blade, censer, or staff;
- manifestation commander without becoming a conventional minion class.

### Baseline strengths

- excellent control over where fights happen;
- strong setup into burst payoffs;
- multiple ways to defend through misdirection, displacement, and zone ownership;
- powerful against dense packs when terrain is prepared;
- proprietary oneiric states remain useful against bosses through Break Pressure and vulnerability windows;
- strong conversion hooks between fear, zones, echoes, and direct damage;
- can support allies while retaining complete solo functionality.

### Baseline weaknesses

- lower immediate damage before establishing dream territory;
- poor sequencing can leave key skills in the wrong state;
- mobile enemies can escape weakly placed zones;
- limited baseline armor and life compared with frontline classes;
- Dominion overextension can create resource collapse;
- visually complex builds require strict effect hierarchy;
- some defensive tools require prediction rather than reaction;
- strongest burst often depends on completing two or more dream clauses.

### Intended mastery curve

A new player understands:

- mark enemies with Dread;
- create a dream zone;
- fight inside it;
- spend Lucidity for stronger effects;
- enter Dominion when enough territory is established.

An advanced player masters:

- overlapping compatible zone clauses without visual clutter;
- moving between Waking and Veiled states intentionally;
- placing thresholds where enemies will cross later;
- converting failed control into boss Break Pressure;
- deciding whether to Rupture a dream effect now or preserve it for Dominion;
- routing through hidden paths for offense and defense;
- using echoes without accidentally consuming the wrong setup state.

## 5. Proposed base stats

These are relative targets, not final balance values.

| Stat | Relative rating | Design intent |
|---|---:|---|
| Maximum Life | Medium-low | Survives through territory, Guard, Ward, and displacement rather than raw health. |
| Armor | Low | Heavy armor should be possible only through dedicated gear paths. |
| Evasion | Medium | Veil-oriented builds gain conditional avoidance and tracking disruption. |
| Ward | High | Primary renewable defensive layer for many builds. |
| Oneiric Power | High | Strong scaling for dream zones, Dread, echoes, and manifestations. |
| Weapon Power | Medium-low | Can support duelist builds but is not the default strength. |
| Movement Speed | Medium | Baseline normal movement with excellent route-based repositioning. |
| Recovery | Medium | Strong inside owned territory, weaker when displaced. |
| Resource Stability | Medium-high | Stable during deliberate sequencing; collapses when overextending Dominion. |
| Crowd Control | High | Dread, Slow, Root, Daze, displacement, and Break Pressure. |

### Proposed starting profile

- high Will weighting;
- moderate Finesse;
- moderate Vitality;
- low Might;
- natural resistance to Dread and sleep-like effects;
- no blanket immunity to mental, temporal, or relic influence;
- higher baseline Ward than most launch classes.

## 6. Unique resource: Lucidity

Stable resource ID: `RES-SOM-LUCIDITY`

### Definition

Lucidity represents the Somnarch’s ability to remain self-aware while projecting the Sovereign Dream into waking space.

Lucidity is not mana. It is the tension between control and immersion.

### Generation

Baseline sources:

- striking enemies affected by Dread;
- enemies entering or crossing player-owned dream thresholds;
- completing a dream clause;
- successfully avoiding or Guarding an attack while Veiled;
- Rupturing an expired dream construct at the correct timing;
- defeating enemies inside owned territory;
- recalling or collapsing a manifestation.

### Spending

Lucidity is spent to:

- create major dream zones;
- shift into Veiled state;
- empower echoes;
- bind nightmare effects into physical consequences;
- move dream architecture;
- sustain Dominion;
- trigger emergency awakening effects.

### Capacity and high-value behavior

Lucidity has a visible maximum capacity.

At high Lucidity, the Somnarch becomes **Lucid**, enabling selected skills to add or replace a clause. High Lucidity should create opportunity, not a passive damage bonus that rewards hoarding forever.

### Low-value behavior

At very low Lucidity:

- Veiled movement becomes less reliable;
- major zones cannot be refreshed freely;
- some constructs become suggestive rather than binding;
- the class remains functional through core attacks and low-cost Dread application.

### Persistence

**PROPOSED:** Lucidity decays outside combat to a stable floor rather than emptying completely. Entering town or changing regions resets temporary Dominion state but not persistent build choices.

### Gear interaction rule

Gear may alter generation, capacity, spending patterns, and high/low thresholds, but cannot remove the need to establish dream territory or interact with Dread.

## 7. Signature mechanic: Sovereign Dream

Stable mechanic ID: `MEC-SOM-SOVEREIGN-DREAM`

The Somnarch overlays part of the battlefield with a coherent private dream assembled from **Dream Clauses**.

### Dream Clause categories

1. **Threshold** — triggers when an enemy crosses a line, doorway, edge, or route.
2. **Chamber** — creates a persistent zone with an internal rule.
3. **Witness** — a stationary or moving manifestation that observes, repeats, or records actions.
4. **Edict** — a temporary law applied after conditions are completed.
5. **Nightmare** — an aggressive fear construct that escalates Dread or converts it into damage.

### Territory capacity

The Somnarch has a limited **Territory Budget** defining how many major dream constructs may exist simultaneously.

- small thresholds consume little budget;
- chambers consume moderate budget;
- major witnesses and edicts consume more;
- Dominion may temporarily overcap the budget;
- exceeding the budget outside allowed effects collapses the oldest eligible construct after a readable warning.

### Ownership rule

Every dream construct has an explicit owner, duration, tags, and collapse behavior. Multiple Somnarchs in co-op may overlap territory without silently overwriting one another.

### Completion rule

Many clauses begin as **Suggested** and become **Binding** after their condition completes.

Examples:

- a door appears; crossing it completes the Threshold;
- an enemy attacks inside a Chamber; the Chamber records the action;
- a Witness observes three marked targets; its next echo becomes Binding;
- a feared enemy reaches the center of a zone; the Nightmare gains physical force.

### Collapse and Rupture

Dream constructs can end in three ways:

1. **Expire** — duration completes naturally.
2. **Recall** — the player removes the construct for partial Lucidity return.
3. **Rupture** — the player ends it early for a build-specific burst, displacement, echo, or defensive effect.

The shared verb **Rupture** is used consistently, but Somnarch dream Rupture must remain mechanically distinct from Graftborn enemy Rupture state.

## 8. Secondary mechanic: Dread

Stable ailment interaction ID: `STS-SOM-DREAD`

Dread is a shared ailment with class-specific depth for the Somnarch.

### Baseline behavior

- Dread increases oneiric vulnerability and weakens control resistance within caps.
- Ordinary enemies may hesitate, mis-track, or become easier to displace at defined thresholds.
- Dread does not make enemies act randomly.
- Boss Dread contributes to **Nightmare Pressure**, which converts into Break Pressure, damage windows, or clause completion rather than hard fear.

### Dread thresholds

**PROPOSED:** Somnarch skills recognize three thresholds:

- **Uneasy:** basic oneiric vulnerability.
- **Afraid:** stronger clause interactions and tracking disruption.
- **Overwhelmed:** eligible enemies may trigger nightmare payoffs; bosses instead grant increased Break Pressure contribution.

### Resistance rule

Dread-resistant enemies reduce threshold buildup but never invalidate the entire class. The Somnarch can convert resisted Dread into Lucidity, Ward, or Break Pressure through tree investment.

## 9. Combat states: Waking, Veiled, and Dominion

### Waking

The default state.

- clear targeting;
- stable Lucidity generation;
- direct placement of constructs;
- strongest control over territory layout;
- ordinary defensive profile.

### Veiled

A tactical state entered through selected movement, defensive, and class skills.

- partially desynchronizes the Somnarch from enemy perception;
- improves access to hidden routes and threshold crossing;
- may reduce direct outgoing damage unless specialized;
- enables certain skills to target dream geometry instead of enemies;
- consumes Lucidity or has limited duration.

Veiled is not full invisibility and must remain readable in PvE and possible future PvP contexts.

### Dominion

Stable mechanic ID: `STA-SOM-DOMINION`

Dominion is a high-authority state entered when the Somnarch has enough completed or Binding territory.

Baseline Dominion effects:

- completed dream constructs gain improved persistence;
- the Somnarch may temporarily overcap Territory Budget;
- selected skills gain Edict variants;
- Lucidity drains steadily or per authoritative action;
- collapsing constructs may extend Dominion through deliberate sequencing;
- Dominion ends when Lucidity is exhausted, all qualifying territory is lost, or the player exits manually.

Dominion should feel like ruling a battlefield already prepared, not pressing a generic transformation cooldown.

## 10. Weapons and equipment identity

### Supported weapon families

Primary proposed families:

- one-handed blades and ritual knives;
- long blades and execution swords;
- staves;
- censers and chained foci;
- scepters;
- mirrors or mask-foci;
- one-handed weapon plus dream focus;
- two-handed court implements or processional weapons.

### Weapon integration

The Somnarch should not ignore weapons even in caster builds.

Weapons may define:

- how Dread is applied;
- whether core attacks are melee, projected, or zone-linked;
- which constructs can echo weapon strikes;
- threshold geometry;
- Lucidity generation cadence;
- whether a build favors Waking, Veiled, or Dominion play.

### Class Relic proposal

Class Relic category: **Dream Seal**

Possible forms:

- sleep mask;
- sealed crown fragment;
- funerary plaque;
- bottled room;
- black mirror;
- miniature door;
- witness bell;
- name-tablet from a forgotten court.

Dream Seals modify Territory Budget, clause categories, Dominion rules, Dread thresholds, or manifestation behavior.

## 11. Movement identity

### Design principle

The Somnarch moves by taking routes that exist only inside the Sovereign Dream.

Movement must feel spatially uncanny but remain predictable and collision-respecting.

### Core movement skill: Step Through the Unseen

Stable skill ID: `SKL-SOM-04`

- place or use a short-lived dream threshold;
- move through it to a valid nearby location;
- crossing an existing owned threshold reduces cost or cooldown;
- while Veiled, the endpoint may target another dream construct within range;
- invalid endpoints fail safely without consuming the full resource cost;
- collision, navigation, and arena-boundary rules remain authoritative.

### Movement variants

- short route blink between constructs;
- backward waking step leaving a decoy Witness;
- threshold swap with an eligible nightmare;
- corridor dash that bends around terrain but never crosses invalid space;
- emergency awakening that destroys nearby constructs for Guard and displacement resistance.

### Non-negotiable movement rule

The Somnarch cannot become functionally untargetable through chained hidden routes. Cooldowns, Lucidity costs, arrival tells, and Territory Budget must preserve counterplay.

## 12. Foundational active skill kit

Values are prototype targets only.

### `SKL-SOM-01` — Courtly Cut

**Role:** low-cost core action, weapon integration, Lucidity generation.

A measured strike or projected blade-line that deals Physical and Oneiric damage.

- deals increased Dread buildup to enemies inside owned territory;
- striking through a Threshold echoes a reduced portion from the opposite edge;
- weapon family changes range and cadence;
- remains useful without full setup.

### `SKL-SOM-02` — Name the Fear

**Role:** resource builder, mark, Dread application.

Marks a target with a visible nightmare-name.

- marked target gains Dread when entering dream territory or using a major action;
- completing the mark generates Lucidity;
- bosses convert completion into Nightmare Pressure;
- only a limited number of primary nightmare-names may exist by default.

### `SKL-SOM-03` — Room Without Exit

**Role:** Chamber, control, setup.

Creates a bounded dream room over terrain.

- enemies inside are Slowed and accumulate Dread when moving toward the edge;
- crossing the edge completes the Chamber’s first clause;
- once Binding, the room may Root ordinary enemies briefly or add Break Pressure to bosses;
- Rupturing the room pulls eligible enemies toward its center or converts the boundary into damage.

### `SKL-SOM-04` — Step Through the Unseen

**Role:** movement, Veiled access.

Travel through an impossible route to a valid destination.

- crossing from one owned construct to another grants brief Veiled state;
- arrival can complete nearby Threshold clauses;
- upgrades may leave a Witness or nightmare decoy.

### `SKL-SOM-05` — Shut Your Eyes

**Role:** defense, Guard, state shift.

The Somnarch closes off waking perception for a short timing window.

- grants Guard and tracking disruption;
- correctly timing against a hit creates Lucidity and leaves a Witness recording the attack;
- failure still grants reduced mitigation so the skill is not all-or-nothing;
- may enter Veiled state through path investment.

### `SKL-SOM-06` — The Watching Chair

**Role:** Witness, echo engine, stationary utility.

Places an empty throne or chair that records the first qualifying action performed nearby.

- can echo a reduced version of the recorded player skill;
- can instead record an enemy attack for defensive or retaliatory builds;
- only one primary recording exists at a time unless specialized;
- does not become a fully autonomous turret by default.

### `SKL-SOM-07` — Procession of the Faceless

**Role:** moving nightmare, lane control.

Summons a line of faceless court figures that marches along a chosen path.

- applies Dread and Daze;
- pushes fodder enemies and contributes Break Pressure to elites and bosses;
- crossing a Threshold causes the procession to reappear from another owned edge;
- manifestations dissolve after completing their route.

### `SKL-SOM-08` — Remember the Fall

**Role:** delayed burst, Echo payoff.

Selects a target or location and records damage dealt within a short window.

- after the delay, a oneiric Echo repeats a capped portion;
- repeated damage cannot recursively record itself;
- Dread thresholds improve the conversion ratio or add Break Pressure;
- Rupturing a nearby Witness may trigger the Echo early at reduced strength.

### `SKL-SOM-09` — Mercy of Sleep

**Role:** recovery, control conversion.

Creates a brief sleep-like suspension on eligible ordinary enemies and a non-disabling pressure state on bosses.

- damage ends ordinary suspension and grants Ward to the attacker or caster;
- bosses instead grant Ward based on Nightmare Pressure applied;
- can be built as support, self-defense, or execution setup;
- never permanently removes elite participation from combat.

### `SKL-SOM-10` — Crown the Nightmare

**Role:** major spender, signature payoff.

Consumes Dread from enemies in an owned Chamber to create a crowned nightmare manifestation.

- manifestation performs one powerful authored behavior based on the consumed clause type;
- Threshold consumption creates pursuit;
- Witness consumption creates echoes;
- Chamber consumption creates area denial;
- Edict consumption creates a short rule effect;
- the manifestation is temporary and mechanically closer to a moving skill than a permanent minion.

### `SKL-SOM-11` — Edict of Stillness

**Role:** Edict, high-control payoff.

After completing enough clauses, declare that motion has become a punishable act within the dream territory.

- moving enemies take Oneiric damage and Dread;
- stationary enemies become more vulnerable to direct strikes or echoes;
- bosses choose neither behavior but accumulate Break Pressure based on actions;
- the player still needs to position and attack; the Edict does not play the game automatically.

### `SKL-SOM-12` — Wake the Palace

**Role:** major territory payoff.

Temporarily connects all active constructs into a single Palace network.

- thresholds link;
- Witnesses share recordings under strict recursion limits;
- Chambers inherit one minor compatible clause;
- enemy displacement toward one construct may route through another;
- high Territory Budget cost and long cooldown prevent constant uptime.

### `SKL-SOM-ULT` — The Kingdom Dreams Back

**Role:** ultimate, full Dominion expression.

The Sovereign Dream becomes briefly self-supporting.

- immediately enters Dominion;
- freezes normal Territory Budget collapse for the duration;
- completes one eligible Suggested clause per construct;
- generates a central throne-zone that echoes the player’s first major skill of each category;
- Dread thresholds are treated as one stage higher within the throne-zone;
- on ending, the player chooses or automatically applies one aftermath: Recall for Lucidity, Rupture for offense, or Shelter for Ward and Guard.

The ultimate should transform existing preparation. It must not create maximum value on an empty battlefield.

## 13. Class-specific progression paths

The shared framework currently proposes three major paths. This bible defines four thematic path identities that can be represented as three primary trunks plus one cross-path convergence layer if the final tree framework remains limited to three visible starting branches.

### Path A — Sovereign Architect

Focus:

- Chambers;
- Threshold networks;
- Territory Budget;
- Binding clauses;
- Dominion duration;
- battlefield ownership.

Playstyle:

Prepare a coherent dream-palace and force enemies to move through its rules.

Representative nodes:

- additional minor Threshold capacity;
- Chambers become larger but cost more Lucidity;
- Binding clauses grant Ward;
- linked constructs share part of Dread application;
- recalling a completed construct reduces movement-skill cooldown;
- Dominion drains Lucidity more slowly while standing in a Chamber.

### Path B — Nightmare Regent

Focus:

- Dread thresholds;
- manifestations;
- fear conversion;
- Break Pressure;
- aggressive Rupture;
- close-range nightmare pressure.

Playstyle:

Drive enemies into escalating fear, then give that fear physical form.

Representative nodes:

- Dread application through weapon strikes;
- overwhelmed enemies create small nightmare zones on death;
- Crown the Nightmare gains alternate manifestations;
- resisted Dread produces Break Pressure;
- Ruptured nightmares grant temporary attack speed;
- melee attacks against Afraid targets gain Oneiric conversion.

### Path C — Lucid Wanderer

Focus:

- Veiled state;
- movement routes;
- defensive timing;
- Witness recordings;
- echoes;
- duelist or skirmisher play.

Playstyle:

Move through impossible routes, record attacks, and strike from positions enemies cannot maintain.

Representative nodes:

- Step Through the Unseen gains a second route charge under conditions;
- perfectly timed Shut Your Eyes grants a free Threshold;
- Witnesses can record movement skills;
- Veiled attacks apply stronger Dread but reduce raw direct damage;
- crossing three distinct thresholds makes the next Echo Binding;
- leaving Veiled grants brief Guard rather than invisibility.

### Path D — Sleepless Heretic

Focus:

- high/low Lucidity manipulation;
- self-risk;
- uncontrolled dream leakage;
- extended Dominion;
- conversion of Ward, Life, or Dread into authority;
- dangerous hybridization.

Playstyle:

Refuse to wake, sustaining impossible territory by sacrificing safety and identity boundaries.

Representative nodes:

- spend Ward when Lucidity is insufficient;
- low Lucidity increases Dread output but weakens recovery;
- Dominion may continue at zero Lucidity by draining Life within strict limits;
- collapsed constructs leave hostile-neutral dream scars that both sides can trigger;
- overcapped Territory Budget increases skill power and incoming damage;
- enemy oneiric effects can be partially claimed after resisting them.

This path carries the strongest narrative temptation and requires the clearest accessibility warnings.

## 14. Cross-path synergies

### Palace of Teeth

Sovereign Architect + Nightmare Regent

- Chambers gain aggressive nightmare anatomy;
- thresholds bite, crush, or drag enemies;
- high Dread allows territory to Rupture for direct damage;
- loses some defensive Ward generation.

### The Unseen Duel

Nightmare Regent + Lucid Wanderer

- Veiled weapon attacks consume Dread for echoes;
- threshold routes become pursuit tools;
- manifestations mirror selected melee finishers;
- lower persistent-zone coverage.

### Court of One Witness

Sovereign Architect + Lucid Wanderer

- fewer constructs, but each can record or link actions;
- one central Witness controls a compact network;
- stronger tactical play in boss arenas;
- weaker dense-screen coverage.

### Never Wake

Lucid Wanderer + Sleepless Heretic

- extended Veiled state;
- movement consumes Ward or Life after Lucidity is exhausted;
- perfect timing restores control;
- severe punishment for route mistakes.

### Kingdom of the Afraid

Nightmare Regent + Sleepless Heretic

- Dread becomes both resource and risk;
- overwhelmed enemies extend Dominion;
- resisted Dread causes backlash unless converted;
- extremely high offensive ceiling with unstable defense.

### The Locked Refuge

Sovereign Architect + Sleepless Heretic

- creates durable defensive dream territory;
- allies and player gain Shelter inside Chambers;
- leaving owned territory causes penalties or resource loss;
- powerful stationary identity with reduced mobility.

## 15. Proposed keystones

### `KEY-SOM-01` — The Door Was Always Open

Thresholds no longer require direct placement on reachable ground; they may attach to eligible owned constructs within range.

Cost:

- Thresholds cannot deal direct damage;
- route misuse can collapse the destination construct;
- lower baseline Threshold duration.

### `KEY-SOM-02` — A Kingdom Needs Witnesses

Witnesses may hold two recordings and choose the most contextually valid one when triggered.

Cost:

- each Witness consumes significantly more Territory Budget;
- repeated skill categories suffer diminishing echo value;
- no recursive Witness-to-Witness recording.

### `KEY-SOM-03` — Fear Has Weight

Dread thresholds add Physical impact and displacement to nightmare skills.

Cost:

- reduced pure Oneiric damage;
- Dread buildup is slower against unstaggered enemies;
- best value requires close positioning.

### `KEY-SOM-04` — The Merciful Lie

Enemies suspended by Mercy of Sleep cannot be damaged for a short protected interval, but their prevented damage is recorded and partially applied when they wake.

Cost:

- delayed damage is capped;
- bosses receive no invulnerability, only improved Nightmare Pressure conversion;
- poor timing can delay kills.

### `KEY-SOM-05` — No Morning Beyond This Point

Dominion may continue at zero Lucidity by draining Ward, then Life.

Cost:

- healing received is reduced during extended Dominion;
- emergency awakening destroys all constructs;
- cannot be combined freely with effects granting infinite Ward loops.

### `KEY-SOM-06` — The Empty Throne Commands

The Somnarch may designate one Chamber as a Throne Chamber. Skills cast outside it originate a reduced echo from the throne.

Cost:

- only one Chamber may exist;
- losing the Throne Chamber causes a brief Daze and Lucidity loss;
- encourages deliberate stationary territory.

### `KEY-SOM-07` — Wakefulness Is the Intrusion

Waking state becomes temporary after combat begins; the Somnarch naturally drifts into Veiled state.

Cost:

- direct placement range is reduced while Veiled;
- core attacks deal less immediate damage unless exiting Veiled intentionally;
- requires active state management.

### `KEY-SOM-08` — Every Nightmare Is Someone’s Memory

Enemy attacks recorded by Witnesses may be reinterpreted as player-owned oneiric echoes.

Cost:

- only defined safe attack archetypes are eligible;
- damage scales from player stats rather than copying enemy damage directly;
- recording a hostile action prevents recording a player action until released.

## 16. Ultimate design

### The Kingdom Dreams Back

Stable ID: `SKL-SOM-ULT`

### Fantasy

The Somnarch stops projecting a private dream and allows the dream to recognize the battlefield as part of itself.

### Activation requirements

**PROPOSED:** Standard cooldown plus at least one completed construct. Builds may replace the cooldown with event-driven generation, but the ultimate should never be strongest with no preparation.

### Baseline phases

1. **Recognition:** active constructs become connected and visibly orient toward the throne-zone.
2. **Coronation:** the Somnarch enters Dominion and completes eligible Suggested clauses.
3. **Reign:** first major skill of each category produces a controlled echo.
4. **Aftermath:** Recall, Rupture, or Shelter resolves the remaining territory.

### Boss compatibility

- completed clauses generate Nightmare Pressure;
- echoes respect boss vulnerability windows and recursion limits;
- control becomes Break Pressure rather than hard disable;
- throne-zone placement matters throughout the phase.

### Failure prevention

- entering the ultimate with only one construct still produces functional but lower value;
- constructs cannot be deleted arbitrarily by boss phase changes without refund or relocation handling;
- the ultimate cannot duplicate itself or other ultimates through Witness recording.

### General affix families

- Lucidity capacity;
- Lucidity generation from completed clauses;
- Territory Budget for specific construct categories;
- Dread buildup;
- Dread threshold effect;
- Oneiric damage;
- mixed weapon/Oneiric conversion;

- Threshold duration and route range;
- Ward gained inside owned territory;

### Affix guardrails

- generic cooldown reduction cannot create infinite Step Through routing;
- Territory Budget increases must be rare and category-specific;
- Witness effects require explicit recursion rules;
- Dread effectiveness cannot become mandatory for every build;
- Dominion uptime should come from sequencing, not one affix stack;
- Ward generation must avoid self-sustaining invulnerability loops;
- item effects should alter clauses, routes, costs, or state transitions rather than merely adding visual noise.

### Weapon-family interactions

- blades favor direct Dread application and duelist echoes;
- staves favor Chamber authority and zone size;
- censers favor moving manifestations and processions;
- mirrors favor Witnesses, mirrored targeting, and echoes;
- scepters favor Edicts and Dominion;
- two-handed court weapons favor fewer, stronger constructs.

## 20. Party and co-op identity

### Self-sufficient solo loop

The Somnarch can apply Dread, establish territory, create Witnesses, gain Ward, trigger echoes, and complete clauses without another player.

### Party contributions

- Chambers can provide Shelter, recovery, route control, or enemy debuffs;
- Thresholds may create optional ally routes with clear consent input or safe automatic rules;
- Dread improves group Break Pressure without requiring allies to understand the full class system;
- Witnesses may record designated ally skill categories only through explicit build choices;
- Edicts can create readable team windows;
- Mercy of Sleep can protect allies or create recovery opportunities.

### Co-op restrictions

- allies are never forcibly teleported by Somnarch Thresholds;
- one Somnarch cannot overwrite another’s territory;
- ally skills cannot be copied at full power without explicit caps and ownership rules;
- multiple Dread sources contribute cleanly without multiplying threshold effects uncontrollably;
- party value must not make solo play feel incomplete.

### Same-class co-op

Multiple Somnarchs may:

- link selected compatible Thresholds through consent rules;
- contribute to shared Dread thresholds while retaining separate ownership;
- create separate Palaces with visually distinct edge patterns;
- trigger alternate effects when constructs overlap instead of simple duplication.

## 23. Technical implementation risks

### Risk 1 — Persistent zone ownership

Dream territory requires robust owner IDs, durations, tags, stacking rules, and cleanup behavior.

Mitigation:

- implement one reusable zone-owner data model;
- separate functional geometry from decorative visuals;
- test death, room transition, boss phase change, multiplayer disconnect, and scene reload cleanup.

### Risk 2 — Territory Budget desynchronization

Construct creation and collapse can desync UI and gameplay state.

Mitigation:

- central authoritative budget manager;
- deterministic add/remove events;
- no hidden decorative construct that consumes budget;
- automated tests for create, Recall, Rupture, expiration, replacement, and ultimate overcap.

### Risk 3 — Echo recursion

Witnesses, Remember the Fall, throne echoes, and ally recording can create recursive triggers.

Mitigation:

- every action carries origin, generation, and echo-depth metadata;
- default echo depth of one unless explicitly overridden;
- echoed skills cannot record themselves or trigger the same recording source;
- hard tests for multi-Witness and co-op cases.

### Risk 4 — Navigation and threshold movement

Impossible routes can bypass collision, arena bounds, hazards, or scripted gates.

Mitigation:

- server or authoritative navigation validation;
- endpoint preview;
- safe failure and partial refund;
- no arbitrary free-camera teleport targeting;
- explicit boss-arena permissions.

### Risk 5 — Boss control invalidation

A control-heavy class risks feeling useless against bosses.

Mitigation:

- all control maps to Nightmare Pressure and Break Pressure;
- bosses expose specific clause interactions;
- Dread-resistant bosses still generate alternative value;
- no blanket immunity without compensation.

### Risk 6 — Screen clutter

Multiple zones, boundaries, witnesses, manifestations, and echoes may obscure enemy attacks.

Mitigation:

- strict visual priority tiers;
- scalable decoration;
- silhouette-first construct design;
- cap large persistent manifestations;
- enemy telegraphs always render above non-critical dream decoration.

### Risk 7 — AI behavior around fear

Randomized fear can break encounters and pathfinding.

Mitigation:

- use authored states: hesitation, tracking penalty, forced facing, bounded retreat, route preference;
- ordinary enemies receive controlled behavior packages;
- elites and bosses convert effects to pressure systems.

### Risk 8 — Performance

Connected territory and dynamic geometry may be expensive.

Mitigation:

- pooled construct scenes;
- simple collision primitives;
- graph updates only on add/remove/link events;
- visual-only geometry separated from gameplay collision;
- maximum active constructs enforced by budget.

### Risk 9 — Save compatibility

Persistent builds may reference clause IDs, item conversions, and tree nodes that change during development.

Mitigation:

- stable IDs throughout;
- versioned migration table;
- missing-node refunds;
- avoid serializing transient battlefield constructs.

## 24. Non-negotiable design rules

1. The Somnarch is a territory sovereign, not a generic sleep mage.
2. Dream effects create coherent, legible spatial rules.
3. Dread never becomes random enemy behavior.
4. Bosses remain meaningfully affected through Nightmare Pressure and Break Pressure.
5. The class remains functional before full territory setup.
6. Lucidity is a decision resource, not recolored mana.
7. Dominion rewards preparation rather than replacing the normal kit.
8. Witness echoes use strict recursion limits.
9. Movement respects collision, navigation, and arena boundaries.
10. Dream manifestations are temporary authored skills, not an uncontrolled permanent army.
11. Territory Budget is visible and authoritative.
12. Suggested and Binding states are communicated through more than color.
13. Every build has a solo defensive plan.
14. Co-op utility never requires allies to complete the class loop.
15. Powerful dream control must carry placement, timing, or resource costs.
16. Horror remains serious, readable, and connected to memory, consent, and sovereignty.
17. Persistent class trees, gear, and saved builds remain the progression foundation.
18. No mechanic introduces mandatory in-combat three-choice upgrade interruptions.

## 25. Cross-class consistency audit

### Voidbringer

No direct conflict.

- Voidbringer controls gravity, portals, soul energy, and Corruption.
- Somnarch routes exist through oneiric territory rather than spatial rupture.
- portal and Threshold visuals must remain distinct: Voidbringer wounds or folds space; Somnarch reveals a route the dream insists was present.

### Penitent

No direct conflict.

- Penitent openly writes battlefield law through Brands, Circles, and rites.
- Somnarch creates conditions reality follows through fear, memory, and dream sovereignty.
- both use zones, but Penitent geometry should be declarative and sacrificial while Somnarch geometry is architectural and perceptual.

### Graftborn

No direct conflict.

- Graftborn self-authors anatomy through Biomass, Body Regions, and Adaptations.
- Somnarch authors territory and perception through Lucidity and clauses.
- Graftborn’s proprietary Rupture state remains distinct from the shared verb used to end dream constructs.
- dream attempts to define an “original body” support the Graftborn campaign arc without stealing its mechanic.

### Relic Host

Boundary required but compatible.

- Relic presences have inherited continuity, material provenance, and autonomous history.
- Somnarch Witnesses and manifestations are temporary authored dream functions.
- a dream of a dead saint is not automatically the saint’s relic consciousness.

### Gorgon

Boundary required but compatible.

- Gorgon fixes, reveals, or transforms material identity through gaze and venomous transformation.
- Somnarch changes perceived and oneiric conditions.
- petrified memory in Act 04 should remain materially distinct from dream preservation.

### Tidewrought

Compatible conversion space.

- Tidewrought controls pressure, current, brine, and abyssal transformation.
- Somnarch may access drowned memories but does not own tidal identity.
- cross-tag uniques can support Oneiric/Tidal builds without requiring co-op.

### Anachron

Boundary required but compatible.

- Anachron alters chronology, delay, repetition, and erased sequence.
- Somnarch echoes are memory or dream reenactments, not time travel.
- a future memory inside a dream may foreshadow temporal truth but cannot grant unrestricted timeline mechanics.

### Reserved Choirborn

Somnarch must not become a voice-command or collective-song class. Parliament dialogue and whispers should remain political and oneiric, not musical-system identity.

### Reserved Echo Thief

Somnarch Witnesses record narrowly defined actions through owned constructs. They do not steal enemy or ally kits broadly. Echo Thief remains reserved for deeper appropriation identity.

### Reserved Plaguebringer

Somnarch Dread and nightmare manifestations must not become disease spread, infection stacks, or plague minions.
